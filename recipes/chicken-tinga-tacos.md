pitch: >-
  Tinga, engineered. Chicken breasts go into a 125°F bath with a smoky
  chipotle-tomato sauce until they're absurdly tender, then get shredded and
  crisped in a ripping-hot pan so the edges go dark and craggy. The reserved
  sauce reduces down to a brick-red, spoon-coating glaze that clings to every
  strand. Pile it into charred tortillas with white onion, cilantro, cotija,
  and a squeeze of lime, and let quick-pickled red onions cut the richness.
  *Reduce the sauce until a spoon dragged through leaves a trail that holds for
  a second — that's peak taco stickiness.*

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & seasoning
    items:
      - 1–1.25 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts
      - 1 heaping tsp kosher salt
      - ½ tsp black pepper
      - ½ tsp ground cumin
      - ½ tsp smoked paprika
      - ¼ tsp Mexican oregano
  - section: Tinga sauce
    items:
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - ½ white or yellow onion, sliced
      - 2 large garlic cloves, smashed
      - 1 (14.5 oz) can crushed or whole tomatoes
      - 1–2 chipotle peppers in adobo (1 for medium, 2 for hot)
      - 1–2 tsp adobo sauce
      - ½ tsp ground cumin
      - ½ tsp smoked paprika
      - ½ tsp kosher salt
      - Splash of apple cider vinegar or lime juice
      - 1 tsp butter, to finish (optional)
  - section: Quick pickled red onions
    items:
      - 1 red onion, thinly sliced
      - ½ cup apple cider vinegar
      - ½ cup water
      - 1 tbsp sugar
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - 1–2 thin slices serrano or jalapeño (from the garden)
  - section: To assemble
    items:
      - 8–12 corn or flour tortillas
      - Diced white onion
      - Fresh cilantro, chopped (from the garden)
      - Cotija or queso fresco, crumbled
      - Lime wedges
      - Optional - sliced radish, avocado, crema
steps:
  - section: Make the tinga sauce
    items:
      - Heat the oil in a skillet over medium. Sauté the sliced onion 4–5 minutes until soft and lightly golden, then add the garlic and stir 1 minute.
      - Add the tomatoes, chipotles, adobo, cumin, smoked paprika, and salt. Simmer 10 minutes, then blend until smooth. Taste — more adobo for heat, a splash of vinegar or lime for brightness.
  - section: Season & bag
    items:
      - Season the chicken with the salt, pepper, cumin, smoked paprika, and oregano. Let it sit 10 minutes.
      - Place the chicken in a sous-vide or zip bag with ⅓ cup of the blended sauce; reserve the rest. Remove the air by water displacement or vacuum sealer.
  - section: Sous-vide
    items:
      - Cook at 125°F for 1 hour 45 minutes for a silky, juicy texture. (For a more classic firm shred, run 140°F for 90 minutes instead.)
  - section: Pickle the onions
    items:
      - While the chicken cooks, pack the sliced red onion and chile into a jar. Simmer the vinegar, water, sugar, and salt until dissolved, pour over the onions to submerge, and let steep at least 15 minutes.
  - section: Reduce, crisp & sauce
    items:
      - Pour the reserved sauce into a pan over medium-low and simmer 8–12 minutes until thick, glossy, and spoon-coating. Adjust salt.
      - Remove the chicken from the bag and shred gently into medium strands (don't over-shred). Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wide skillet over medium-high, spread the chicken in a thin layer, and leave it undisturbed 45–60 seconds to crisp before flipping for another 30 seconds.
      - Add the reduced sauce and toss to coat; simmer 1–2 minutes just to marry. Stir in the butter off the heat for a silky finish if you like.
  - section: Assemble
    items:
      - Char the tortillas over a flame or in a dry skillet (for flour, skillet-warm then steam under a towel).
      - Pile in the tinga and top with white onion, cilantro, cotija, pickled onions, and a squeeze of lime.
tips:
  - At 125°F the chicken stays at that temperature far longer than the few minutes needed for pasteurization, so it's chef-safe — but it won't fall apart like braised tinga. Crisping and saucing relaxes the fibers into taco-ready strands.
  - Make the sauce and season the chicken up to a day ahead, but only bag the chicken with sauce right before it goes in the bath — long acid contact softens the texture too far.
  - For thin corn tortillas, double them up; saucy tinga will blow through a single layer.
  - If your tomatoes are very acidic, a teaspoon of honey in the sauce rounds it out.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Drop a thin slice of serrano or jalapeño into the pickling brine for heat, and finish the tacos with a heavy hand of garden cilantro.
  - label: Crema upgrade
    note: Blend ¼ cup crema or sour cream with a roasted jalapeño, lime juice, and salt for a cooling drizzle.
  - label: Make-ahead
    note: The tinga sauce keeps 5 days in the fridge or 3 months frozen, and tastes even better after a day as the aromatics meld.
