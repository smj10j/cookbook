title: Slow-Roasted Cod with Melted Leeks & Tarragon
slug: slow-roasted-cod-melted-leeks
tagline: Cod roasted low until it barely holds together, over leeks cooked to silk.
pitch: >-
  Low heat is the most forgiving way to cook fish, and cod rewards it more than
  most. The leeks go first — sliced into ribbons and stewed in olive oil with a
  splash of wine until they collapse into silk. The cod sits on top and roasts
  at 300°F until it turns opaque and barely flakes, never seizing, never dry.
  *There is no minute when this overcooks fast — the low oven gives you a
  five-minute grace window instead of thirty seconds.* Tarragon and lemon
  finish it like a beurre blanc that never needed the butter.
serves: 2

ingredients:
  - section: Leeks
    items:
      - 2 leeks, white and light green parts, halved and sliced thin
      - 1½ tbsp olive oil
      - 2 tbsp dry white wine (or broth)
      - ¼ tsp kosher salt
  - section: Cod
    items:
      - 8 oz cod fillet, cut into 2 portions
      - 1 tsp olive oil
      - Freshly ground black pepper
      - 1 tbsp fresh tarragon, chopped
      - ½ lemon, in wedges
steps:
  - Heat the oven to 300°F. Wash the sliced leeks well — grit hides between the layers.
  - In an ovenproof skillet, stew the leeks in the olive oil over medium-low with the salt until completely soft and sweet, 10–12 minutes; add the wine and let it bubble out.
  - Pat the cod dry, rub with the 1 tsp oil, and season with pepper. Nestle the portions on the leeks.
  - Roast 15–18 minutes, until the fish is opaque and a knife slides through without resistance.
  - Shower with tarragon and serve from the skillet with lemon wedges.
tips:
  - Cod is done at 130°F in the center — with a 300°F oven you can check twice without penalty.
  - If your fillets are thin tail pieces, fold them double so they cook at the thick-piece pace.
  - Tarragon is the classic here, but garden thyme stewed with the leeks is just as good.
