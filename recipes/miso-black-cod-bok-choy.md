ingredients:
  - section: Cod & miso marinade
    items:
      - 2 black cod (sablefish) fillets, about 6 oz each
      - 2 tbsp white miso paste
      - 1 tbsp mirin
      - 1 tbsp sake (or dry white wine)
      - 1 tsp soy sauce
      - 1 tsp honey (or sugar)
      - Neutral oil, for the pan
  - section: Charred bok choy
    items:
      - 4 baby bok choy, halved lengthwise
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil (grapeseed or avocado)
      - Flaky salt
  - section: Ginger-scallion oil
    items:
      - 2 scallions, finely minced
      - 1 tbsp fresh ginger, finely grated
      - 1 small garlic clove, minced
      - 3 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 tsp sesame oil
      - Pinch of chili flakes (optional)
      - Salt, to taste
  - section: Quick pickled cucumber
    items:
      - "½ English cucumber (or 2 Persian), thinly sliced"
      - 3 tbsp rice vinegar
      - 1 tsp sugar
      - "½ tsp salt"
      - 1 tsp sesame seeds
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - Steamed jasmine or sushi rice (optional)
      - Toasted sesame seeds and cilantro, to garnish
steps:
  - section: The night before
    items:
      - Whisk the miso, mirin, sake, soy sauce, and honey until smooth. Pat the cod dry, coat it evenly in the marinade, and refrigerate at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. Black cod is rich and forgiving — longer means deeper umami.
  - section: Pickles & oil
    items:
      - Stir the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved, then toss with the cucumber and sesame seeds. Let sit at least 15 minutes (longer is better).
      - Combine the scallions, ginger, garlic, chili flakes, and sesame oil in a heatproof bowl. Heat the neutral oil until shimmering and pour it over the aromatics — it should sizzle. Stir, salt to taste, and set aside to bloom.
  - section: Broil the cod
    items:
      - Position a rack in the upper third and preheat the broiler on high. Line a sheet with foil and lightly oil it. Wipe excess marinade off the fillets, leaving a thin coating to prevent scorching.
      - Broil 8–10 minutes until nearly cooked through, watching closely — miso burns fast, so a light char is the target, not black. The cod is done when it flakes easily and the edges are glossy amber.
  - section: Char the bok choy
    items:
      - Brush the bok choy halves with neutral oil. Sear cut-side down in a hot skillet (or on the grill) 2–3 minutes until charred, flip for 1 minute more, and finish with flaky salt.
  - section: Plate
    items:
      - Fan the bok choy on each plate and spoon over the ginger-scallion oil. Lay the cod slightly offset, glazed side up.
      - Nest a small pile of pickled cucumber alongside (or on a mound of rice), then finish with sesame seeds and a few cilantro leaves.
tips:
  - Miso scorches in seconds under a broiler — leave only a thin film on the fish and stay at the oven door.
  - No mirin? Mix 1 tbsp white wine with 1 tsp sugar and 1 tsp water until dissolved; it works perfectly in the marinade.
  - Don't overcook the bok choy — its crunch and vivid green are what make the plate pop against the rich fish.
  - Make extra pickled cucumber; spooned over a bite of cod, the acidity is the contrast the whole plate is built on.
extras:
  - label: Sea bass on the grill
    note: Sea bass is a great alternative — marinate just 10–15 minutes (it's leaner), then grill skin-side down 3–4 minutes, flip, brush with glaze, and grill 3–4 minutes more. Move it to indirect heat after searing if the honey-miso starts to scorch.
  - label: Regular cod swap
    note: Standard cod works too — it's leaner, so marinate no longer than 12 hours and shorten the broil to 6–8 minutes plus a 2-minute char so it stays moist.
  - label: Restaurant finishing drizzle
    note: "For extra shine, melt 1 tbsp butter and stir in 1 tsp each soy, honey, and rice vinegar plus a pinch of grated ginger; drizzle this ginger-soy butter over the fish right before serving."
  - label: From the garden
    note: Finish with garden cilantro, and use a garden cucumber for the quick pickle. A pinch of jalapeño in the pickling liquid adds a gentle background heat.

Restaurant elegance from a make-ahead marinade: the miso does its quiet work
overnight, so the night-of is just a fast broil, a hot sizzle of ginger-scallion
oil, and a charred green. The pickled cucumber is the secret — bright acidity to
cut the buttery, lacquered cod.
