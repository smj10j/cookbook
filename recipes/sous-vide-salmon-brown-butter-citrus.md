  This is the refined one — precision and patience over fire. Salmon poaches in a
  120°F bath until it's so tender it barely holds a flake, then takes a 30-second
  kiss in a hot pan for the faintest golden crust. The finish is the showpiece: a
  beurre noisette swirled with orange and lemon zest until it smells nutty and
  toasty, spooned warm over the fillet so it pools into the couscous below.
  *The whole plate turns on that brown butter — pull it the second it smells like
  hazelnuts and goes amber, because a few seconds more is the difference between
  nutty and burnt.* Charred asparagus brings the bitter-green contrast.

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (skin-on or skinless, 5–6 oz each)
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - 2 sprigs fresh thyme or dill (optional)
      - 1 garlic clove, smashed (optional)
      - A pat of butter or drizzle of olive oil (for the bag)
      - Neutral oil (for the final sear)
  - section: Brown-butter citrus glaze
    items:
      - 4 tbsp unsalted butter
      - 1 small orange (zest plus 1–2 tsp juice)
      - 1 lemon (zest plus 1–2 tsp juice)
      - 1 tsp honey (optional, for balance)
      - 1 tbsp capers, drained (optional twist)
      - Pinch of flaky salt
  - section: Charred asparagus
    items:
      - 1 bunch asparagus, woody ends trimmed
      - Olive oil
      - Salt and pepper
      - Shaved Parmesan or lemon zest, to finish (optional)
  - section: Herbed couscous
    items:
      - 1 cup couscous
      - 1¼ cups chicken or vegetable broth
      - 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
      - 1 shallot or scallion, minced
      - 1–2 tbsp chopped fresh herbs (parsley, dill, mint, or basil)
steps:
  - section: Bath
    items:
      - Set the sous-vide to 120°F for medium-rare (125°F for medium). Season the fillets with salt and pepper.
      - Bag each fillet with a pat of butter or a drizzle of oil, plus thyme and smashed garlic if using. Seal by vacuum or water-displacement.
      - Submerge and cook 45 minutes (you can hold up to 1 hour without overcooking).
  - section: Sides
    items:
      - "Couscous: bring the broth to a boil, stir in couscous, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Cover, pull off the heat, and let sit 5 minutes. Fluff, then fold in the shallot and herbs."
      - "Asparagus: toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper and set aside, ready to char."
  - section: Glaze & finish
    items:
      - In a light-colored saucepan, melt the butter over medium and swirl constantly. After 3–5 minutes it foams, then turns amber with golden flecks and a nutty aroma — pull it immediately.
      - Off the heat, stir in the orange and lemon zest, 1–2 tsp of each juice, the honey, and capers if using. Keep warm.
      - Sear the asparagus in a hot pan or grill pan until lightly charred and crisp-tender. Pat the salmon dry and sear presentation-side down 30–60 seconds for a golden crust.
      - Spoon couscous onto each plate, add the asparagus, and set the salmon alongside. Drizzle warm brown-butter glaze over the fish and finish with a little extra zest.
tips:
  - Brown butter turns on a dime — use a light-colored pan so you can see the color change, and have your zest and juice measured before you start.
  - Pour the finished brown butter into a warm bowl off the heat; the residual pan heat will keep darkening it otherwise.
  - Toast the dry couscous in a dry pan for a minute before adding broth for a deeper, nuttier flavor.
  - The sear is for color and aroma only — 30 to 60 seconds. The fish is already cooked, so any longer and you lose the silky bath texture.
extras:

  - label: Pairs with

The elegant entry in the salmon rotation: sous-vide for an unbroken, custardy
flake, then a beurre noisette brightened with orange and lemon. The original lemon-
butter-caper idea grew into a proper brown-butter glaze, and the herbed couscous
and charred asparagus turn it into a full, restaurant-grade plate for two.
