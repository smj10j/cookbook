ingredients:
  - section: Sweet potato base
    items:
      - 4–5 medium sweet potatoes (about 3 lbs), peeled and cubed
      - ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
      - ¼ cup brown sugar
      - ¼ cup heavy cream (or a dairy-free alternative)
      - ½ tsp vanilla extract
      - ½ tsp ground cinnamon
      - Pinch of freshly grated nutmeg
      - ½ tsp kosher salt
  - section: Brown-sugar pecan topping
    items:
      - ½ cup pecans, chopped
      - ¼ cup brown sugar
      - 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
      - 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
      - Pinch of salt
steps:
  - section: Cook the sweet potatoes
    items:
      - Heat the oven to 375°F and butter a 9x13 baking dish.
      - Boil the cubed sweet potatoes in salted water until fork-tender, about 20 minutes. Drain well and let the steam dissipate for a minute so the mash isn't watery.
  - section: Build the base
    items:
      - Mash the sweet potatoes until smooth, then beat in the melted butter, brown sugar, cream, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Taste and adjust the sweetness.
      - Spread the mixture into the prepared dish in an even layer.
  - section: Top and bake
    items:
      - In a bowl, rub the pecans, brown sugar, melted butter, flour, and salt together with your fingers until crumbly. Scatter evenly over the sweet potatoes.
      - Bake 25–30 minutes, until the topping is golden and the edges are bubbling. Let rest 5–10 minutes before serving so it sets.
tips:
  - Roasting the sweet potatoes instead of boiling concentrates their sugars — halve them, roast cut-side down at 400°F until collapsing, then scoop and mash for a deeper flavor.
  - Toast the pecans in a dry skillet for a few minutes before mixing the streusel; it doubles their aroma in the finished crust.
  - Assemble the whole casserole a day ahead and refrigerate unbaked; add 10 minutes to the bake time straight from the fridge.
  - If you like a crisper top, run it under the broiler for the last 1–2 minutes — watch closely so the sugar doesn't scorch.
extras:
