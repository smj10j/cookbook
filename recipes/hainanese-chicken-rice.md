title: Hainanese Chicken Rice

tagline: Gently poached silky chicken with fragrant schmaltz rice and a trio of bright sauces.
pitch: >-
  The "national dish of Singapore," and a genuine technique to add to your kit: a whole
  chicken poached to silky, just-set tenderness, then the rice cooked in the chicken-rich
  poaching broth so every grain tastes like the bird. It comes with a trio of sauces —
  fiery chili-garlic, bright ginger-scallion, and dark soy. *It looks plain and tastes
  like the most comforting thing you've ever made — proof that poaching, done right, beats
  roasting for pure tenderness.* Restrained, elegant, and unlike anything else on the menu.

ingredients:
  - section: Poached chicken
    items:
      - 3.5 lb whole chicken
      - 6 slices ginger
      - 4 scallions
      - 4 cloves garlic, smashed
      - 1 tbsp kosher salt
  - section: Chicken rice
    items:
      - 2 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
      - 2 tbsp neutral oil
      - 4 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tbsp grated ginger
      - 2½ cups reserved poaching broth
  - section: Ginger-scallion oil
    items:
      - 3 scallions, finely sliced
      - 2 tbsp grated ginger
      - ¼ cup neutral oil
      - ½ tsp salt
  - section: Chili sauce
    items:
      - 2 serranos (or red chiles)
      - 2 cloves garlic
      - 1 tbsp grated ginger
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 tsp granulated sugar
      - 2 tbsp poaching broth
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 1 cucumber, sliced
      - Cilantro
      - Dark soy sauce, for drizzling
steps:
  - section: Poach
    items:
      - Rub the chicken with the salt. Put it in a pot with the ginger, scallions, and smashed garlic and cover with cold water. Bring to a bare simmer, then cook very gently 30–35 minutes (or turn off the heat and steep, covered, 45 minutes), until it reaches 165°F in the thigh.
      - Lift the chicken out and plunge it into ice water for 5 minutes to set the skin. Reserve the poaching broth.
  - section: Rice
    items:
      - Heat the oil in a pot and cook the garlic and ginger 1 minute. Add the rinsed rice and toast 2 minutes.
      - Add 2½ cups of the poaching broth, bring to a boil, cover, and cook on low 15 minutes. Rest, covered, 10 minutes, then fluff.
  - section: Sauces
    items:
      - "Ginger-scallion oil: combine the scallion, ginger, and salt in a heatproof bowl. Heat the oil until shimmering and pour it over so it sizzles."
      - "Chili sauce: blend the serranos, garlic, ginger, lime juice, sugar, and broth until smooth."
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Carve the chicken and serve over or alongside the rice with cucumber, cilantro, the two sauces, and a drizzle of dark soy.
tips:
  - Poach at a bare shiver, never a rolling boil — that's the secret to silky, just-set meat instead of stringy.
  - The ice bath right after poaching sets the skin into its signature glossy, slippery texture.
  - Cook the rice in the poaching broth, not water — that's where all the chicken flavor lives.
  - The three sauces are the dish — don't skip the ginger-scallion oil or the chili, and make extra of both.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Garden serranos or jalapeños in the chili sauce, and cilantro over everything.
  - label: Short on time
    note: A few bone-in chicken thighs poach faster than a whole bird and still make a rich broth.
  - label: Make it a spread
    note: A small bowl of the hot poaching broth with sliced scallion alongside is traditional.
