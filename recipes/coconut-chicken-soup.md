title: Coconut Chicken Soup (Tom Kha Gai)

  This is the bowl you want when you need comfort with a pulse. A fragrant base

  brightness flat and bitter.*

ingredients:
  - section: Broth
    items:
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 thumb fresh ginger, sliced into coins
      - 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised and cut into 2-inch lengths (or extra ginger)
      - 2 garlic cloves, smashed
      - 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
      - 1 (13.5 oz) can full-fat coconut milk
      - 1–2 tsp Thai chili paste (or 1 fresh chile, sliced)
      - 1–2 tbsp fish sauce, to taste
      - 1 tsp sugar
  - section: To finish
    items:
      - 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
      - 6 oz mushrooms (cremini or shiitake), sliced
      - Juice of 1–2 limes, to taste
      - Fresh cilantro (from the garden)
      - 1 serrano or Thai chile, thinly sliced (from the garden)
      - Steamed jasmine rice, to serve (optional)
steps:
  - section: Build the broth
    items:
      - Heat the oil in a saucepan over medium. Add the ginger, lemongrass, and garlic and stir 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
      - Pour in the chicken broth and coconut milk, stir in the chili paste and sugar, and bring to a gentle simmer (don't let it hard-boil, which can split the coconut milk).
  - section: Poach & simmer
    items:
      - Add the sliced chicken and mushrooms. Simmer gently 8–10 minutes until the chicken is just cooked through and the broth has taken on the aromatics.
      - Fish out the lemongrass and large ginger coins if you like. Stir in fish sauce to taste.
  - section: Finish
    items:
      - Take the pot off the heat. Stir in the lime juice, tasting as you go — you want it bright and balanced between salty, sour, and rich.
      - Ladle into bowls (over rice if using) and top with torn cilantro and thin slices of fresh chile.
tips:
  - Keep the broth at a bare simmer, never a rolling boil — high heat splits coconut milk into oily and grainy parts.
  - Balance at the end on the trio of fish sauce (salt), lime (sour), and a pinch of sugar (sweet). Adjust one at a time until the broth makes you want another spoonful.
  - Slice the chicken thin and add it raw to the simmering broth; it cooks in minutes and stays tender instead of rubbery.
  - No lemongrass? A wider strip of lime zest plus extra ginger gets you most of the way to that citrusy aroma.
extras:

  - label: Make it heartier
    note: Serve over jasmine rice, or add a handful of rice noodles to the pot in the last 3 minutes.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: Crusty bread for dunking, or just a wedge of extra lime on the side.
