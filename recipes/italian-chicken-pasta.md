title: Tomato-Basil Chicken Fettuccine with Fresh Mozzarella
slug: italian-chicken-pasta
tagline: Seared chicken and a quick fresh-tomato sauce tossed with fettuccine and melty mozzarella.
pitch: >-
  This is the weeknight pasta that tastes like late summer. Chicken sears golden
  in olive oil, then fresh tomatoes cook down in the same pan into a loose,
  jammy sauce flecked with garlic and a whisper of chili. Fettuccine goes in to
  catch it all, and cubes of fresh mozzarella melt into soft, creamy pockets as
  you toss. Torn basil and an optional thread of balsamic at the end keep it
  bright instead of heavy.
  *Stir the mozzarella in off the heat so it goes molten and stretchy rather
  than tough — those creamy pockets are the whole point.*

ingredients:
  - 8 oz fettuccine (thin spaghetti also works)
  - 2 small chicken breasts (or 1 large), sliced into thin strips
  - 2 large or 4 medium fresh tomatoes, diced
  - 4 garlic cloves, minced
  - 4 oz fresh mozzarella, cut into small cubes
  - A handful of fresh basil, torn (from the garden)
  - 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  - Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  - 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar (optional)
  - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
steps:
  - section: Pasta
    items:
      - Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the fettuccine until just al dente, then reserve ½ cup pasta water and drain.
  - section: Chicken
    items:
      - Season the chicken strips with salt and pepper. Heat 1–2 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high and cook the chicken 3–5 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  - section: Tomato sauce
    items:
      - Lower the heat to medium and add a little more oil if needed. Sauté the garlic 1–2 minutes until fragrant and just golden — don't let it burn.
      - Add the diced tomatoes with a pinch of salt, pepper, and chili flakes if using. Cook 5–7 minutes, stirring, until they break down into a slightly chunky sauce.
  - section: Combine
    items:
      - Return the chicken to the pan and toss to coat. Add the drained fettuccine, loosening with a splash of pasta water until the sauce clings.
      - Off the heat, fold in the mozzarella cubes and torn basil so the cheese softens into creamy pockets.
      - Plate, finish with a thread of balsamic if you like, and top with a few extra basil leaves.
tips:
  - Add the mozzarella off the heat — residual warmth melts it into soft, stretchy pockets, while direct heat over-cooks it into rubber.
  - A spoonful of reserved pasta water emulsifies the tomato oil into a sauce that actually coats the noodles.
  - For extra richness, swirl a knob of butter or a splash of cream into the tomatoes before adding the pasta.
  - Use the ripest tomatoes you can find; in winter, a cup of halved cherry tomatoes breaks down sweeter than mealy slicers.
