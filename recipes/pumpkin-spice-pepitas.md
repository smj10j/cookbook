title: Maple Pumpkin-Spice Pepitas

pitch: >-
  These are the snack you make once and keep reaching for — raw green pepitas tossed
  with a little oil, real maple syrup, and warm pumpkin pie spice, then roasted low and
  slow until they're glossy, toasty, and shatter-crisp. The maple caramelizes into the
  barest candied shell while the spice does its cozy fall thing. Scatter them on
  oatmeal, yogurt bowls, and salads, or just eat them by the handful.
  *They crisp up as they cool, so pull them while they still look slightly soft — that's
  exactly right.*

ingredients:
  - 1 cup raw pepitas (hulled pumpkin seeds)
  - 2 tsp olive oil (or melted coconut oil)
  - 1 tbsp maple syrup
  - 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  - ¼ tsp sea salt
steps:
  - Preheat the oven to 325°F and line a baking sheet with parchment.
  - In a bowl, toss the pepitas with the oil, maple syrup, pumpkin pie spice, and salt until evenly coated.
  - Spread in a single layer on the baking sheet and roast 12–15 minutes, stirring once halfway, until golden and fragrant.
  - Let cool completely on the pan — they'll crisp up as they cool. Store in an airtight jar.
tips:
  - Watch the last few minutes closely; the maple sugar can go from toasty to scorched quickly.
  - Add a pinch of cayenne or chipotle for a sweet-heat version that's great on soups and salads.
  - Pepitas (hulled, green) roast faster than in-shell pumpkin seeds — if you start from whole roasting-pumpkin seeds, rinse, dry, and roast at 300°F for about 45 minutes instead.

  - label: Make a batch
    note: Doubles or triples easily on two sheet pans — they keep in an airtight container for a couple of weeks.

Brittany Mullins' cozy fall move — pumpkin pie spice and a drizzle of maple — applied to
quick-roasting hulled pepitas. Toss, roast, and cool to crisp; then put them on
everything.
