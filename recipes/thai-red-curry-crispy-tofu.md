ingredients:
  - section: Crispy tofu
    items:
      - 14 oz extra-firm tofu, cubed
      - 2 tbsp cornstarch
      - 2 tbsp neutral oil
      - Pinch of salt
  - section: Curry
    items:
      - 3 tbsp red curry paste
      - 1½ cups coconut milk
      - 1 cup vegetable broth
      - 1 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp brown sugar
      - 1 red bell pepper, sliced
      - 1 cup green beans, trimmed
      - 1 zucchini, half-mooned
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - ¼ cup Thai basil
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - Jasmine rice
      - Lime wedges and extra basil
steps:
  - section: Crisp the tofu
    items:
      - Press the tofu dry, cube it, and toss with the cornstarch and salt. Pan-fry in the oil over medium-high until golden and crisp on all sides, 8–10 minutes, then remove.
  - section: Build the curry
    items:
      - In the same pan, fry the curry paste 1 minute until fragrant. Add the thick top of the coconut milk and cook until it sputters and the oil separates, about 2 minutes.
      - Add the rest of the coconut milk, broth, soy sauce, and brown sugar and simmer 3 minutes. Add the bell pepper, green beans, and zucchini and simmer 5–7 minutes, until crisp-tender.
      - Stir in the lime juice and basil. Fold the crispy tofu back in, or pile it on top to keep it crunchy.
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Serve over jasmine rice with lime wedges and extra basil.
tips:
  - Crisp the tofu first and add it at the very end (or on top) so it stays crunchy against the silky sauce.
  - Bloom the curry paste in a little of the thick coconut cream until the oil separates and it smells fragrant — that step makes the curry taste restaurant-deep.
  - Swap the protein freely — poached chicken or chunks of firm white fish go in with the vegetables; just simmer until cooked through.
  - Curry-paste brands vary wildly in heat, so start with 2 tbsp and build up to taste.

feel like a treat — but the sauce is happy to carry chicken or fish just as well.
