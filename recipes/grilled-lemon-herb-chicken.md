title: Grilled Lemon-Herb Chicken with Garden Vegetables

pitch: >-
  Proof that simple and healthy can still taste like the best night of summer.
  Chicken breasts soak in a bright marinade of lemon, garlic, rosemary, and
  thyme until they're juicy and herb-perfumed, then hit a hot grill for smoky
  char marks. Skewers of zucchini, bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes cook right
  alongside, blistering and going sweet over the flame. A final squeeze of
  lemon over everything pulls the whole plate together.
  *Pound the breasts to an even thickness before they go on — that's the
  difference between juicy all the way through and dry, leathery edges.*

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & marinade
    items:
      - 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
      - 3 tbsp olive oil
      - Juice and zest of 1 lemon
      - 3 garlic cloves, minced
      - 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped (from the garden)
      - 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves (from the garden)
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  - section: Garden vegetables
    items:
      - 1 zucchini, cut into thick half-moons
      - 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - Kosher salt and pepper
  - section: To finish
    items:
      - Lemon wedges
      - Flaky salt
      - A little extra chopped rosemary or thyme
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Whisk the olive oil, lemon juice and zest, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken and turn to coat. Marinate 30 minutes to 2 hours in the fridge.
  - section: Prep & heat
    items:
      - Heat the grill to medium-high and oil the grates. Thread the zucchini, pepper, and cherry tomatoes onto skewers and brush with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  - section: Grill
    items:
      - Grill the chicken 5–6 minutes per side until char marks form and the internal temperature reaches 160°F. Rest 5 minutes (carryover brings it to 165°F).
      - Grill the vegetable skewers alongside, turning, until charred and tender — about 3–4 minutes per side; pull the tomatoes as soon as they blister.
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Slice the chicken and plate with the grilled vegetables. Squeeze fresh lemon over everything and finish with flaky salt and a scatter of extra herbs.
tips:
  - Pound the breasts to an even thickness so they cook through at the same rate and don't dry out at the thin ends.
  - Pull the chicken at 160°F and let it rest — carryover heat finishes it at a safe, juicy 165°F.
  - "Don't over-marinate: the lemon's acid starts to firm up the surface of the chicken past a couple of hours, so 30 minutes to 2 hours is the sweet spot."
  - Oil the grates right before the chicken goes on to prevent sticking and tearing the char you worked for.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Rosemary and thyme straight from the garden make the marinade; toss a sliced serrano onto the skewers if you want a little heat.
  - label: Swap the veg
    note: Asparagus, red onion wedges, or corn on the cob all grill beautifully in place of (or alongside) the zucchini and peppers.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A chilled white like Pinot Grigio, or sparkling water with a lot of lemon for a clean summer plate.
