title: Herbed Rice Pilaf with Blistered Green Beans & a Jammy Egg

pitch: >-
  This is bistro logic applied to a pantry dinner. The rice toasts in olive oil
  with shallot until it smells like popcorn, then steams in broth while green
  beans blister hard in a dry skillet. A seven-minute egg goes on top and the
  yolk becomes the sauce. *Slice the egg at the table — the slow gold spill over
  the herbs is the dinner bell.*

ingredients:
  - section: Pilaf
    items:
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 shallot, minced
      - ½ cup long-grain rice
      - 1 cup low-sodium vegetable broth
      - ¼ tsp kosher salt
      - 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
      - 2 tbsp parsley, chopped
      - 1 tbsp chives, snipped
      - Zest of ½ lemon
  - section: Beans & egg
    items:
      - 8 oz green beans, trimmed
      - 2 tsp olive oil
      - 2 large eggs
      - Freshly ground black pepper
steps:
  - Warm the 1 tbsp oil in a small pot, soften the shallot 2 minutes, then toast the rice in it until the grains turn chalky and smell nutty.
  - Add the broth and salt, cover, and simmer on low 15 minutes; rest 5 off the heat, then fork in the thyme, parsley, chives, and lemon zest.
  - Meanwhile lower the eggs into boiling water for 7 minutes, then shock in ice water and peel.
  - Blister the green beans in the 2 tsp oil over high heat, mostly undisturbed, until deeply spotted and just tender, 5–6 minutes.
  - Pile the beans over the pilaf, halve the eggs on top, and finish with black pepper.
tips:
  - Toasting the rice before the liquid is what separates pilaf from plain rice — wait for the popcorn smell.
  - Seven minutes is the jammy line; eight gets fudgy, nine is hard. Set a real timer.
  - Let the beans sit still in the pan — moving them steals the blister.
