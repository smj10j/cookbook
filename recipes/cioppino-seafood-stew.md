ingredients:
  - section: The seafood (white fish is the constant — add any mix of the rest)
    items:
      - 1 lb cod or halibut, cut into 2-inch chunks
      - ½ lb littleneck clams, scrubbed
      - ½ lb mussels, scrubbed and debearded
      - ¼ lb lump crab or calamari
  - section: Broth
    items:
      - 3 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 onion, diced
      - 1 fennel bulb, diced
      - 4 cloves garlic, minced
      - ½ tsp red pepper flakes
      - 2 tbsp tomato paste
      - 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
      - 1 cup dry white wine
      - 3 cups fish stock
      - Pinch of saffron threads
      - 1 bay leaf
      - 1 tbsp chopped oregano
      - Salt to taste
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - ¼ cup parsley, chopped
      - Grilled bread
      - Lemon wedges
steps:
  - section: Build the broth
    items:
      - Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over medium. Cook the onion and fennel until soft, 6–8 minutes. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes and cook 1 minute, then stir in the tomato paste and cook 1 minute more.
      - Add the wine and simmer 2 minutes. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, fish stock, saffron, bay leaf, and oregano. Simmer 20 minutes, until the broth is rich and fragrant. Season with salt.
  - section: Add the seafood
    items:
      - Tuck the clams and mussels into the broth, cover, and cook 3–4 minutes, until they begin to open.
      - Add the fish chunks (and crab or calamari) and simmer gently 4–5 minutes, until the fish is just opaque and the shells have all opened. Discard any that stay shut.
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Shower with parsley and ladle into bowls. Serve with grilled bread and lemon wedges.
tips:
  - The fish and broth are the whole recipe — the clams, mussels, crab, and calamari are all optional. Use any mix to about 1 lb total, or skip them entirely for a fish-only stew that Stephen can dig into.
  - Poach gently and pull the pot the moment the fish turns opaque; seafood overcooks fast in hot broth.
  - The broth is even better made a day ahead — cool and chill it, then reheat and add the seafood just before serving.
  - No fish stock? Use bottled clam juice, or a mix of vegetable broth and a splash of the wine.
