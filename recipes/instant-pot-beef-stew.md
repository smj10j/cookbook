pitch: >-
  This is the stew that fills the kitchen with star anise and ginger the second the
  lid comes off. Flank steak gets browned hard in the Instant Pot, then simmered
  under pressure with fennel, fish sauce, soy, and a few whole star anise until the
  meat goes spoon-tender and the broth turns deep and savory-sweet. It lands
  somewhere between a Vietnamese bò kho and a Chinese braise — bright, aromatic, and
  built for a bowl of rice or crusty bread. *Fish sauce and star anise are the
  backbone here: one brings the funky umami, the other the warm, licorice perfume
  that makes the whole pot taste slow-cooked in 45 minutes flat.*

ingredients:
  - section: Stew
    items:
      - 1 lb flank steak, cut into 1–2 inch pieces
      - 1 tbsp sesame oil
      - 1 onion, chopped
      - 4 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 inch fresh ginger, sliced
      - 1 small bulb fennel, thinly sliced
      - 1 tbsp fish sauce
      - 2 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
      - 1 tbsp sugar (or honey)
      - "1 tbsp hot sauce (optional, to taste)"
      - 2–3 whole star anise
      - 4 cups beef broth (or water)
      - Salt and pepper, to taste
  - section: To finish & serve
    items:
      - 4–5 scallions, cut into 2-inch pieces (reserve some for garnish)
      - Fresh cilantro, for garnish
      - Steamed rice or crusty bread
steps:
  - section: Brown & build
    items:
      - Set the Instant Pot to Sauté. When hot, add the sesame oil, onion, garlic, and ginger and cook 2–3 minutes until aromatic.
      - Add the flank steak and brown on all sides to build flavor in the base.
      - Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and hot sauce. Cook 1–2 minutes so the sauces coat the beef.
  - section: Pressure cook
    items:
      - Add the fennel, star anise, and broth, stirring so the beef and fennel are submerged.
      - Seal the lid and pressure-cook on high for 35 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release the rest.
  - section: Finish
    items:
      - Open the lid and stir in the scallions. Taste and adjust with salt and pepper.
      - Ladle into bowls over rice (or with bread alongside) and garnish with reserved scallions and cilantro.
tips:
  - Brown the flank steak in batches if your pot is crowded — a hard sear on the meat is where the broth's depth comes from.
  - Star anise is potent; two to three pods is plenty, and pull them before serving so no one bites one.
  - For more body and sweetness, add carrot or daikon coins before pressure cooking — they soften into the broth and round it out.
  - Adjust the fish sauce and sugar at the end, a little at a time, to balance salty against sweet to your taste.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Finish each bowl with a heap of garden cilantro and a sliced serrano or jalapeño for fresh heat that cuts the rich broth.
  - label: Make ahead
    note: Like most braises, this is even better the next day — the spices deepen overnight. Store and reheat gently on the stove.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A bowl of jasmine rice or a torn baguette, plus a cold lager or an off-dry Riesling.

A pantry-and-garden fusion that leans Vietnamese with a Chinese braise's backbone:
fish sauce, star anise, ginger, and fennel doing the work while the Instant Pot
turns flank steak tender. Bright, aromatic, and ready for rice or bread.
