  This is the pumpkin pie that ruins canned filling for you forever. You roast a sugar
  pumpkin until it slumps and sweetens, purée it down, and whisk it into a glossy spiced
  custard set with eggs and evaporated milk. It pours into an all-butter crust and bakes
  into a deep, burnished round that quivers ever so slightly in the center when you pull it.
  *Roast your own pumpkin — the purée is richer, less watery, and tastes like actual squash
  instead of a tin.*

ingredients:
  - section: Pumpkin purée
    items:
      - 1 pie pumpkin (2½–3 lb)
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
  - section: Crust
    items:
      - 1¼ cups all-purpose flour
      - ½ tsp salt
      - 1 tbsp sugar
      - ½ cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
      - 3–4 tbsp ice water (or use a good store-bought crust)
  - section: Filling
    items:
      - 2 cups fresh pumpkin purée
      - ¾ cup brown sugar
      - ¼ cup granulated sugar
      - ½ tsp salt
      - 1½ tsp cinnamon
      - ½ tsp ground ginger
      - ¼ tsp ground cloves
      - ¼ tsp nutmeg
      - 2 large eggs plus 1 yolk
      - 1 cup evaporated milk or heavy cream
      - 1 tsp vanilla extract
steps:
  - Halve and seed the pumpkin, rub the cut sides with the oil, and roast cut-side down at 400°F for 35–45 minutes until fork-tender. Scoop and purée smooth, draining in a sieve if watery, then measure out 2 cups.
  - For the crust, mix the flour, salt, and sugar, then cut in the cold butter until the mixture looks like coarse crumbs.
  - Add ice water a tablespoon at a time, just until the dough holds together, then chill it 30 minutes.
  - Roll the dough out and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish, crimp the edge, and chill again while you make the filling.
  - For the filling, whisk the eggs and yolk, then whisk in the purée, both sugars, salt, spices, and vanilla.
  - Stream in the evaporated milk or cream and whisk until completely smooth, then pour into the chilled crust.
  - Bake at 425°F for 15 minutes, then reduce the heat to 350°F and bake 40–45 minutes more, until the center is just set and barely jiggles.
  - Cool completely, about 2 hours, before slicing so the custard firms up.
tips:
  - A fresh pie pumpkin beats canned every time — the purée is richer, sweeter, and far less watery.
  - If your purée seems loose, drain it in a sieve or cheesecloth before measuring, or the custard won't set cleanly.
  - Resist cutting it warm — let the pie cool fully so the custard sets into clean, sliceable wedges.
  - Serve with lightly sweetened whipped cream or a thin drizzle of maple syrup over each slice.
extras:
  - label: Make it ahead
    note: Bake the pie a day early and refrigerate — the flavor deepens overnight and the slices cut even cleaner cold.
  - label: Blind-bake insurance
    note: For an extra-crisp bottom, line the crust with foil and pie weights and bake 12 minutes at 375°F before filling.
