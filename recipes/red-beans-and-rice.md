ingredients:
  - section: Red beans
    items:
      - ½ lb dried red kidney beans, soaked overnight (or quick-soaked)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 yellow onion, diced
      - 1 green bell pepper, diced
      - 2 celery stalks, diced
      - 4 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1½ tsp smoked paprika
      - 1 tsp dried thyme
      - 1 tsp dried oregano
      - ¼–½ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
      - 1–2 bay leaves
      - ½ tsp smoked salt or 1 tsp soy sauce (optional, for depth)
      - 4 cups vegetable broth or water
      - Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper (added at the end)
      - 1 tsp red wine vinegar or hot sauce, to finish
  - section: Rice & garnish
    items:
      - 1 cup long-grain white rice
      - 2 cups water
      - 2 green onions, thinly sliced
      - 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
      - Hot sauce, for serving
steps:
  - section: Soak
    items:
      - Soak the beans overnight in cold water, or quick-soak — bring them to a boil for 5 minutes, then cover, take off the heat, and let sit 1 hour. Drain and rinse.
  - section: Build the base
    items:
      - Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery (the holy trinity) and cook 7–9 minutes, letting them brown a little for deeper flavor.
      - Add the garlic and cook 1 minute, then stir in the smoked paprika, thyme, oregano, and cayenne and toast 30 seconds until fragrant.
  - section: Simmer
    items:
      - Add the drained beans, bay leaves, the optional smoked salt or soy sauce, and the broth. Bring to a boil, then lower to a gentle simmer.
      - Simmer uncovered 1½–2½ hours, stirring occasionally and adding water if it gets dry, until the beans are completely tender and the liquid has thickened.
      - Mash a ladleful of beans against the side of the pot and stir it back in to thicken the gravy and make it creamy.
      - Only now season generously with salt and black pepper. Discard the bay leaves and stir in the vinegar or hot sauce to brighten.
  - section: Rice & serve
    items:
      - While the beans finish, rinse the rice, then combine with the water and a pinch of salt in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce to low, and cook 15–18 minutes until tender. Let rest 5 minutes, then fluff.
      - Spoon the beans over the rice and finish with green onions, parsley, and more hot sauce at the table.
tips:
  - Salt the beans only at the very end — added early, salt toughens the skins and stops them from turning creamy.
  - Smoked paprika is doing the work that andouille usually does; bloom it in the hot oil with the other spices so its flavor opens up. A drop of liquid smoke pushes it even further if you like.
  - For the silkiest texture, mash more beans than you think you need — that starchy gravy is the whole point of red beans and rice.
  - This is a make-ahead dream — it tastes even better the next day, so cook the full pot and reheat with a splash of broth.
extras:
  - label: Add chicken sausage
    note: For a meaty version, brown 2 sliced chicken andouille or smoked chicken sausages in the pot first, remove, then build the base in the drippings and return the sausage to simmer with the beans.
  - label: Instant Pot
    note: Skip the soak. Sauté the trinity and spices on the Sauté setting, add the rinsed beans and broth, then cook on High Pressure 35–40 minutes with a natural release before mashing and seasoning.
  - label: From the garden
    note: Stir in fresh thyme from the garden and finish with chopped parsley; a diced garden serrano or jalapeño in the trinity adds fresh heat.

Stephen's red beans skip the andouille and turkey entirely and lean on smoked
paprika, a hard-browned trinity, and a long, low simmer for that signature Cajun
depth. Mashing some of the beans back into the pot gives you the creamy gravy with
no cream — and an optional chicken-sausage version is right there if you want it.
