pitch: >-
  Gochujang is the sweet-savory-spicy note most weeknight salmon is missing, and here it
  turns into a glaze that caramelizes into a sticky, blistered crust. The fillets get
  coated, broiled until the edges char and bubble, and finished with a second brush of
  glaze for shine, over quick sesame-charred bok choy. *Run it under the broiler for the
  last minute and the glaze goes lacquered and blackened-in-spots — the best part.* It's
  on the table in twenty-odd minutes and built on the salmon you cook all the time.

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon & glaze
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each)
      - 2 tbsp gochujang
      - 1 tbsp honey
      - 1 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
      - 1 tsp sesame oil
      - 2 cloves garlic, grated
      - 1 tsp grated ginger
  - section: Sesame bok choy
    items:
      - 4 heads baby bok choy, halved
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 tsp sesame oil
      - 2 cloves garlic, sliced
      - 1 tbsp soy sauce
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 2 scallions, thinly sliced
      - 1 tbsp sesame seeds
      - Steamed rice
steps:
  - section: Glaze
    items:
      - Whisk the gochujang, honey, soy, rice vinegar, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Pat the salmon dry and coat it with half the glaze; reserve the rest for finishing. Let it sit 10 minutes if you have time.
  - section: Broil
    items:
      - Heat the broiler with a rack in the upper third and line a sheet pan with foil. Broil the salmon 6–8 minutes, until the glaze caramelizes and the fish reaches 125–130°F, brushing on the reserved glaze halfway through.
  - section: Bok choy & plate
    items:
      - Meanwhile, heat the neutral and sesame oils in a skillet over medium-high. Add the garlic, then the bok choy cut-side down, and sear 2 minutes.
      - Splash in the soy sauce and a tablespoon of water, cover, and steam 2 minutes more, until crisp-tender.
      - Plate the salmon and bok choy over rice, and finish with scallions and sesame seeds.
tips:
  - Reserve half the glaze for the end — gochujang scorches fast, so the second coat goes on after the heat for shine without burning.
  - Broil on the upper rack and watch it; you want lacquered, blistered edges, not blackened ones.
  - Pull the salmon at 125°F for silky or 130°F for firm — it keeps cooking off the heat.
  - "Heat dial: gochujang brands vary a lot — start at 2 tbsp and add a pinch of gochugaru or a squeeze of sriracha if you want more burn."
