  This is the whole Fresa's plate at home: a butterflied chicken lacquered in
  achiote and freshly squeezed orange and lime until it's stained a deep
  brick red, then grilled skin-down over wood until the annatto edges char and
  the citrus caramelizes. It comes off the fire alongside smoky charred onions

  tomato-stained Mexican rice. *The move is a hot two-zone fire and a patient
  skin-side-down sear — that's where the achiote turns to a crackling, savory
  crust instead of steaming pale.* Pile it onto warm corn tortillas with a
  squeeze of lime and the creamy jalapeño salsa, and it eats like the patio on
  South First.

ingredients:
  - section: Achiote citrus marinade
    items:
      - 3 tbsp achiote paste (recado rojo)
      - ½ cup fresh orange juice
      - ¼ cup fresh lime juice
      - 6 cloves garlic, grated
      - 2 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 tbsp Mexican oregano
      - 1 tsp black pepper
      - 2 tsp kosher salt
      - 3 tbsp olive oil
  - section: Chicken
    items:
      - 3.5 lb whole chicken, spatchcocked
  - section: Black beans
    items:
      - 3 cups cooked black beans (two 15-oz cans, drained and rinsed)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - ½ white onion, finely diced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 bay leaf
      - ¾ cup chicken broth
      - ½ tsp kosher salt
      - 2 tbsp chopped cilantro
  - section: Mexican rice
    items:
      - 1 cup long-grain white rice
      - 2 tbsp neutral oil
      - ¼ white onion, finely diced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 2 tbsp tomato paste
      - 1¾ cups chicken broth
      - ½ tsp ground cumin
      - ½ tsp kosher salt
  - section: Grilled onions & jalapeños
    items:
      - 1 large white onion, cut into thick rounds
      - 4 whole jalapeños
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - Pinch of kosher salt
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - Corn tortillas, warmed
      - Lime wedges
      - Chopped cilantro
      - Creamy jalapeño-cilantro salsa (recipe in this book)
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Whisk all the marinade ingredients into a loose, brick-red paste — the achiote should dissolve into the citrus and oil.
      - "Pat the chicken dry and spatchcock it: with kitchen shears, cut out the backbone and press the bird flat, breast-up, until you hear the keel bone crack."
      - Coat the chicken all over with the marinade, working it under the skin and into every crease. Chill at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
  - section: Black beans
    items:
      - Warm the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat and soften the onion 4–5 minutes. Add the garlic and cumin and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
      - Add the black beans, bay leaf, broth, and salt. Simmer gently 15–20 minutes, mashing a few beans against the side to thicken. Discard the bay leaf and stir in the cilantro.
  - section: Mexican rice
    items:
      - Heat the neutral oil in a lidded pan over medium. Add the rice and toast, stirring, until it smells nutty and turns chalky-white, 3–4 minutes.
      - Stir in the onion, garlic, and tomato paste and cook 1 minute. Add the broth, cumin, and salt, bring to a boil, then cover and cook on low 18 minutes. Rest off heat 5 minutes and fluff.
  - section: Grill the chicken
    items:
      - Build a two-zone fire and add a handful of soaked post oak or hardwood chips. Bring the direct side to medium-high; oil the grates.
      - Start the chicken skin-side DOWN over the direct side, 6–8 minutes, until the achiote crust chars and releases cleanly. Flip and move to the indirect side.
      - Cover and grill over indirect heat 25–35 minutes, until the thigh reads 165°F. Rest 10 minutes, then cut into quarters.
  - section: Onions, jalapeños & serve
    items:
      - While the chicken rests, toss the onion rounds and whole jalapeños with olive oil and salt. Grill over direct heat, turning, until blistered and soft, 6–8 minutes.
      - Serve the carved chicken with the beans, rice, charred onions and jalapeños, warm tortillas, lime wedges, cilantro, and plenty of the creamy jalapeño salsa.
tips:
  - "White meat, dark meat, one bird: a whole spatchcocked chicken carves into both, so Lauren can take the breast and Stephen gets the thighs and legs — nobody has to compromise."
  - Achiote paste is stubborn — loosen it in the citrus and oil first (a few pulses in a blender helps), so it coats evenly instead of clumping.
  - Work the marinade UNDER the skin, not just over it, and give it overnight; the annatto and citrus need time to stain the meat, not just the skin.
  - Wood is the point of "al carbon" — a few soaked post oak or mesquite chips on the coals gives that Fresa's smoke even on a gas grill.

Stephen's home version of the Fresa's plate he and Lauren order on South First: a
whole bird stained deep red with achiote and fresh citrus, grilled over wood, and
served the family-style way — with beans, tomato-stained rice, blistered onions and
jalapeños, warm tortillas, and that creamy green salsa. One chicken feeds both the
white-meat and the dark-meat camp.
