ingredients:
  - section: Mushrooms
    items:
      - 8 oz mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, oyster), sliced
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tbsp butter
      - 1 tbsp thyme
      - 2 tbsp parsley, chopped
  - section: Risotto
    items:
      - 1 cup arborio rice
      - 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
      - ½ cup dry white wine
      - 1 shallot, minced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 3 tbsp butter
      - ½ cup grated parmesan
      - Salt and pepper
steps:
  - section: Sear the mushrooms
    items:
      - Heat the oil and butter in a wide pan over medium-high. Sear the mushrooms in a single layer until deep golden, 6–8 minutes. Stir in the thyme and a pinch of salt, then set aside, reserving some for garnish.
  - section: Build the risotto
    items:
      - In the same pan, warm the oil over medium and cook the shallot 2 minutes. Add the garlic and rice and toast 2 minutes, until the edges of the grains turn translucent.
      - Add the wine and stir until absorbed. Add the warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring often and letting each ladle absorb before the next, 18–22 minutes, until the rice is creamy and al dente.
  - section: Finish
    items:
      - Off the heat, beat in the butter and parmesan until glossy, then fold in most of the mushrooms. Season with salt and pepper.
      - Spoon into shallow bowls and top with the reserved mushrooms, parsley, and more parmesan.
tips:
  - Keep the broth hot in a separate pot — cold broth shocks the rice and stops it releasing the starch that becomes the "cream" (no dairy required).
  - Sear the mushrooms hard and don't crowd the pan; you want deep browning, not steam.
  - The final off-heat beating of butter and cheese — mantecatura — is what makes it glossy and luxurious. Don't skip it.
  - It should spread like lava, not stand in a mound; loosen with a splash more broth if it tightens up.
