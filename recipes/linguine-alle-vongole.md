title: Linguine alle Vongole (Linguine with Clams)
slug: linguine-alle-vongole
tagline: Linguine finished in a briny garlic-white-wine clam sauce with chili and lemon.
pitch: >-
  The Italian-coast classic, done right: fresh clams steam open in a pan of garlic, white
  wine, olive oil, and chili flake, then the linguine finishes cooking in that briny liquor
  so every strand drinks up the sauce, with parsley and lemon to brighten. *No cream, no
  cheese — the clams' own salty juice and a knob of butter are the entire sauce, and they're
  plenty.* It's a fast, restaurant-feeling weeknight dish and a natural counterpart to the
  piccata-style pastas already in this book.
serves: 2

ingredients:
  - section: Clams & sauce
    items:
      - 2 lb littleneck clams, scrubbed
      - 12 oz linguine
      - ¼ cup olive oil
      - 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
      - ½ tsp red pepper flakes
      - ½ cup dry white wine
      - 2 tbsp butter
      - ¼ cup parsley, chopped
      - 1 lemon
      - ½ cup reserved pasta water
      - Salt
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Scrub the clams and discard any that won't close. Soak them in cold salted water 20 minutes to purge grit, then drain. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil for the pasta.
  - section: Steam the clams
    items:
      - Heat the oil in a large pan over medium. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes and cook until the garlic is pale gold, 1–2 minutes — don't let it burn.
      - Add the wine and clams, cover, and steam 4–6 minutes, until they open. Discard any that stay shut. Lift the clams out, leaving the briny liquor in the pan; pull some meats from their shells if you like.
  - section: Finish the pasta
    items:
      - Cook the linguine to 1 minute shy of al dente and reserve ½ cup pasta water. Add the pasta to the clam liquor with a splash of pasta water and toss vigorously 1–2 minutes to emulsify.
      - Off the heat, swirl in the butter, parsley, and a squeeze of lemon, then return the clams. Twirl into bowls and spoon the clams and sauce over.
tips:
  - Purge the clams in salted water before cooking so they spit out their grit — nothing ruins vongole like sand.
  - No cheese on vongole — the briny clam liquor and a knob of butter are the whole sauce.
  - Finish the pasta in the clam broth with a splash of starchy pasta water; that's what makes the sauce cling instead of pool.
  - Swap for Stephen — build the same garlic-wine-chili sauce without clams and toss in seared firm white fish (or sautéed mushrooms) for a shellfish-free "vongole."
