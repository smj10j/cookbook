pitch: >-
  Halibut is the meatiest, most luxurious of the white fish, and the move here is a
  hard cast-iron sear that lacquers the top golden while the inside stays just-set and
  silky. It lands on a warm succotash of blistered summer corn, jammy cherry tomatoes,
  shallot, and basil, then gets a spoonful of brown butter poured over at the table.
  *The whole trick is pulling it at 130°F so it flakes in big translucent petals instead
  of drying to cotton.* It's a thirty-minute, one-pan dinner that eats like the coast.

ingredients:
  - section: Halibut
    items:
      - 2 halibut fillets (about 6 oz each), skinless
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tbsp butter
      - Kosher salt and black pepper
  - section: Charred corn succotash
    items:
      - 2 ears corn, kernels cut off (about 1½ cups)
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      - 1 shallot, finely diced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tbsp butter
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 tbsp chopped basil
      - 1 tbsp lemon juice
      - Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
      - Salt and pepper to taste
  - section: Brown butter
    items:
      - 2 tbsp butter
      - 1 tbsp lemon juice
      - 1 tbsp chopped basil
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Pat the halibut very dry, season both sides with salt and pepper, and let it sit at room temperature while you make the succotash.
      - Cut the corn kernels off the cobs, halve the tomatoes, dice the shallot, and mince the garlic.
  - section: Succotash
    items:
      - Heat 1 tbsp oil and 1 tbsp butter in a cast-iron skillet over medium-high. Add the corn in an even layer and let it char undisturbed 2–3 minutes, then stir and cook 2 minutes more.
      - Add the shallot and garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant. Add the tomatoes and red pepper flakes and cook 2–3 minutes, until the tomatoes slump.
      - Stir in the basil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Scrape into a bowl and wipe out the pan.
  - section: Sear & finish
    items:
      - Heat 1 tbsp oil in the same pan over medium-high until shimmering. Lay the halibut down presentation-side first and sear undisturbed 3–4 minutes, until deep golden.
      - Flip, add 1 tbsp butter, and baste 2–3 minutes, until the fish reaches 130°F at the center and flakes. Rest 2 minutes.
      - Wipe the pan, melt 2 tbsp butter over medium, and swirl until it smells nutty and turns golden-brown, about 2 minutes. Off the heat, stir in the lemon juice and basil.
      - Spoon the succotash onto plates, set the halibut on top, and pour the brown butter over.
tips:
  - Halibut is lean and dries out fast — pull it at 130°F and let carryover heat finish it to a flaky 135°F off the pan.
  - Get the fish bone-dry and the pan properly hot before it goes in; that's the entire secret to a golden crust that doesn't stick.
  - No cast iron? Any heavy stainless pan works — just skip nonstick, which won't build the same crust.
  - Not at the counter? This works beautifully with cod, sea bass, or swordfish in halibut's place.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Stir garden basil (and a little thyme) into the succotash, and use ripe garden cherry tomatoes for blistering.

  - label: Pairs with
