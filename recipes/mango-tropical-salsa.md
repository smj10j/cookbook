ingredients:
  - 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced small
  - ¼ cup red cherry tomatoes, quartered
  - ¼ cup green cherry tomatoes, halved or quartered
  - 1 small cucumber, peeled, seeded, and diced
  - 1 jalapeño, finely diced (seeded for less heat)
  - 1 serrano, very finely diced (with seeds if you're bold)
  - ½ habanero, minced very finely (start with less if unsure)
  - 2 tbsp red onion, finely diced
  - 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  - 2 tbsp fresh basil, chiffonade (or cilantro for a classic profile)
  - Zest and juice of 1 lime
  - 1 tsp rice vinegar (optional, for brightness)
  - Pinch of kosher salt, to taste
steps:
  - Dice the mango, cucumber, and onion small and keep everything roughly uniform — aim for a salsa-crudo texture, never pureed.
  - Mince the jalapeño, serrano, and habanero very finely. Taste a sliver of each first; heat varies, so adjust to your comfort.
  - In a bowl, combine the mango, both tomatoes, cucumber, onion, all the peppers, and garlic.
  - Add the lime zest and juice, the vinegar if using, salt, and basil. Stir gently to combine.
  - Let it sit at room temperature 10–15 minutes (or chill up to 4 hours) so the flavors meld. Taste and adjust salt, lime, and heat before serving.
tips:
  - Balance the heat by adding more mango or cucumber, not by skimping on flavor — sweetness and cool crunch tame the habanero.
  - Treat the basil like mint — let it lift the salsa rather than dominate. A tiny chiffonade goes a long way.
  - For a smoky depth, grill the mango (or cucumber) briefly before dicing — the char plays beautifully against the fruit's sweetness.
  - No fresh mango? Stir in 2–3 tbsp pineapple juice, or simmer ¼ cup down to a syrupy reduction and use a spoonful for concentrated tropical punch.
