  tacos, eggs, or anything that needs waking up.

ingredients:
  - 6 fresh jalapeños, stemmed (seed some for less heat)
  - 2 serrano peppers, stemmed (optional, for extra heat)
  - 4 cloves garlic, unpeeled
  - 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves and tender stems
  - Juice of 1 lime
  - ½ tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
  - ½ cup neutral oil (canola or grapeseed)
steps:
  - section: Char the peppers and garlic
    items:
      - Set the broiler to high and position a rack 4–6 inches below the element. Line a sheet pan with foil.
      - Spread the jalapeños, serranos, and unpeeled garlic on the pan. Broil about 10 minutes, rotating the peppers every 2–3 minutes, until blistered and charred all over. Tuck the garlic under a pepper so it softens gently without burning.
      - Let everything cool slightly. Peel the garlic. Seed some or all of the peppers if you want to dial the heat back.
  - section: Blend and emulsify
    items:
      - In a blender, combine the roasted peppers, garlic, cilantro, lime juice, and salt. Blend until mostly smooth.
      - With the blender running on low, slowly drizzle in the oil until the salsa turns creamy, pale green, and emulsified. Thin with a tablespoon of water at a time if needed.
      - Taste and adjust salt and lime. Let it rest at least 30 minutes for the flavors to meld before serving.
tips:
  - The slow oil drizzle is non-negotiable — pour it in a thin stream while blending so it emulsifies instead of separating.
  - Keep the garlic slightly off to the side under the broiler, or tuck it beneath a pepper; it needs a gentler roast than the peppers.
  - Taste a sliver of each pepper before blending — heat varies wildly even within one batch, so seed accordingly.
  - The salsa deepens overnight; if it's too fiery fresh, a night in the fridge mellows the edge.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Built for garden jalapeños and serranos. For a smokier, slightly sweet version, char a handful of ripe cherry tomatoes alongside the peppers and blend them in.
  - label: No-cook shortcut
    note: Skip the broiler and blend the peppers raw for a brighter, grassier, more aggressive heat — faster, with a more vivid green.
  - label: Storage
    note: Keeps in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Great on tacos, grilled meats, eggs, or avocado toast.
