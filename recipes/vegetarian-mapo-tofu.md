ingredients:
  - section: Mains
    items:
      - 14 oz soft tofu, cubed
      - 4 oz shiitake or cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  - section: Sauce
    items:
      - 2 tbsp doubanjiang (chili bean paste)
      - 1 tbsp fermented black beans, rinsed and chopped (optional)
      - 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, toasted and ground
      - 3 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tbsp grated ginger
      - 3 scallions, sliced (whites and greens separated)
      - 2 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 tsp red pepper flakes
      - 1 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine
      - 1 tsp granulated sugar
      - 1 cup vegetable broth
      - 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - Steamed rice
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Gently cube the tofu and slide it into a bowl of warm salted water to hold (this keeps it intact). Toast the Sichuan peppercorns in a dry pan until fragrant, then grind.
  - section: Build the sauce
    items:
      - Heat the oil in a wok over medium-high. Add the mushrooms and cook until browned and dry, 4–5 minutes. Push them aside, add the doubanjiang and black beans, and fry 1 minute until the oil turns red.
      - Add the garlic, ginger, scallion whites, and red pepper flakes and cook 30 seconds. Add the soy, Shaoxing, sugar, and broth and simmer 2 minutes.
  - section: Finish
    items:
      - Drain the tofu and slide it in. Simmer gently 3–4 minutes, nudging rather than stirring so the cubes stay whole. Stir in the cornstarch slurry and simmer until glossy and thickened.
      - Off the heat, sprinkle with the ground Sichuan pepper and scallion greens. Serve over steamed rice.
tips:
  - The mushrooms are the meat here — brown them hard and dry so they turn chewy and savory, the texture that usually comes from pork.
  - Fry the doubanjiang in the oil until it stains the oil red; that bloomed chili-bean flavor is the backbone of mapo.
  - Nudge, don't stir — soft tofu falls apart if you're rough, so fold gently with the back of the spoon.
  - That numbing tingle is the má in mapo — toast and grind whole Sichuan peppercorns fresh for the full buzz.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Scallion greens and a pinch of garden serrano for extra heat.
  - label: Make it heartier

  - label: Dial the heat
    note: Go easy on the doubanjiang and flakes for mild, or add a spoon of chili oil for a real Sichuan burn.

Mapo tofu without the pork loses nothing — browned mushrooms bring the savory chew, and the
doubanjiang-and-Sichuan-pepper sauce does the rest. Numbing, spicy, and deeply comforting
over rice.
