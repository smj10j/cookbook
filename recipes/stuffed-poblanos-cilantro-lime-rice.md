pitch: >-
  Poblanos are the rare pepper that gets silkier as it roasts, and these are
  roasted twice — once naked until the skins blister, once stuffed until the
  filling steams into the flesh. Inside is rice folded with charred corn, a
  handful of cilantro, and enough lime zest to make the whole thing hum.
  *Queso fresco goes on as a garnish, not a filling — one crumbled ounce reads
  as abundance without turning it into a cheese dish.*

ingredients:
  - section: Poblanos
    items:
      - 2 large poblano peppers, halved lengthwise and seeded
      - 2 tsp olive oil
  - section: Cilantro-lime rice & corn
    items:
      - ⅓ cup long-grain rice
      - ⅔ cup low-sodium vegetable broth
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - ¾ cup fresh or frozen corn kernels
      - 2 scallions, thinly sliced
      - ½ cup cilantro, chopped
      - Zest and juice of 1 lime
      - ½ tsp ground cumin
      - Salt to taste (the queso and lime usually carry it)
  - section: To finish
    items:
      - 1 oz queso fresco, crumbled
      - Lime wedges, for serving
steps:
  - Heat the oven to 425°F. Rub the poblano halves with the olive oil, set them cut side down on a sheet pan, and roast 12–15 minutes until the skins blister and the flesh slumps.
  - Meanwhile simmer the rice in the broth, covered, until tender and the liquid is absorbed, about 15 minutes.
  - Char the corn in a dry skillet over high heat until it pops and catches color in spots, 3–4 minutes.
  - Fold the corn, scallions, cilantro, lime zest and juice, cumin, salt, and the 1 tbsp olive oil into the rice.
  - Flip the poblanos cut side up, mound the filling in, and roast 8–10 minutes more until the tops toast.
  - Shower with queso fresco and serve with lime wedges.
tips:
  - Pick poblanos that sit flat when halved — they hold the filling instead of tipping it onto the pan.
  - Charring the corn dry (no oil) is what gets the toasty, esquites-like flavor.
  - If a pepper tears, overlap the split — the filling glues it shut as it bakes.
