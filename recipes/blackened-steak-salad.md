pitch: >-
  Hot steak, cold greens — the contrast is the whole point. A sirloin gets a
  brown-sugar-and-cayenne rub that chars into a dark, smoky crust on a screaming
  grill, then rests while cherry tomatoes blister in the same heat until their
  skins split and go jammy. Everything lands on mixed baby greens with crisp red
  onion, green pepper, and a balsamic-honey Dijon that you shake in a jar.
  *The money move: pull the steak 5°F early and let carryover finish it, so every
  slice stays pink against the cool leaves.*

ingredients:
  - section: Steak & rub
    items:
      - 1 lb sirloin steak (or tenderloin)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil (for rub adhesion)
      - 1½ tsp kosher salt
      - 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
      - 1½ tsp smoked paprika
      - ½ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
      - ½ tsp onion powder
      - 1 tsp brown sugar
  - section: Optional marinade
    items:
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
      - 1 tsp soy sauce
      - 1 clove garlic, minced
  - section: Salad
    items:
      - 4 cups mixed baby greens
      - 1 cup cherry or grape tomatoes
      - ½ red onion, thinly sliced
      - 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
      - ⅓ cup crumbled blue cheese
  - section: Balsamic-honey Dijon
    items:
      - ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
      - 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
      - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tsp honey
      - Pinch of salt and pepper
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - If marinating, combine the marinade ingredients and refrigerate the steak 1–2 hours. Remove, pat very dry.
      - Rub the steak lightly with olive oil. Mix the rub ingredients and coat the steak evenly, pressing it in.
      - Whisk (or jar-shake) the dressing until emulsified. Slice the vegetables; soak the red onion in ice water for 10 minutes.
  - section: Grill
    items:
      - Heat the grill to high (~500°F) and oil the grates. Sear the steak 2–3 minutes per side until a dark crust forms.
      - Move to medium/indirect heat (~350°F) and cook to 125°F for medium-rare (it climbs to 130°F as it rests).
      - While the steak rests 5–10 minutes, toss the tomatoes in a little oil and blister them over high heat until the skins split and char.
  - section: Assemble
    items:
      - Bed the greens with red onion, bell pepper, and blistered tomatoes.
      - Slice the rested steak thinly against the grain and fan it over the top.
      - Scatter blue cheese, drizzle with the dressing, and finish with cracked pepper.
tips:
  - To find the grain, look for the parallel muscle fibers and slice perpendicular to them — short fibers mean tender bites.
  - Pull the steak 5°F below your target; carryover heat finishes it during the rest so it never overcooks.
  - Soaking the sliced red onion in ice water for 10 minutes mellows its bite and makes it crisp.
  - A jar with a tight lid emulsifies the dressing better than a whisk — and stores the leftovers.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Use garden cherry tomatoes for blistering and stir a pinch of chopped thyme or basil into the dressing.
  - label: Add crunch
    note: Toasted walnuts, pecans, or pepitas; sliced avocado for creaminess; or grilled corn for sweetness.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A bold red — Zinfandel or Syrah. Serve the warm steak over the cool salad for the temperature contrast.

A house favorite that Stephen has dialed in over several rounds: the brown sugar in
the rub caramelizes against the cayenne for a sweet-hot crust, and blistering the
tomatoes on the same grill turns this from a plain steak salad into something with
real restaurant contrast. The blue cheese is the upgrade from the original feta.
