title: Moroccan Chicken Tagine with Green Olives & Preserved Lemon
slug: moroccan-chicken-tagine
tagline: Spice-rubbed chicken thighs braised with saffron, green olives, and preserved lemon over couscous.
pitch: >-
  A new cuisine built from flavors you already reach for — olives, lemon, warm spice.
  Chicken thighs get a cumin-paprika-ginger rub and a hard sear, then braise low with
  onion, saffron, cracked green olives, and preserved lemon until they're falling off the
  bone in a glossy, briny sauce. *The preserved lemon is the whole magic trick — it goes
  silky and floral and makes the entire pot taste expensive.* It's a forgiving one-pot
  braise that scales up, reheats even better, and lands over buttery couscous.

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & spice rub
    items:
      - 6 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (about 2 lb)
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 tsp paprika
      - 1 tsp ground ginger
      - ½ tsp turmeric
      - ½ tsp cinnamon
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - ½ tsp black pepper
  - section: Braise
    items:
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 large onion, sliced
      - 4 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger
      - 1 tsp ground coriander
      - Pinch of saffron threads
      - 1½ cups chicken broth
      - 1 preserved lemon, rind only, sliced
      - ¾ cup green olives (Castelvetrano or cracked)
  - section: To finish & serve
    items:
      - ¼ cup cilantro, chopped
      - ¼ cup parsley, chopped
      - Couscous, for serving
steps:
  - section: Sear
    items:
      - Pat the thighs dry. Mix the cumin, paprika, ground ginger, turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper, and rub it all over the chicken.
      - Heat the oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high. Brown the thighs skin-side down 5–6 minutes until golden, flip for 2 minutes, then remove.
  - section: Braise
    items:
      - Lower the heat and cook the onion 5 minutes until soft. Add the garlic, fresh ginger, coriander, and saffron and cook 1 minute.
      - Pour in the broth and scrape up the browned bits. Nestle the chicken back in skin-side up and scatter the preserved lemon and olives around it.
      - Cover and simmer gently (or braise in a 350°F oven) 35–40 minutes, until the chicken is tender and nearly falling off the bone. Uncover for the last 10 minutes to reduce the sauce.
  - section: Finish
    items:
      - Shower with the cilantro and parsley and serve over couscous, spooning the briny sauce on top.
tips:
  - Use only the rind of the preserved lemon — scrape away and discard the soft, intensely salty pulp, then slice the rind into thin ribbons.
  - Brown the thighs hard and don't crowd the pan; that fond is the backbone of the sauce.
  - The salt comes mostly from the olives and preserved lemon, so hold off on adding more until the very end.
  - Like most braises, it's even better the next day — cool, chill, and reheat gently.

  - label: Pairs with

The classic Moroccan pairing — salt-cured lemon and green olives against saffron and warm
spice — does all the work here. Bone-in thighs stay juicy through the long braise and make
their own rich sauce.
