title: Reverse-Sear New York Strip with Garlic-Herb Broccoli
slug: reverse-sear-ny-strip
tagline: A low-and-slow strip finished with a screaming butter-baste, beside charred garden broccoli.
pitch: >-
  This is the steakhouse trick that takes the guesswork out of a perfect crust.
  The strip starts in a gentle 250°F oven on a wire rack until it creeps to
  115°F, edge-to-edge rosy and dry on the surface — then it hits a smoking
  cast-iron for a 60-second-a-side sear and a bath of foaming butter, smashed
  garlic, and garden thyme spooned over the top. Garden broccoli chars hard in
  the same pan, picking up the drippings and a hit of lemon. *The dry surface is
  the secret: a steak that's already warm and bone-dry browns in a minute flat,
  so the inside never overshoots medium-rare.*

ingredients:
  - section: Steak
    items:
      - 2 New York strip steaks (about 1.5 inches thick)
      - 2 tsp kosher salt
      - 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or grapeseed)
      - 2 tbsp unsalted butter
      - 3 cloves garlic, smashed
      - 3 sprigs fresh thyme
      - 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  - section: Garlic-herb broccoli
    items:
      - 1 large head broccoli, cut into bite-size florets
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
      - "Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)"
      - 1 tsp lemon juice, plus zest of ½ lemon
      - Kosher salt and black pepper
  - section: Herb butter (optional)
    items:
      - 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
      - ½ tsp chopped thyme
      - ½ tsp chopped rosemary
      - 1 small clove garlic, grated
      - Pinch of salt
steps:
  - section: Dry-brine & slow-roast
    items:
      - Salt the steaks on all sides and rest on a wire rack at room temperature for at least 40 minutes (or salt the night before, uncovered in the fridge, for the best crust).
      - Heat the oven to 250°F. Pepper the steaks and set the rack over a baking sheet. Roast until the internal temperature reads 115°F for medium-rare, about 35–45 minutes for 1.5-inch steaks. Use a probe thermometer and start checking early — thinner steaks move fast.
      - Pull the steaks and rest 5–10 minutes while you heat the pan. The surface should look dry; pat it with a paper towel if not.
  - section: Sear & baste
    items:
      - Heat a cast-iron skillet over high until just smoking. Add the neutral oil, then lay in the steaks and sear 45–60 seconds per side until a deep crust forms.
      - Add the butter, smashed garlic, thyme, and rosemary. Tilt the pan and spoon the foaming butter over the steaks for 30 seconds a side. Pull at 125–130°F (it climbs to 130–135°F as it rests).
      - Rest the steaks 5–10 minutes, topped with a dollop of herb butter if using. Slice against the grain.
  - section: Char the broccoli
    items:
      - Toss the florets with olive oil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Heat a skillet (or use the steak pan after searing) over medium-high until smoking hot.
      - Add the broccoli cut-side down in a single layer and char undisturbed 2–3 minutes until deeply browned. Toss and cook 2 more minutes.
      - Add the sliced garlic, then a splash of water and cover for 1–2 minutes to steam the stems tender. Uncover, let the liquid cook off, and finish with lemon juice and zest. Serve alongside the sliced steak.
tips:
  - The reverse sear's whole advantage is a bone-dry, pre-warmed surface — that's why the steak browns in under a minute without graying the interior. Don't skip the rest before searing.
  - For a ¾-inch steak, pull from the oven at 110°F (around 20 minutes) and sear hard and fast, 30 seconds a side, or it will sail past medium-rare.
  - No oven? Bring the steak up gently on the stovetop over low heat with a butter baste, rest, then hard-sear — or sous-vide at 125°F for 45 minutes for edge-to-edge precision.
  - Let the broccoli sit undisturbed to char; constant stirring steams it gray instead of building those deep brown, caramelized edges.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Garden thyme and rosemary are the heart of the baste; a few sliced cremini or oyster mushrooms sautéed in the steak butter make a rich third element.
  - label: Make ahead
    note: The herb butter can be mixed and refrigerated days in advance; bring to room temperature before topping the steak.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A structured red — Cabernet Sauvignon or Malbec — and a crusty bread to mop the garlic-herb butter.

Stephen has run this technique enough times to trust it cold: the gentle oven does
the cooking, and the cast-iron does only the crust. The garden thyme and a hard
char on the broccoli turn a weeknight strip into something that eats like a
steakhouse plate.
