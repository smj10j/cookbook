---
title: Chicken & Ginger Lettuce Wraps with Sesame-Lime Drizzle
slug: chicken-ginger-lettuce-wraps
tagline: Hot gingery chicken crumbles in cold butter-lettuce cups, built at the table.
pitch: >-
  These are takeout lettuce wraps with the hoisin syrup deleted and the ginger
  turned up. Ground chicken sears in a ripping-hot skillet with a full
  tablespoon of ginger, garlic, and scallion whites until the crumbles brown at
  the edges; celery and carrot go in at the end so they stay snappy. Everything
  spoons into cold butter-lettuce cups with a sesame-lime drizzle. *Let the
  chicken sit untouched for the first two minutes — the browned crust is
  where all the flavor lives.*
serves: 2
times:
  prep: 15
  cook: 10
  total: 25
difficulty: easy
protein: chicken
methods:
  - stove
cuisine: Chinese
course: main
heat: mild
tags:
  - weeknight
  - low-carb
ingredients:
  - section: Filling
    items:
      - 8 oz ground chicken
      - 2 tsp neutral oil
      - 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 4 scallions, whites minced and greens sliced
      - 2 stalks celery, finely diced
      - 1 carrot, finely diced
  - section: Sesame-lime drizzle
    items:
      - 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
      - 1 tsp honey
      - Pinch of white pepper
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 1 head butter lettuce, leaves separated and chilled
      - 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
steps:
  - Whisk the drizzle ingredients together and set aside.
  - Heat the oil in a large skillet until shimmering. Add the chicken, press it flat, and leave it alone 2 minutes to brown.
  - Break it into crumbles; add the ginger, garlic, and scallion whites and stir-fry 1 minute until fragrant.
  - Add the celery and carrot and toss 2 minutes — they should stay bright and crunchy.
  - Off the heat, stir in half the drizzle. Serve the filling in the cold lettuce cups, topped with scallion greens, sesame seeds, and the rest of the drizzle.
tips:
  - Chill the lettuce cups in ice water and spin them dry — the cold snap against the hot filling is the dish.
  - Do not crowd the pan; a wet pile of chicken steams gray instead of browning.
  - Ground chicken thigh has far more flavor than breast — ask the butcher to grind to order if you can.
