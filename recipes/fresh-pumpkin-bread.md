  Canned pumpkin can't touch this. You roast a sugar pumpkin until it caramelizes at the
  edges, then purée it into something silkier and far more flavorful than anything from a

  whisper of cardamom — and bakes into a dark, tender crumb shot through with toasted nuts
  and a sugar-crisp top.

  between you and a perfect crumb.*

ingredients:
  - section: Pumpkin purée
    items:
      - 1 sugar or pie pumpkin (2½–3 lb)
  - section: Wet
    items:
      - 1¾ cups fresh pumpkin purée
      - 2 large eggs, room temp
      - ½ cup avocado or light olive oil
      - 1 cup granulated sugar
      - ½ cup light brown sugar
      - 1 tsp vanilla extract
  - section: Dry
    items:
      - 1½ cups all-purpose flour
      - 1 tsp baking soda
      - ½ tsp baking powder
      - ½ tsp salt
      - 1½ tsp cinnamon
      - 1 tsp nutmeg
      - ½ tsp ground ginger
      - ¼ tsp ground cloves
      - pinch of cardamom (optional)
  - section: Add-ins
    items:
      - ½ cup chopped toasted nuts (walnuts, almonds, or hazelnuts)
steps:
  - Halve and seed the pumpkin, then roast it cut-side down at 400°F for 35–45 minutes until fork-tender. Scoop the flesh from the skin and purée until smooth.
  - If the purée looks watery, drain it in a fine sieve or cheesecloth until it's thick like canned pumpkin, then measure out 1¾ cups.
  - Preheat the oven to 350°F and grease and parchment-line a 9×5-inch loaf pan.
  - Whisk the wet ingredients together — purée, eggs, oil, both sugars, and vanilla — until smooth.
  - In a separate bowl, whisk all the dry ingredients to distribute the spices evenly.
  - Fold the dry into the wet just until combined; don't overmix, or the loaf bakes up tough.
  - Fold in the toasted nuts, then scrape the batter into the prepared pan.
  - Bake 55–65 minutes until a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs (internal temperature around 205–208°F).
  - Cool in the pan 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.
tips:
  - Watery purée is the enemy of a good crumb — drain it in a sieve or cheesecloth until it mounds on a spoon like canned pumpkin.
  - Mix by hand; an electric mixer over-aerates the batter and leaves you with a gummy, sunken loaf.
  - If the top browns too fast before the center sets, tent the loaf loosely with foil for the last 15 minutes.
  - Toast the nuts at 350°F for 6–8 minutes first — it wakes up their oils and adds a deeper, roastier crunch.
extras:
  - label: Make it ahead
    note: The loaf keeps wrapped at room temp for 3 days and freezes beautifully — slice first, then thaw a piece at a time.
  - label: Roast extra
    note: One pumpkin usually yields more than 1¾ cups; freeze the surplus purée in ¼-cup portions for pancakes or oatmeal.
