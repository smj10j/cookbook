title: Grilled Shrimp & Summer Squash Skewers with Charred Lime

tagline: Snappy shrimp and squash off a hot grill, finished with smoky charred lime.
pitch: >-
  Shrimp cook in the time it takes to close the grill lid, so the trick is
  building the skewer for the shrimp's schedule — squash cut thin enough to
  char in the same three minutes, everything slicked with smoked-paprika oil.
  The limes go on the grill too, cut side down, until the sugars blacken.
  *Squeeze the charred lime over everything at the table — warm, smoky citrus
  is a sauce you didn't have to make.*
serves: 2

  prep: 15

  - grill

tags:

ingredients:
  - section: Skewers
    items:
      - 6 oz large shrimp, peeled and deveined
      - 1 small zucchini, in ¼-inch half-moons
      - 1 small yellow squash, in ¼-inch half-moons
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tsp smoked paprika
      - ¼ tsp kosher salt
      - Freshly ground black pepper
  - section: To finish
    items:
      - 2 limes, halved
      - 2 tbsp cilantro, chopped
steps:
  - Soak wooden skewers 15 minutes (or use metal). Heat the grill to high.
  - Toss the shrimp and squash with the oil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  - Thread, alternating shrimp and squash, packing the pieces snugly so they support each other.
  - Grill 2–3 minutes a side, until the shrimp turn opaque and the squash chars at the edges; set the lime halves cut side down alongside until blackened.
  - Shower with cilantro and squeeze the charred limes over everything.
tips:
  - Buy shrimp labeled "shell-on, untreated" when you can — treated shrimp carry extra sodium and a rubbery snap.
  - Shrimp are done the moment they curl into a loose C; a tight O is overcooked.
  - For Stephen's plate, thread a second skewer with swordfish or halibut chunks (or extra-firm tofu) in the same oil — same timing, same char.

    note: Thread cherry tomatoes between the shrimp, and use garden basil instead of cilantro for an Italian lean.
  - label: Make it dinner-sized

  - label: Pairs with
    note: A dry rosé or sparkling water with the spare charred lime.
