pitch: >-
  Halibut is the fish that punishes a hot pan — lean enough to turn to chalk in
  the sixty seconds you look away. Sous vide deletes the risk entirely.
  Forty minutes at 130°F and it emerges pearl-white, seamed into thick petals,
  set just past translucent. Against it goes a cold salad of smashed cucumber,
  dill, and mint in rice vinegar, and a spoon of lemon-zest olive oil instead
  of a butter sauce. *The temperature contrast — warm silk, cold crunch — is
  the whole plate.*

ingredients:
  - section: Halibut
    items:
      - 7 oz halibut fillet, cut into 2 portions
      - 1 tsp olive oil
      - Freshly ground black pepper
  - section: Cucumber-herb salad
    items:
      - 1 English cucumber
      - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
      - ¼ tsp kosher salt
      - 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
      - 1 tbsp fresh mint, torn
  - section: Lemon oil
    items:
      - 1½ tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
      - Zest of 1 lemon
      - Squeeze of lemon juice
steps:
  - Set the sous-vide bath to 130°F. Rub the halibut with the 1 tsp oil and a few grinds of pepper, seal in a bag, and cook 40 minutes.
  - Meanwhile smash the cucumber with the flat of a knife, tear into ragged bites, and toss with the vinegar and salt; chill.
  - Stir the lemon zest and juice into the olive oil and let it steep.
  - Just before serving, fold the dill and mint into the cucumbers.
  - Lift the halibut from the bag onto plates — no sear needed — spoon the lemon oil over, and pile the cold salad alongside.
tips:
  - 130°F gives a just-set, silky center; run 125°F if you like it closer to sashimi-tender.
  - Smashed (not sliced) cucumber sheds water and drinks the dressing — ragged edges are the point.
  - If you want a crust, a 30-second kiss in a screaming skillet after the bath works, but pat the fish bone-dry first.
  - The bagged fish holds at temperature an extra 20 minutes without penalty — dinner can wait for you.
