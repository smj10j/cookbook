pitch: >-
  Swordfish is the steak of the sea, and it wants to be treated like one: a hot
  pan, a golden crust, and a slick of butter to finish. These thick steaks sear
  until they're bronzed on both faces, then get basted in a foaming compound butter
  loaded with garden parsley, dill, and chives until every bite tastes of herb and
  brown butter. Garlicky roasted Brussels sprouts with a flick of lemon zest round
  it into a full plate. *Tilt the pan and spoon that herb butter over the fish for
  the last minute — that's the whole trick to a steakhouse finish.*

ingredients:
  - section: Swordfish & herb butter
    items:
      - 2 swordfish steaks (about 6 oz each)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 tbsp unsalted butter, plus more for basting
      - 2 tbsp mixed fresh herbs (parsley, dill, chives), finely chopped
      - 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
      - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
      - Lemon wedges, for serving
  - section: Garlic roasted Brussels sprouts
    items:
      - "1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved"
      - 3–4 garlic cloves, minced
      - 2–3 tbsp olive oil
      - "½ tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)"
      - Salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - Zest of 1 lemon
steps:
  - section: Roast the sprouts
    items:
      - Preheat the oven to 400°F. Toss the halved Brussels sprouts with the minced garlic, olive oil, red pepper flakes, and a generous pinch of salt; spread in a single layer on a sheet pan.
      - Roast 20–25 minutes, stirring once halfway, until crispy at the edges and tender inside. Toss with lemon zest and black pepper the moment they come out.
  - section: Make the herb butter
    items:
      - Soften 2 tbsp butter and mash in the chopped herbs and garlic with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside (it'll melt and foam in the pan when you baste).
  - section: Sear the swordfish
    items:
      - Pat the steaks dry and season both sides with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a cast-iron skillet over medium-high.
      - Lay the steaks in the hot pan and cook undisturbed 3–4 minutes per side, until a golden crust forms and the fish is just opaque through (thickness dictates the exact time).
  - section: Baste & serve
    items:
      - In the last minute, drop the herb butter into the pan. As it foams, tilt the skillet and spoon the herby brown butter over the fish again and again.
      - Plate the swordfish with the roasted Brussels sprouts, pour over any pan butter, and serve with lemon wedges.
tips:
  - Swordfish dries out fast — pull it the moment it turns opaque in the center; it should still feel springy, not firm.
  - A dry surface is everything for the crust; blot the steaks well and don't crowd the pan.
  - Build the compound butter ahead and chill it in a log; slice off coins to melt over the fish right at the table.
  - If grilling instead, oil the grates and cook over medium-high 3–4 minutes per side, then finish with a coin of the herb butter melting on top.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Use whatever's thriving — parsley, dill, chives, even a little thyme or basil — in the compound butter, and the lemon zest from a garden Meyer lemon if you've got one.
  - label: Side swaps
    note: From the same source trio, a grilled corn and bell pepper salad (lime-honey vinaigrette) or a spicy corn and Brussels sprout sauté both pair beautifully if you want something brighter than the roast.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A medium-bodied white like a lightly oaked Chardonnay or a dry rosé stands up to the meaty fish and brown butter.

Swordfish takes to a sear like a good steak, and a garden-herb compound butter
spooned over the top in the last minute turns it into something restaurant-worthy.
Garlicky roasted Brussels sprouts with lemon zest make it a complete, easy plate
for two.
