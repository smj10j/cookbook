  Warm fish, cool greens — that contrast is the whole pleasure. Salmon goes
  straight over a medium-high grill, skin-side down, and stays put until it lifts

  greens beneath it — the dressing clings, the leaves soften just so, and every
  forkful gets fish, crunch, and tang at once.*

ingredients:
  - section: Salad
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (6–7 oz each, skin on or off)
      - Kosher salt and cracked black pepper
      - Olive oil, for brushing
      - 5 oz baby spring mix
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      - ½ large cucumber, thinly sliced (peeled if thick-skinned)
      - ¼ small red onion, very thinly sliced
      - ¼–⅓ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
      - ¼ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  - section: Dijon-balsamic vinaigrette
    items:
      - 1½ tbsp Dijon mustard
      - 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
      - 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
      - 1 tsp honey or maple syrup (optional, for balance)
      - Pinch of salt and black pepper
steps:
  - section: Dressing & prep
    items:
      - Whisk the Dijon, balsamic, and a pinch of salt and pepper in a small bowl or jar; add honey if you want a softer edge. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking until emulsified. Set aside.
      - Halve the tomatoes and olives. Thinly slice the cucumber and red onion (a mandoline gets them paper-thin). Soak the onion in ice water for 10 minutes to mellow its bite, then drain. Crumble the feta.
  - section: Grill the salmon
    items:
      - Heat the grill to medium-high and oil the grates. Brush the salmon with olive oil and season generously with salt and pepper.
      - Grill skin-side down first and don't move it for about 4 minutes, until it releases cleanly. Flip and cook 3–4 minutes more, until it flakes easily with a just-pink center (125–130°F internal). Rest 2 minutes.
  - section: Assemble
    items:
      - Toss the spring mix, tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion with a little of the vinaigrette and divide between two shallow bowls or plates.
      - Set the salmon on top, whole or flaked into large chunks. Scatter the olives and feta, drizzle more vinaigrette over the fish, and serve right away while it's warm.
tips:
  - Let the salmon tell you when to flip — it sticks until the skin crisps and releases, then lifts cleanly off the grates with no tearing.
  - Soaking the sliced red onion in ice water for 10 minutes tames the raw bite and crisps it up.
  - A lidded jar emulsifies the vinaigrette better than a whisk and stores the extra; shake hard right before dressing.
  - Dress the greens lightly and save the bolder drizzle for over the warm salmon, where it pools and clings.

A dinner salad that came together from a full garden haul: cucumber and cherry
tomatoes from the bed, spring mix and red onion from the store, and a Dijon-
balsamic vinaigrette shaken in a jar. Kalamata olives and feta nudge it
Mediterranean, and the warm grilled salmon over cool greens is the contrast that
makes it dinner, not a side.
