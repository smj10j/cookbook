title: Roasted Fennel with Olives & Garlic

pitch: >-
  Roasting is fennel's best friend — the high heat mellows its anise edge into something
  deep, sweet, and almost candied. Thick wedges go cut-side down on a sheet pan with
  smashed garlic, briny olives, and a generous slick of olive oil, then roast undisturbed
  until the flat sides turn burnished and the layers go meltingly tender. The garlic
  softens to a paste and the olives crisp at the edges. It's a quietly stunning side that
  asks almost nothing of you.
  *Leave a little core on each wedge so the layers stay together, then don't move them —
  that undisturbed contact is what gives you those deep-caramelized faces.*

ingredients:
  - 2 large fennel bulbs (about 1½ lb), trimmed and cut into ¾-inch wedges through the core
  - 6 garlic cloves, smashed and peeled
  - ⅔ cup pitted Kalamata or oil-cured black olives
  - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  - ½ tsp kosher salt
  - ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  - Reserved fennel fronds and a squeeze of lemon, for serving (optional)
steps:
  - Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  - On a sheet pan, toss the fennel wedges, garlic, and olives with the olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange the fennel cut-side down in a single layer.
  - Roast for 20 minutes, then flip the wedges to the other cut side and roast 15–20 minutes more, until deeply caramelized and tender when pierced.
  - Pile onto a platter, scatter with chopped fennel fronds, and finish with a squeeze of lemon if you like.
tips:
  - Don't crowd the pan — give the wedges room so they roast and caramelize instead of steaming.
  - A pinch of crushed red pepper or fennel seed in the oil deepens the flavor; a little grated Parmesan over the top in the last 5 minutes is excellent if you're not keeping it vegan.
  - Leftovers are great chopped into a grain bowl or smashed onto toast with the soft roasted garlic.

Molly Stevens' approach to roasting fennel: thick wedges, smashed garlic, and olives on
a hot sheet pan until the cut faces caramelize and the layers go silky. A simple,
elegant side that practically cooks itself.
