title: Chicken Shawarma with Garlic Toum

pitch: >-
  Spit-roast flavor from your oven: chicken thighs marinated in cumin, coriander, paprika,
  and turmeric, roasted until the edges crisp and char, then piled with tomato, cucumber,
  and a cloud of toum — the billowy, emulsified Lebanese garlic sauce that's a genuinely
  fun technique to master. *Toum is garlic whipped into a fluffy white mayo-without-egg —
  pure pungent magic, and it goes on everything.* It's a platter or a wrap, and it's built
  on the garden herbs and chiles you already grow.

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & marinade
    items:
      - 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
      - 3 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 tbsp lemon juice
      - 4 cloves garlic, grated
      - 2 tsp ground cumin
      - 2 tsp ground coriander
      - 1 tsp paprika
      - ½ tsp turmeric
      - ½ tsp cinnamon
      - ¼ tsp cayenne
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - ½ tsp black pepper
  - section: Garlic toum
    items:
      - ¼ cup garlic cloves (about 10)
      - 1 cup neutral oil
      - 3 tbsp lemon juice
      - ½ tsp salt
      - 2 tbsp ice water
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 4 pita breads, warmed
      - 1 tomato, diced
      - 1 cup cucumber, diced
      - ½ red onion, thinly sliced
      - ¼ cup parsley
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Toss the chicken with the oil, lemon, garlic, and all the spices, salt, and pepper. Chill at least 1 hour, ideally overnight.
  - section: Make the toum
    items:
      - In a food processor, blend the garlic and salt to a paste. With the motor running, drizzle in the oil very slowly — drop by drop at first — alternating with the lemon juice and ice water, until it turns thick, white, and fluffy like mayonnaise.
  - section: Roast & build
    items:
      - Roast the chicken on a sheet pan at 425°F for 20–25 minutes, until charred at the edges (or grill it), broiling the last 2 minutes for extra char. Rest, then slice.
      - Pile the chicken into warm pita with tomato, cucumber, red onion, and parsley, and a generous smear of toum.
tips:
  - Toum lives or dies on a slow oil drizzle — go drop by drop at first. If it breaks, start over with a fresh garlic clove and dribble the broken sauce back in.
  - Use thighs, not breasts — they stay juicy through the high-heat char that makes shawarma taste like shawarma.
  - Crank the broiler for the last 2 minutes to mimic the crispy edges of a vertical spit.
  - Marinate overnight if you can; the spices need time to bloom into the meat.
extras:

    note: Parsley, mint, and a sliced garden tomato and cucumber for the platter.

  - label: Heat dial
    note: A little harissa or extra cayenne in the marinade pushes the warmth.
