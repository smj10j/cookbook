  This is the cleanest kind of weeknight dinner: a mild fillet, oil-salt-pepper, and a
  ripping-hot broiler that turns it opaque and flaky in minutes. The quiet star is the
  sauce — orange juice simmered down with shallot and tarragon until it's syrupy and

ingredients:
  - 2 mild fish fillets (sole, tilapia, or arctic char), about 4 oz each
  - 1½ cups orange juice
  - 1 shallot, minced
  - ½ tsp dried tarragon
  - 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  - ½ tsp fine sea salt
  - ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  - 6 Kalamata olives, pitted and chopped (optional)
steps:
  - Combine the orange juice, shallot, and tarragon in a small saucepan. Simmer until thickened and syrupy, 15–20 minutes. Cover and set aside.
  - Heat the broiler. Rub both sides of the fish with the olive oil and season with the salt and pepper.
  - Place the fillets on a parchment-lined baking sheet and broil 5–6 inches from the heat until opaque and just flaking, about 5 minutes per half-inch of thickness.
  - Transfer each fillet to a plate with a wide spatula, spoon the citrus sauce over the top, and scatter with chopped olives.
tips:
  - Watch the broiler like a hawk — thin fillets can go from perfect to dry in under a minute.
  - No orange juice? Reduce white wine or water with a good squeeze of lemon instead; swap dried dill for the tarragon.
  - For extra crunch and richness, finish with toasted nuts or seeds in place of (or alongside) the olives.

A no-fuss broiled fillet that earns its keep with a reduced orange-tarragon sauce.
Simmer the juice down until it's glossy, broil the fish fast, and dinner's on the
table in 25 minutes.
