ingredients:
  - section: Tuna
    items:
      - 2 everything bagel–seasoned tuna steaks, about 6 oz each
      - 1 tbsp olive oil, for brushing
      - "½ tsp black pepper"
      - "½ tsp coarse salt (optional — depends on how salty the seasoning is)"
  - section: Horseradish cream
    items:
      - "½ cup full-fat Greek yogurt"
      - 1 tbsp prepared horseradish (adjust to taste)
      - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tsp lemon juice
      - 1 tsp honey or agave
      - "½ tsp grated garlic (or a pinch of garlic powder)"
      - Salt and pepper, to taste
  - section: Charred lemon broccolini
    items:
      - 1 bunch broccolini, trimmed
      - 2 lemons, halved
      - "1½ tbsp olive oil"
      - Flaky salt
      - "Optional: red pepper flakes or a chili-oil drizzle"
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Heat the grill to medium-high (around 450°F) and oil the grates well so the seasoning crust doesn't tear.
      - Stir together the yogurt, horseradish, Dijon, lemon juice, honey, and garlic; season with salt and pepper and chill — it thickens and mellows as it rests.
      - Toss the broccolini with olive oil and salt. Brush the cut sides of the lemons with a little oil.
  - section: Grill the sides
    items:
      - Grill the broccolini 6–8 minutes, turning occasionally, until charred and tender. Add the lemons cut-side down for the last 3 minutes until caramelized.
      - Move the broccolini and lemons to a warm plate and tent loosely with foil.
  - section: Grill the tuna
    items:
      - Brush the tuna lightly with olive oil. Grill 45–60 seconds per side for rare (ruby center), or up to 90 seconds for medium-rare. Rotate halfway through each side if you want crosshatch marks.
      - Rest the steaks 2 minutes — carryover heat finishes the sear without cooking the center.
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Smear a generous spoonful of horseradish cream onto each plate and set the tuna on top or slightly offset.
      - Add the broccolini, squeeze over the charred lemon, and finish with flaky salt and an optional drizzle of chili oil.
tips:
  - The second tuna turns opaque all the way through it goes chalky — pull it while the center is still rosy and let carryover do the rest.
  - Flip with a fish spatula or tongs, gently; the everything-bagel crust wants to grab the grates.
  - Charred lemons get sweeter and more aromatic — they're for squeezing and for looks, so don't skip the grill marks.
  - Toss a small chunk of cedar or fruitwood on the coals for a minute before the tuna goes on for a whisper of smoke.

Those pre-seasoned tuna steaks are a head start to a fast, impressive dinner: the
grill toasts the crust, the center stays rare, and a cool horseradish cream plays
the role of a steakhouse sauce gone coastal. Char the lemons hard — they're half
the flavor and all of the drama.
