  Poaching is the gentlest way to cook cod, and this brothy tomato bath is built for it.
  Garlic and Aleppo pepper bloom in olive oil, then crushed canned tomatoes, white wine,
  bay, and a pinch of saffron simmer into a fragrant, faintly golden broth. The cod
  slips in and cooks at a bare simmer until it's barely opaque and falls into big silky
  flakes. Serve it in shallow bowls with the broth spooned over and plenty of bread for
  mopping.
  *Keep the liquid at a whisper of a simmer — aggressive heat is the only thing that
  can toughen cod this tender.*

ingredients:
  - 2 skinless cod fillets (about 5 oz each)
  - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  - ½ tsp Aleppo pepper (or ¼ tsp crushed red pepper flakes)
  - 1 (14.5 oz) can whole peeled tomatoes, drained
  - ¼ cup dry white wine
  - 1 bay leaf
  - 1 pinch saffron threads
  - ½ cup water
  - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
steps:
  - Heat the oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and Aleppo pepper and cook, stirring often, until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  - Add the tomatoes, crushing them with your hands as you go, along with the wine, bay leaf, saffron, and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the flavors meld, 5–7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  - Reduce the heat to medium-low. Season the cod with salt and pepper and nestle it into the broth.
  - Cover and cook at a bare simmer until the cod is opaque throughout and beginning to flake, 5–7 minutes (thicker pieces take longer).
  - Gently transfer the cod to shallow bowls and spoon the poaching broth over the top.
tips:
  - Saffron blooms best with a little warmth — crumble the threads into the broth as it simmers so the color and aroma fully release.
  - Any firm white fish works here — halibut, sea bass, or haddock all poach beautifully in this broth.
  - Serve with grilled or toasted crusty bread; the broth is the whole point and you'll want to mop up every drop.
