pitch: >-
  A love letter to the Brown Sugar Salmon Salad at Cafe Blue out in Bee Cave.
  Atlantic salmon gets a sweet-smoky crust of brown sugar and ancho chile, then
  roasts skin-side down on a soaked cedar plank until the sugars set into a
  burnished bark and the wood breathes gentle smoke up into the fish. It lands
  warm over a big cool tangle of mixed greens, shredded red cabbage and carrot,
  cucumber, and cherry tomatoes, finished with fresh orange segments, toasted
  almonds, and crumbled goat cheese. A bright raspberry vinaigrette ties the
  sweet crust to the tart-creamy toppings. *The cedar plank is the secret — it
  perfumes the salmon with campfire smoke while shielding it from direct flame,
  so the crust caramelizes without ever scorching.*

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon & brown sugar-ancho crust
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each), skin-on
      - 2 tbsp brown sugar, packed
      - 2 tsp ancho chile powder
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - ½ tsp smoked paprika
      - ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 cedar grilling plank, soaked 1–2 hours
  - section: Salad
    items:
      - 5 cups mixed greens
      - 1 cup shredded red cabbage
      - ½ cup shredded carrots
      - 1 cup cucumber, sliced
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      - 1 orange, segmented
      - "⅓ cup sliced almonds, toasted"
      - ¼ cup crumbled goat cheese
  - section: Raspberry vinaigrette
    items:
      - ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
      - 2 tbsp raspberry preserves
      - 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
      - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tsp honey
      - Pinch of salt and pepper
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Submerge the cedar plank in water and weight it down so it stays under; soak at least 1 hour (2 is better). This keeps it from catching fire and lets it steam-smoke the fish.
      - Toast the sliced almonds in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden and fragrant, about 3–4 minutes; set aside. Cut the orange into segments over a bowl to catch the juice.
      - Mix the brown sugar, ancho chile powder, salt, smoked paprika, and pepper. Pat the salmon dry, rub lightly with olive oil, and press the crust evenly onto the flesh side.
  - section: Cedar plank grill
    items:
      - Heat the grill to medium (~375–400°F). Set the soaked plank on the grate and close the lid for 3–4 minutes until it crackles and just starts to smoke.
      - Lay the salmon skin-side down on the hot plank, close the lid, and grill 12–15 minutes without flipping, until the crust is glossy and the thickest part reads 125–130°F.
      - Let the salmon rest on the plank a couple of minutes; carryover heat finishes it to a silky medium.
  - section: Salad & plate
    items:
      - Shake or whisk the vinaigrette (add a splash of the reserved orange juice) until emulsified.
      - Toss the greens, red cabbage, carrot, cucumber, and tomatoes with just enough vinaigrette to coat, then divide between two plates.
      - Scatter the orange segments, toasted almonds, and goat cheese over the greens. Lay a salmon fillet on each and drizzle with a little more vinaigrette.
tips:
  - Ancho chile is smoky and mild, not hot — for real heat, swap in chipotle powder or add a pinch of cayenne to the crust.
  - No grill or plank? Roast the crusted salmon at 400°F on parchment for 12–14 minutes; you lose the smoke, so lean on the smoked paprika (or a single drop of liquid smoke in the crust).
  - Cook to 125–130°F at the thickest point and let it rest — salmon climbs a few degrees off the heat and stays moist.
  - No raspberry preserves? Mash ¼ cup fresh or thawed raspberries into the dressing and strain out the seeds for the same tart-sweet result.

Stephen and Lauren's homage to a Cafe Blue favorite out on Lake Travis: the
sweet-smoky ancho-and-brown-sugar salmon is the star, but the salad around it —
oranges, goat cheese, toasted almonds, and a raspberry vinaigrette — is what
makes it feel like a night out. The cedar plank does the heavy lifting on flavor.
