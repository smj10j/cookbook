title: Steak au Poivre with Cognac-Peppercorn Cream
slug: steak-au-poivre
tagline: A peppercorn-crusted steak seared in cast iron, finished with a silky cognac-cream pan sauce.
pitch: >-
  This is the French-bistro classic worth learning for the sauce alone: a steak pressed
  in coarsely cracked black pepper, seared hard in cast iron, then set aside while you
  build a pan sauce on its fond. Cognac goes in to deglaze — flamed or simmered off —
  and reduces with broth and cream into something glossy and peppery. *Igniting the
  cognac burns off its raw edge and leaves a deep, toasty backbone in the sauce — a
  thirty-second piece of theater.* It eats like a restaurant and teaches you a proper
  pan sauce in one go.

ingredients:
  - section: Steak
    items:
      - 2 filet mignon or New York strip steaks (about 6 oz each)
      - 2 tbsp black peppercorns, coarsely cracked
      - Kosher salt
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 tbsp butter
  - section: Cognac-peppercorn sauce
    items:
      - 1 shallot, minced
      - ⅓ cup cognac (or brandy)
      - ½ cup beef broth
      - ½ cup heavy cream
      - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tbsp butter
      - Salt to taste
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Pat the steaks very dry and season all over with salt. Crack the peppercorns coarsely (rock a heavy pan over them on the board) and press them onto both flat sides of each steak.
  - section: Sear
    items:
      - Heat the oil and 1 tbsp butter in a cast-iron skillet over medium-high. Sear the steaks 3–4 minutes per side, to 125°F for medium-rare. Move them to a plate to rest.
  - section: Sauce
    items:
      - Lower the heat to medium and cook the shallot in the pan drippings 1 minute. Pull the pan off the heat, add the cognac, then return it — and either flame it off with a long lighter or simmer 1 minute to cook off the alcohol — scraping up the fond.
      - Add the beef broth and reduce by half. Stir in the cream and Dijon and simmer until the sauce coats a spoon, 2–3 minutes. Off the heat, swirl in the butter and season with salt.
      - Return the steaks and any resting juices to the pan, spoon the sauce over, and serve.
tips:
  - Crack the peppercorns coarse, not fine — you want fragrant, crunchy cracks, not a dusty burn.
  - "Flambé safely: pull the pan off the heat before the cognac goes in, then ignite with a long lighter (or just simmer it off) — keep your face and the hood clear."
  - Pull the steaks at 125°F; they climb to a perfect medium-rare while the sauce comes together.
  - No cognac? Brandy or bourbon works, and a broth-and-cream sauce with no spirit is still excellent.

  - label: Make it richer
    note: A spoonful of brined green peppercorns stirred into the sauce doubles the pepper character.
