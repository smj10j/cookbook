title: Chicken Piccata with Thin Spaghetti & Lemon-Caper Pan Sauce
slug: chicken-piccata
tagline: Golden chicken cutlets in a buttery lemon-caper sauce, tangled with thin spaghetti.
pitch: >-
  This is the piccata that earns its butter. Thin chicken cutlets get a quick
  flour dredge and a hard sear until the edges go lacquer-brown, then the pan
  becomes the whole show: white wine deglazed against the fond, lemon, briny
  capers, and cold butter swirled in off the heat until the sauce turns glossy
  and clings. Thin spaghetti drinks it all up, and crispy fried capers scattered
  on top give you little salty pops of crunch.
  *The trick is to swirl the butter into the sauce off the heat — that's how you
  get a silky emulsion instead of a greasy puddle.*

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken
    items:
      - 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts, sliced horizontally into 4 thin cutlets
      - ½ cup all-purpose flour (whole wheat works too)
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tbsp butter
  - section: Lemon-caper pan sauce
    items:
      - 2 garlic cloves, minced
      - Pinch of red chili flakes (optional)
      - ¼ cup dry white wine
      - ½ cup low-sodium chicken broth
      - Juice of 1 lemon, plus zest of ½ lemon
      - 2 tbsp capers, drained (plus 1 tbsp extra for crisping)
      - 3 tbsp cold butter, cubed
  - section: Pasta & finish
    items:
      - 4 oz thin spaghetti
      - 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped (from the garden)
      - Lemon slices, for garnish
      - Toasted pine nuts (optional)
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Pound the cutlets gently to an even ¼-inch thickness. Season the flour with salt and pepper, then dredge the cutlets and shake off the excess.
      - Bring a pot of salted water to a boil for the spaghetti.
  - section: Crispy capers & chicken
    items:
      - Heat the olive oil and 1 tbsp butter in a large skillet over medium-high. Add the extra 1 tbsp capers and fry until they burst open and crisp, about 1 minute; lift out and drain on a paper towel.
      - Add the chicken to the same pan and cook 3 minutes per side until deep golden and cooked through. Remove and tent loosely with foil.
  - section: Pan sauce
    items:
      - Lower the heat to medium. Add the garlic and chili flakes and sauté 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in the white wine and let it reduce by half, scraping up the browned bits.
      - Add the broth, lemon juice, lemon zest, and the 2 tbsp capers. Simmer 3–4 minutes until reduced slightly.
      - Pull the pan off the heat and swirl in the cold butter a few cubes at a time until the sauce is glossy and emulsified. Taste and adjust salt and lemon.
  - section: Combine & serve
    items:
      - Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti until just al dente, reserving a splash of pasta water. Drain and toss the pasta directly into the sauce, loosening with pasta water if needed.
      - Return the chicken to the pan, spooning sauce over the top to rewarm for a minute.
      - Plate the spaghetti, lay the cutlets on top, and finish with parsley, crispy capers, lemon slices, and pine nuts if using.
tips:
  - Pounding the cutlets to an even thickness is the whole game — they cook in minutes and stay juicy instead of drying out at the thin ends.
  - Swirl the butter in off the heat; too much direct heat breaks the emulsion and you lose the glossy, restaurant-style finish.
  - A heaping spoonful of reserved pasta water helps the sauce coat the spaghetti instead of sliding off it.
  - Fry the capers first in the chicken oil — they turn into salty little crisps that are the best garnish on the plate.
extras:

  - label: Pairs with
