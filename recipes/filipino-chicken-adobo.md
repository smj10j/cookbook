title: Filipino Chicken Adobo
slug: filipino-chicken-adobo
tagline: Chicken thighs braised in soy, vinegar, garlic, and bay until glossy and falling apart.
pitch: >-
  The easiest deeply-savory braise you'll ever make, and a whole new cuisine in one pot.
  Chicken thighs simmer in soy, cane vinegar, a smashed head of garlic, bay, and black
  pepper until they're glossy and tender, the sauce reducing to a tangy-salty-sweet glaze
  you spoon over rice. *Vinegar and soy is the whole secret — it tastes like it took all
  day and asks for five minutes of work.* One pot, big leftovers, and it's even better the
  next night.

ingredients:
  - section: Braise
    items:
      - 6 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (about 2 lb)
      - ½ cup soy sauce
      - ⅓ cup cane vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
      - 8 cloves garlic, smashed
      - 6 bay leaves
      - 1 tbsp black peppercorns
      - 1 tbsp brown sugar
      - 1 cup water
  - section: To finish & serve
    items:
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - 2 scallions, sliced
      - Steamed jasmine rice
steps:
  - section: Braise
    items:
      - Combine the chicken, soy, vinegar, garlic, bay, peppercorns, brown sugar, and water in a Dutch oven. If you have time, let it marinate 30 minutes.
      - Bring to a boil, then lower to a simmer, cover, and cook 30 minutes. Uncover and simmer 10–15 minutes more, until the sauce reduces and the chicken is tender.
  - section: Crisp & serve
    items:
      - For crispy edges, lift out the thighs and sear them skin-side down in the oil in a hot pan until browned, then reduce the sauce until glossy and return the chicken to coat.
      - Serve over jasmine rice with sliced scallions and plenty of the sauce.
tips:
  - Don't stir while the vinegar first cooks — let it boil a couple of minutes so it mellows from sharp to savory before you stir.
  - For crispy skin, pull the thighs after braising and crisp them in a hot pan, then reduce the sauce to a glaze.
  - It genuinely improves overnight as the garlic and soy deepen — a great make-ahead.
  - A whole smashed head of garlic is correct here; it melts right into the sauce.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: A shower of garden scallion to finish, and a fresh bay leaf or two if you grow it.
  - label: Make it a meal
    note: Steamed jasmine rice is non-negotiable for soaking up the salty-sour glaze.
  - label: Swap
    note: Works just as well with drumsticks or a mix of bone-in chicken pieces.

The unofficial national dish of the Philippines — and the rare braise that's genuinely
hard to mess up. Soy, vinegar, garlic, bay: four things that turn into something far
greater than their sum.
