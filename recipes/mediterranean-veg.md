title: Mediterranean Stuffed Peppers with Quinoa, Chickpeas & Feta
slug: mediterranean-veg
tagline: Sweet roasted bell peppers packed with herbed quinoa, chickpeas, and melty feta.
pitch: >-
  Bell peppers turn silky and sweet in the oven, the perfect vessel for a filling
  of nutty quinoa, creamy chickpeas, and salty feta laced with oregano and lemon.
  The trick is to pre-roast the empty pepper halves so they soften and concentrate
  before they're stuffed — no raw, squeaky pepper, no watery base. The filling is
  built to stand on its own, brightened with lemon and fresh herbs so it tastes
  like more than the sum of its pantry parts. A final blast under the heat melts
  the feta into golden pockets across the top. *Toasting the quinoa dry in the pan
  before it simmers is the quiet upgrade — it deepens everything into something
  warm and nutty rather than flat.*

ingredients:
  - section: Peppers
    items:
      - 2 large bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange), halved and seeded
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper
  - section: Filling
    items:
      - ½ cup quinoa, rinsed
      - 1 cup vegetable broth (or water)
      - 1 cup canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
      - 1 small shallot or ¼ red onion, finely diced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, quartered
      - 2 tbsp Kalamata olives, chopped
      - 1 tsp dried oregano
      - ½ tsp ground cumin
      - Juice and zest of ½ lemon
      - ½ cup crumbled feta, divided
      - 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (plus more to finish)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - section: To finish
    items:
      - Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
      - Lemon wedges, for serving
steps:
  - section: Roast the peppers
    items:
      - Preheat the oven to 400°F. Rub the pepper halves inside and out with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
      - Set them cut-side up in a baking dish and roast 15 minutes until they begin to soften and slump. Leave the oven on.
  - section: Make the filling
    items:
      - Toast the rinsed quinoa in a dry saucepan over medium heat for 2–3 minutes until it smells nutty. Add the broth, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer 12–15 minutes until the liquid is absorbed. Fluff with a fork.
      - In a skillet, warm 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat and soften the shallot 2–3 minutes. Add the garlic, oregano, and cumin and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
      - Stir in the chickpeas, tomatoes, and olives and cook 3–4 minutes until the tomatoes start to break down.
      - Fold in the cooked quinoa, lemon juice and zest, parsley, and half the feta. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  - section: Stuff & bake
    items:
      - Spoon the filling generously into the roasted pepper halves and scatter the remaining feta over the top.
      - Return to the oven and bake 18–22 minutes, until the peppers are tender and the feta is golden in spots.
      - Finish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges.
tips:
  - Pre-roasting the empty peppers before stuffing is the difference between sweet and tender versus raw and watery — don't skip it.
  - Rinse the quinoa even if the package says pre-rinsed; it washes off the bitter saponin coating that can make the filling taste soapy.
  - These reheat beautifully, so make the full batch — the filling tastes even better the next day once the flavors settle.
  - For golden, bubbling tops, finish under the broiler for the last 2 minutes; watch closely so the feta doesn't scorch.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Use garden cherry tomatoes and a handful of chopped fresh mint or basil in the filling; a little fresh oregano in place of dried sings here.
  - label: Make it vegan
    note: Swap the feta for a few spoonfuls of dairy-free feta or a dollop of seasoned cashew cream stirred through the warm filling.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A simple cucumber-yogurt sauce or tzatziki on the side, and a glass of dry rosé or a light red like Grenache.
