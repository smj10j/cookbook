  The creamy Indian classic, and the dish that finally puts Indian on your menu.
  Yogurt-marinated chicken gets a hard char, then simmers into a velvety tomato-cream-butter

  perfect creamy counterpoint to all your bright, acidic dishes.

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & marinade
    items:
      - 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
      - ½ cup greek yogurt
      - 1 tbsp lemon juice
      - 2 cloves garlic, grated
      - 1 tbsp grated ginger
      - 1 tsp garam masala
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 tsp paprika
      - ½ tsp turmeric
      - ½ tsp cayenne
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
  - section: Sauce
    items:
      - 3 tbsp butter
      - 1 onion, diced
      - 4 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tbsp grated ginger
      - 1 tbsp garam masala
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 tsp paprika
      - ½ tsp turmeric
      - ½ tsp cayenne
      - 1 (14 oz) can crushed tomatoes
      - ½ cup heavy cream
      - 1 tsp granulated sugar
      - 1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves
      - Salt to taste
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - ¼ cup cilantro
      - Basmati rice or naan
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Toss the chicken with the yogurt, lemon, garlic, ginger, and the marinade spices and salt. Marinate at least 30 minutes, or overnight.
  - section: Sear
    items:
      - Melt 1 tbsp of the butter in a skillet over medium-high. Sear the marinated chicken in batches until browned but not fully cooked through, then set aside.
  - section: Sauce
    items:
      - Melt the remaining 2 tbsp butter and cook the onion until soft, 5 minutes. Add the garlic, ginger, and the sauce spices and cook 1 minute. Add the crushed tomatoes and simmer 10 minutes, then blend smooth with an immersion blender.
      - Return the chicken and simmer 8–10 minutes, until cooked through. Stir in the cream, sugar, and fenugreek (crushed between your palms) and simmer 2 minutes. Season with salt.
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Finish with cilantro and serve over basmati rice or with warm naan.
tips:
  - Blend the tomato base smooth before the cream goes in — that velvety, lump-free sauce is what separates restaurant makhani from a chunky curry.
  - Char the marinated chicken hard before it simmers; those browned edges are flavor you can't get by poaching it in the sauce.
  - Crush the dried fenugreek leaves between your palms as you add them — it releases that distinctive restaurant aroma.
  - A pinch of sugar balances the tomato's acidity; taste and adjust at the very end.
