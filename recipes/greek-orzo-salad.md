title: Greek Orzo Salad with Arugula, Tomato & Red Onion

pitch: >-
  A Greek salad given backbone — tender orzo folded into the crisp, fresh tangle
  of tomato, red onion, and peppery arugula instead of letting everything wilt in
  the dressing. The pasta is cooked in well-salted water, then cooled so it stays
  springy and drinks up just enough vinaigrette without going gluey. Everything

  distinct flavor and crunch. *The move is a 10-minute ice-water soak for the
  sliced red onion — it trades raw bite for a sweet, crisp snap that makes the
  whole bowl read fresh.*

ingredients:
  - section: Salad
    items:
      - 6 oz orzo (orecchiette also works beautifully)
      - 2 cups cherry or grape tomatoes, halved
      - 3 cups arugula, loosely packed
      - ½ red onion, thinly sliced
      - ½ English cucumber, diced
      - ⅓ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
      - ⅓ cup crumbled feta
  - section: Red-wine vinaigrette
    items:
      - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
      - 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
      - 1 clove garlic, finely minced or grated
      - 1 tsp dried oregano
      - ½ tsp Dijon mustard (optional, for emulsion)
      - Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Bring a pot of well-salted water to a boil and cook the orzo to al dente. Drain, rinse briefly under cool water to stop the cooking, and toss with a drizzle of olive oil so it doesn't clump.
      - Slice the red onion thin and soak it in a bowl of ice water for 10 minutes to mellow its bite, then drain and pat dry.
      - Whisk the vinaigrette ingredients together (or shake them in a jar) until emulsified. Taste and balance the salt and acid.
  - section: Assemble
    items:
      - In a large bowl, combine the cooled orzo, tomatoes, cucumber, olives, and drained red onion.
      - Just before serving, add the arugula and pour over the vinaigrette. Toss gently so the greens stay crisp and everything is lightly coated.
      - Scatter the feta over the top, finish with cracked pepper, and serve at once.
tips:
  - Dress this Greek-salad style — at the last minute — so the arugula stays perky and each ingredient keeps its own flavor. Dressing early softens everything into a marinated salad (still good, just different).
  - Salt the pasta water generously; orzo seasons from the inside as it cooks, and bland pasta is the easiest way to flatten a cold salad.
  - Hold a little extra vinaigrette back — chilled pasta soaks up dressing as it sits, so refresh leftovers with a splash before serving.
  - For a heartier bowl, add a can of drained chickpeas; they make it a full vegetarian meal.
