  The secret to kale chips that crackle instead of wilt is restraint — dry
  leaves, the lightest film of oil, and a low oven that dehydrates rather than
  steams. Torn into bite-size pieces and spread in a single uncrowded layer,
  the kale shrinks and curls until the edges go translucent and shatter at a

  over-oiled chip steams limp instead of crisping.* A pinch of smoked paprika

ingredients:
  - 1 bunch kale (curly or lacinato)
  - 1–2 tbsp olive oil
  - ½ tsp kosher salt, or to taste
  - "Optional seasonings: garlic powder, smoked paprika, or chili flakes"
steps:
  - Heat the oven to 300°F and line two baking sheets with parchment.
  - Wash the kale and dry it thoroughly — a salad spinner plus a few minutes on a towel. Any clinging water steams the leaves instead of crisping them.
  - Strip the leaves from the tough stems and tear them into bite-size pieces.
  - Toss the kale with just enough olive oil to lightly coat — the leaves should look barely glossy, not slick. Massage it in with your hands.
  - Spread in a single, uncrowded layer across both sheets. Sprinkle with salt and any optional seasonings.
  - Bake 20–25 minutes, rotating the pans and flipping the leaves halfway, until the edges are crisp but not browned.
  - Cool a few minutes on the sheet; they firm up as they cool. Eat the same day for peak crunch.
tips:
  - Less oil is more — over-oiled chips go limp and greasy. A teaspoon per packed handful is plenty.
  - Season after baking if using fine salt or grated cheese-style toppings so they don't slide off the wet leaves.
  - If a few chips still feel bendy, return them to the warm (turned-off) oven for 5 minutes to finish drying.
  - Store fully cooled chips with a paper towel in an airtight container; they keep their crunch for a day or two.
extras:
