title: Greek Salmon Salad with Lemon-Oregano Dressing

pitch: >-
  A classic Greek salad with the volume turned up: oregano-rubbed salmon, seared
  golden in a hot pan, then flaked warm over cucumber, tomato, Kalamata olives,
  and a generous rubble of feta. The dressing is the soul of it — good olive oil,
  plenty of lemon, and dried oregano whisked until it clings to every leaf.
  *Flake the salmon on while it's still warm so it gently wilts the greens and the
  feta turns just creamy at the edges — that warm-meets-cool moment is what makes
  it dinner.* Dried oregano carries the dish, so use the freshest jar you've got.

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (6–7 oz each, skin on or off)
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - ½ tsp dried oregano
      - Olive oil, for searing
  - section: Salad
    items:
      - 5 oz mixed greens (romaine, spinach, or arugula)
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      - ½ large cucumber, sliced
      - ¼ small red onion, thinly sliced
      - ⅓ cup Kalamata olives, pitted
      - ⅓ cup feta cheese, crumbled
      - Fresh dill, for garnish (optional)
  - section: Lemon-oregano dressing
    items:
      - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
      - 1½ tbsp fresh lemon juice
      - ½ tsp dried oregano
      - Pinch of salt and black pepper
steps:
  - section: Dressing & prep
    items:
      - Whisk the olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper in a small bowl or jar until emulsified. Set aside.
      - Halve the tomatoes, slice the cucumber and red onion, and pit the olives. Crumble the feta.
  - section: Sear the salmon
    items:
      - Pat the fillets very dry. Season both sides with salt, pepper, and the dried oregano.
      - Heat 1–2 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high until it shimmers. Sear the salmon presentation-side down (skin-side up if skin-on) and don't move it for 3–4 minutes, until a golden crust forms.
      - Flip gently and cook 3–4 minutes more, until it flakes easily (125–130°F internal). Rest 2 minutes, then break into large flakes.
  - section: Assemble
    items:
      - In a large bowl, toss the greens, tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion with most of the dressing.
      - Divide between two plates and add the olives and feta. Top with the warm salmon flakes, drizzle the remaining dressing over the top, and garnish with dill.
tips:
  - Dried oregano, not fresh, is the authentic Greek-salad move — it's more concentrated and perfumes the dressing better. Crush it between your fingers as you add it to wake up the oils.
  - Pat the salmon bone-dry before it hits the pan; surface moisture is what stops a golden crust from forming.
  - Press the fillet lightly with a spatula for the first 20 seconds in the pan to keep it from curling and ensure even contact.
  - Dress the greens just before serving so they stay crisp — limp lettuce is the one way to lose this salad.

The Greek-salad-meets-salmon version: a classic horiatiki given backbone with warm,
oregano-rubbed fish flaked on top. Pan-searing keeps it weeknight-fast and crisps
the fillet, while the lemon-oregano dressing and good feta keep it firmly,
unapologetically Greek.
