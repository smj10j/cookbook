pitch: >-
  Lighter than the beer-battered Baja version but every bit as good: firm white fish takes
  a quick chili-lime marinade and a fast char on the grill, then folds into warm tortillas

  taco with none of the fryer — the char does the heavy lifting.* It plugs straight into
  the salsas already in this book and comes together in under half an hour.

ingredients:
  - section: Fish & marinade
    items:
      - 1 lb cod or halibut fillets
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 tsp chili powder
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 clove garlic, grated
      - ½ tsp kosher salt
  - section: Quick slaw
    items:
      - 2 cups shredded cabbage
      - 2 tbsp cilantro, chopped
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - Pinch of salt
  - section: Lime crema
    items:
      - ⅓ cup sour cream
      - 1 tbsp mayonnaise
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - Pinch of salt
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 6 corn tortillas, warmed
      - 1 avocado, sliced
      - ¼ cup cotija, crumbled
      - Lime wedges, cilantro, and hot sauce
steps:
  - section: Marinate & mix
    items:
      - Whisk the oil, lime juice, chili powder, cumin, garlic, and salt. Coat the fish and marinate 15 minutes.
      - Toss the cabbage with cilantro, lime juice, oil, and salt. Stir together the crema ingredients.
  - section: Grill
    items:
      - Heat the grill (or a grill pan) to medium-high and oil the grates. Grill the fish 3–4 minutes per side, until charred and just flaking, then break into big chunks.
  - section: Build
    items:
      - Warm the tortillas. Layer slaw, fish, and avocado, drizzle with lime crema, and finish with cotija, cilantro, lime, and a shake of hot sauce.
tips:
  - A fish basket or well-oiled grates keep delicate fillets from sticking; if in doubt, sear them in cast iron instead.
  - Don't over-marinate — 15 minutes is plenty, or the lime starts to "cook" the fish before it hits the grill.
  - Double-stack (or char) the tortillas so they don't fall apart under the juicy fish.
  - Want it crispy? Dredge the fish in seasoned flour and pan-fry instead of grilling for the battered Baja version.
extras:

  - label: Make it a bowl
    note: Skip the tortillas and pile everything over rice or greens for a taco bowl.
