pitch: >-
  Skip the marinade — a salt-and-spice dry rub is the make-ahead move here. Coat the
  fillets in coriander, fennel, and smoked paprika hours before dinner and the salt
  does double duty: it seasons deep and dry-brines the flesh firm, drawing the surface
  to a tacky pellicle that roasts into a genuine crust. At dinner it's a single sheet
  pan — asparagus blisters in a hot oven while the salmon slides in for the last ten
  minutes, finished under the broiler until the spice crust crackles. *The trick a
  marinade can't touch: six hours uncovered in the fridge makes the salmon better, not
  mushier.*

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon & spice rub
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each), skin-on
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - 2 tsp brown sugar
      - 1½ tsp smoked paprika
      - 1 tsp ground coriander
      - ¾ tsp ground fennel
      - ½ tsp garlic powder
      - ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
      - ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
  - section: Asparagus
    items:
      - 1 bunch asparagus (about 1 lb), woody ends trimmed
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - ½ tsp kosher salt
      - Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
      - Lemon wedges, for serving
steps:
  - section: Dry-brine (up to 6 hours ahead)
    items:
      - Pat the salmon completely dry. Mix the salt, brown sugar, smoked paprika, coriander, fennel, garlic powder, black pepper, and cayenne into a rub.
      - Coat the fillets all over with the rub, pressing it into the flesh. Set them skin-side down, uncovered, on a plate in the fridge for 30 minutes and up to 6 hours — the surface will dry to a tacky sheen (a pellicle) that crusts beautifully.
  - section: Roast
    items:
      - About 30 minutes before dinner, heat the oven to 425°F. Trim and toss the asparagus with 1 tbsp olive oil, ½ tsp salt, and pepper, then spread it on a sheet pan and roast 8 minutes.
      - Meanwhile, drizzle the brined fillets with 1 tbsp olive oil. Push the asparagus to the edges, lay the salmon skin-side down in the center, and roast 8–10 minutes until the fish reads 125°F at the thickest point.
      - Switch to broil and give it 1–2 minutes to blister the asparagus tips and crackle the spice crust. Watch closely — the brown sugar can catch.
  - section: Serve
    items:
      - Rest the salmon 3–4 minutes (carryover finishes it to a silky 130°F). Pile the charred asparagus alongside and serve with lemon wedges to squeeze over both.
tips:
  - Even a 30–45 minute dry-brine helps if you're short on time, but 4–6 hours uncovered is where the crust and firm texture really pay off — it's a hands-off window, about 15 active minutes at each end.
  - Pull the salmon at 125°F; carryover heat finishes it to 130°F while it rests, so it stays moist under the crust.
  - Match the asparagus to the oven — pencil-thin spears may be done before the broiler step, so add them later; fat spears can go in with the salmon from the start.
  - No pre-ground fennel or coriander? Toast the whole seeds in a dry pan for 30 seconds and crush them in a mortar for a fresher, more aromatic crust.
extras:
  - label: Make it a plate
    note: A scoop of herbed rice, a smear of Greek-yogurt tzatziki, or a lemony white-bean mash rounds it into a full dinner without undoing the make-ahead ease.
  - label: From the garden
    note: Shower the finished fish with chopped garden dill or a little thyme, and a grate of lemon zest. A pinch of chopped serrano in the rub nudges the heat up.
  - label: Swap the spice lane

Option A from a salmon-and-asparagus brainstorm: the recipe built *around* the
make-ahead constraint rather than fighting it. A dry rub applied in the afternoon
seasons and dry-brines the fish so it roasts up firm and crusty at dinner — everything
a liquid marinade can't do when it sits for six hours.
