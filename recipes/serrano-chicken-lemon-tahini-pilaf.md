title: Serrano-Spiced Chicken with Lemon-Tahini & Date-Almond Pilaf

pitch: >-
  This plate plays sweet, savory, nutty, and bright all at once. Chicken
  breasts get a paprika-garlic-serrano rub that perfumes the meat as it sears,
  laid over a fragrant rice pilaf studded with chopped dates and toasted almonds
  for little hits of caramel and crunch. Garden peppers run through the whole
  dish — minced serrano in the rub, sweet banana pepper and jalapeño in the
  pilaf base, blistered bell pepper strips on top. A cool, lemony tahini drizzle
  ties it together and cuts the richness.
  *The dates are the secret — their caramel sweetness against the savory rice
  and the creamy tahini is what makes this taste like a restaurant dish.*

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & rub
    items:
      - 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness
      - 1 tsp paprika
      - 1 serrano, seeded and minced (from the garden)
      - 1 garlic clove, minced
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - Kosher salt
  - section: Date-almond pilaf
    items:
      - 1 cup sushi or short-grain rice, rinsed
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 garlic clove, minced
      - ½ banana pepper, diced (from the garden)
      - ½ jalapeño, diced (from the garden)
      - 2½ cups hot vegetable stock
      - 4–6 dates, pitted and chopped
      - ¼ cup almonds, toasted and chopped
      - Zest of ½ lemon
      - 2 tbsp parsley, chopped
  - section: Roasted peppers & finish
    items:
      - 1 bell pepper, sliced into strips
      - 1 banana pepper, sliced into strips
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 3–4 tbsp lemon-tahini sauce (or tahini thinned with lemon juice)
      - ½ cucumber, diced
      - Lemon wedges
steps:
  - section: Prep & rub
    items:
      - Heat the oven to 425°F. Pound the chicken to an even thickness, then rub all over with paprika, salt, minced garlic, minced serrano, and olive oil. Let it sit at room temperature while you start the rice.
  - section: Start the pilaf
    items:
      - In a saucepan, heat 1 tbsp olive oil and sauté the garlic with the diced banana pepper and jalapeño until soft, about 2 minutes.
      - Add the rinsed rice and stir to coat, then pour in the hot stock. Cover and cook on very low until tender, about 18 minutes.
  - section: Roast the peppers
    items:
      - Toss the bell pepper and banana pepper strips with olive oil and salt on a sheet pan. Roast at 425°F until blistered and tender, about 10 minutes.
  - section: Sear the chicken
    items:
      - Heat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high. Sear the chicken 5–6 minutes per side until golden and 160°F internal; rest 5 minutes (carryover brings it to 165°F).
  - section: Finish & plate
    items:
      - Fluff the rice and fold in the chopped dates, toasted almonds, lemon zest, and parsley. Taste and season.
      - Mound the pilaf, slice the chicken and fan it on top, then drizzle generously with lemon-tahini. Garnish with roasted peppers, diced cucumber, and a lemon wedge.
tips:
  - Mince the serrano fine and work it into the rub — it perfumes the meat from the inside as it sears without turning the dish into a chile challenge.
  - Pull the breasts at 160°F and rest them; carryover finishes them at 165°F so they stay juicy instead of drying out.
  - Toast the almonds in a dry pan until they smell nutty before chopping — raw almonds taste flat against the sweet dates.
  - If your tahini sauce is thick, loosen it with lemon juice and a splash of water until it drizzles in a ribbon.
