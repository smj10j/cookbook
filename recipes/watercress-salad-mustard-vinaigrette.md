title: Watercress Salad with Mustard Vinaigrette
slug: watercress-salad-mustard-vinaigrette
tagline: Peppery watercress in a sharp Dijon–red wine vinaigrette, ready in minutes.
pitch: >-
  Sometimes the best side is the simplest. Peppery watercress needs almost nothing — just
  a punchy vinaigrette that leans hard on good Dijon, a splash of red wine vinegar, and a
  whisper of grated garlic, all emulsified with fruity olive oil. Toss it at the very
  last second so the leaves stay lively and lifted, never wilted. It's the crisp, bracing
  counterpoint to anything rich coming off the grill or out of the oven.
  *Dress it the moment before serving — watercress is delicate and turns from peppery to
  limp fast.*

ingredients:
  - 2 small bunches watercress, tough stems trimmed (about 10 cups)
  - 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  - 1 tbsp best-quality red wine vinegar
  - ¼ cup olive oil
  - ½ small garlic clove, finely grated
  - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
steps:
  - In a large bowl, whisk together the Dijon, red wine vinegar, grated garlic, and a pinch each of salt and pepper.
  - Whisking constantly, stream in the olive oil until the dressing is emulsified and glossy. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and vinegar.
  - Add the watercress and toss gently to coat. Serve immediately.
tips:
  - Dress only what you'll eat — undressed watercress keeps in the fridge, but once dressed it wilts within minutes.
  - For a milder bite, use half watercress and half butter lettuce or baby greens.
  - The vinaigrette doubles as a great sauce for grilled fish or steak, so make extra.

A two-ingredient idea done right: bracing watercress and a sharp Dijon–red wine
vinaigrette. Whisk, toss at the last second, and serve. The perfect crisp foil to
anything rich.
