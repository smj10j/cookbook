title: Thai Grilled Beef "Crying Tiger" Salad (Nam Tok)
slug: thai-crying-tiger-beef-salad
tagline: Charred grilled beef sliced thin over herbs in a fierce lime, fish sauce, and toasted-rice dressing.
pitch: >-
  This is the Thai grilled-beef salad that hits four things at once — smoke, herbs,
  sour-salty heat, and a nutty grip you can't quite place. A marinated steak chars hard
  over the grill, rests, and gets sliced thin, then tossed warm with mint, cilantro,
  shallot, and a dressing of lime, fish sauce, and chili. *The secret is khao khua —
  raw rice toasted dark and ground to a powder — which gives the dressing a nutty texture
  that clings to every slice.* It's bright, fiery, and built around the grill you already
  love.

ingredients:
  - section: Beef & marinade
    items:
      - 12 oz flank, skirt, or sirloin steak
      - 1 tbsp fish sauce
      - 1 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 tsp brown sugar
      - 2 cloves garlic, grated
      - ½ tsp white pepper
  - section: Nam tok dressing
    items:
      - 2 tbsp fish sauce
      - 2 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 tsp brown sugar (or palm sugar)
      - 2 tsp toasted rice powder
      - 1 tsp red pepper flakes (or 1–2 Thai chiles, minced)
      - 1 shallot, thinly sliced
      - 2 scallions, thinly sliced
      - ¼ cup mint leaves
      - ¼ cup cilantro leaves
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 4 cups butter lettuce or mixed greens
      - 1 cup cucumber, sliced
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Whisk the fish sauce, soy, oil, brown sugar, garlic, and white pepper. Coat the steak and marinate 30 minutes at room temperature (or up to 4 hours, chilled).
      - "If you don't have toasted rice powder: toast 2 tbsp raw rice in a dry pan over medium until deep golden and fragrant, 5–8 minutes, then grind to a coarse powder."
  - section: Grill
    items:
      - Heat the grill to high and oil the grates. Grill the steak 3–4 minutes per side, until charred outside and 130°F for medium-rare.
      - Rest the steak 5 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
  - section: Toss & plate
    items:
      - Stir together the fish sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, toasted rice powder, and chili until the sugar dissolves.
      - Add the warm sliced beef, shallot, scallions, mint, and cilantro and toss to coat.
      - Bed the lettuce with cucumber and tomatoes, pile the dressed beef and herbs on top, and spoon over any dressing left in the bowl.
tips:
  - Toast raw rice until it's the color of dark caramel before grinding — that nutty khao khua is the soul of the dressing, not an optional garnish.
  - Grill to a true medium-rare (130°F) and slice thin against the grain so the beef stays tender against the crunchy herbs.
  - Dress the beef while it's still warm; it drinks up the lime and fish sauce far better than cold.
  - "Heat dial: 1 tsp flakes is a gentle hum — go to 2–3 fresh Thai chiles for a real crying-tiger burn."
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Garden mint and cilantro are the entire herb bill; thinly sliced serranos stand in nicely for the Thai chiles.
  - label: Serve with
    note: Warm sticky rice or jasmine rice to temper the heat and soak up the dressing.
  - label: Make it a spread
    note: Set out extra dressing as a "jaew" dipping sauce alongside the sliced beef.

Nam tok means "waterfall" — for the juices that drip off the grilling beef. It's the
livelier, herb-packed cousin of a steak salad, and the toasted rice is what makes people
ask what's in it.
