  This is weeknight Mediterranean cooking at its most honest: golden garlic bloomed
  slowly in good olive oil, then cherry tomatoes piled in until their skins split and
  go jammy and saucy. Cubes of fresh tuna slip in at the end with oregano, Kalamata

  sauce turns sweet instead of bitter.*

ingredients:
  - 2 fresh tuna steaks (about 5 oz each)
  - 1⅔ cups cherry tomatoes, quartered
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  - 1 tsp dried oregano
  - 6 Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  - 4 fresh basil leaves, plus more torn for serving
  - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
steps:
  - Cut the tuna into 1-inch cubes. Quarter the tomatoes and mince the garlic.
  - Warm the olive oil with the garlic in a skillet over low heat, stirring, until the garlic turns pale gold and fragrant — don't let it brown.
  - Add the tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, raise to medium-high, and cook about 5 minutes, until they collapse into a loose sauce.
  - Stir in the tuna, oregano, olives, and a few basil leaves. Cover and cook 10–15 minutes, until the tuna is cooked through and has soaked up the sauce.
  - Taste and adjust seasoning, finish with freshly torn basil and a grind of black pepper, and serve.
tips:
  - For rosy, sushi-grade tuna, skip the covered simmer — sear the cubes 30–45 seconds per side at the end and just spoon the warm tomato sauce over them.
  - Spoon any leftover sauce over couscous, orzo, rice, or grilled bread; it's too good to leave in the pan.
  - A splash of dry white wine or a squeeze of lemon at the tomato stage brightens the whole dish.

A fast, sunny one-pan supper: fresh tuna gently cooked in a garlicky tomato sauce
with Kalamata olives and basil. Take the garlic only to gold and the sauce stays
sweet — and if you like your tuna pink, just sear it and spoon the sauce over.
