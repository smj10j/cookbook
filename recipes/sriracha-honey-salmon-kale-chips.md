  This is the sweet-heat lacquer move: salmon goes into the bath at a precise
  125°F until it's custard-tender all the way through, then hits a screaming
  grill for ninety seconds a side — just long enough to caramelize a honey-and-

  and it basically is.

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon & glaze
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets, 5–7 oz each, skin removed
      - 2 tbsp honey
      - 1½ tbsp Sriracha
      - 1 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
      - 1 clove garlic, grated
      - 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
      - ½ tsp rice vinegar (optional, brightens the glaze)
      - Kosher salt (a light season before bagging)
  - section: Spiced kale chips
    items:
      - 1 bunch curly kale, stems removed, torn into 2-inch pieces
      - 1½ tbsp olive oil
      - ½–¾ tsp garlic salt
      - ½ tsp smoked paprika
      - ¼ tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
      - Pinch of brown sugar (optional, for better browning)
      - Lime wedge, for finishing (optional)
steps:
  - section: Glaze & bath
    items:
      - Whisk honey, Sriracha, soy, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and optional rice vinegar. Reserve 1 tbsp to brush on after grilling.
      - Set the sous-vide to 125°F. Lightly salt the fillets, bag each with 1–2 tsp of glaze (just a touch — too much dilutes the sear), and seal.
      - Submerge and cook 45 minutes. You can hold up to 15 minutes longer without overcooking.
  - section: Kale chips
    items:
      - Heat the oven to 300°F. Toss kale with olive oil, garlic salt, paprika, cayenne, and the optional brown sugar.
      - Spread in a single layer with no overlapping leaves — airflow is what makes them crisp.
      - Roast 12–15 minutes, rotate the tray, then 3–5 minutes more until shatter-crisp. Watch the last few minutes closely; they go from crisp to scorched fast. Finish with a squeeze of lime if you like.
  - section: Grill-finish & plate
    items:
      - Heat the grill to high; clean and oil the grates. Remove the salmon from the bag and pat gently dry.
      - Brush a thin layer of glaze over the fillets and grill 1–2 minutes per side, lid open — you want char marks and glassy lacquer, not more interior cooking.
      - Brush with the reserved glaze off-heat. Plate with a pile of kale chips alongside and drizzle any extra glaze over the top.
tips:
  - 125°F gives a buttery, restaurant medium-rare that's fully pasteurized over 45 minutes — firm enough to survive the grill without breaking apart.
  - Keep the bag glaze minimal; surface moisture is the enemy of caramelization. Save the real glaze hit for the grill and the finish.
  - Honey scorches quickly over direct flame, so keep the glaze thin and the grill time short — lacquer, then pull.
  - If you'd rather not fire the grill, broil the patted-dry fillets 2–3 inches from the element for 2–3 minutes, glazing once, until the surface blisters.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: A squeeze of garden lime and a scatter of torn cilantro over the finished plate cut the sweetness and add a fresh, herby lift.
  - label: Make it gluten-free
    note: Swap the soy sauce for tamari or coconut aminos; everything else is already gluten-free.
  - label: Heat dial
    note: Push the cayenne and Sriracha for a serious burn, or pull both back and lean on the smoked paprika for a milder, smokier glaze.

A NASA-flavor-division build that Stephen dialed in across a few rounds: sous-vide
sets the protein at a precise 125°F so the grill can do nothing but char and
lacquer. The kale keeps his garlic-salt, paprika, and cayenne profile but roasts
low to stay crisp and bitter-free — a savory, crackling foil to the sticky
sweet-heat fish.
