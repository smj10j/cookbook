  seize into a dark, sticky lacquer with lightly charred edges. Underneath, a

  finish hot so the glaze caramelizes before the inside ever overcooks.* Sweet,
  savory, bright, and on the table in half an hour.

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon & glaze
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each), skin-on
      - 3 tbsp brown sugar, packed
      - 2 tbsp soy sauce
      - 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
      - 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
      - Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - section: Lemon arugula salad
    items:
      - 4 cups arugula
      - 1 cup cucumber, thinly sliced
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      - ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
  - section: Lemon-Dijon vinaigrette
    items:
      - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
      - 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
      - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tsp honey
      - Pinch of salt and pepper
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Whisk the brown sugar, soy sauce, Dijon, olive oil, garlic, ginger, and lemon juice into a glaze. Season the salmon with salt and pepper.
      - Pour about two-thirds of the glaze over the salmon and let it marinate 15–20 minutes at room temperature. Reserve the rest for finishing.
  - section: Bake & broil
    items:
      - Heat the oven to 400°F and line a small sheet pan with foil. Lay the salmon skin-side down and bake 8–10 minutes, until it flakes at the edges but the center is just shy of done.
      - Switch to high broil. Brush the fillets with the reserved glaze and broil 2–3 minutes, watching closely, until the top is dark, bubbling, and lightly charred at the edges.
  - section: Salad & plate
    items:
      - While the salmon bakes, shake or whisk the vinaigrette until emulsified. Soak the sliced red onion in ice water for 10 minutes, then drain.
      - Toss the arugula, cucumber, tomatoes, and onion with just enough vinaigrette to coat.
      - Bed each plate with the salad and lay a glazed fillet alongside. Spoon any pan glaze over the fish and finish with a squeeze of lemon.
tips:
  - Broilers vary wildly — stay at the oven door and pull the fish the moment the glaze turns glossy-dark, since brown sugar goes from caramelized to burnt in seconds.
  - For clean, tender flakes, cook to 125–130°F at the thickest point; carryover heat finishes it to a silky medium as it rests.
  - Pat the fillets dry before glazing so the marinade clings and the broiler caramelizes instead of steams.
  - Dress the salad at the last second and only lightly — you want crisp, peppery leaves to contrast the sticky fish, not a wilted pile.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Garden arugula, cucumber, and cherry tomatoes are made for this salad; a few torn basil or mint leaves in the greens play nicely off the sweet glaze.
  - label: Make it a sheet-pan dinner
    note: Roast broccoli or green beans on the same pan while the salmon bakes, then broil everything together for the last few minutes.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A crisp, off-dry Riesling or a dry rosé — enough fruit to echo the glaze without fighting the char.

Brown sugar salmon is the archetypal fast win — a four-ingredient glaze that
caramelizes into something that tastes slow-cooked. The peppery lemon salad is
the counterweight: keep it sharp and barely dressed so it resets your palate
between rich, sticky bites of fish.
