pitch: >-
  Banana bread, but it went to finishing school. The butter gets browned until it smells
  like toffee, which threads a deep, nutty caramel through the whole loaf, while a spoonful
  of cream of coconut keeps the crumb impossibly moist and lends a faint tropical sweetness.
  Pools of chopped dark chocolate and toasted walnuts do the rest. It bakes up with a craggy,
  sugar-crisp top and a tender, almost custardy center.
  *Brown the butter past golden to genuinely nutty — those toasty bits are the whole point,
  so don't pull it early.*

ingredients:
  - section: Wet
    items:
      - ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
      - 3 ripe bananas, mashed (about 1¼ cups)
      - 2 large eggs
      - ½ cup brown sugar, packed
      - 3 tbsp cream of coconut (such as Coco López)
      - 3 tbsp granulated sugar
      - 1 tsp vanilla extract
      - ¼ tsp lime zest (optional)
  - section: Dry
    items:
      - 1¾ cups all-purpose flour
      - 1 tsp baking soda
      - ½ tsp salt
      - ½ tsp cinnamon
      - ⅛ tsp ground cardamom (optional)
  - section: Mix-ins
    items:
      - ¾ cup chopped dark chocolate or chunks, a little reserved for the top
      - ½ cup walnuts, toasted and chopped
      - 2 tbsp unsweetened shredded coconut (optional)
steps:
  - Brown the butter — melt it in a small saucepan over medium heat, swirling often, until it foams, smells nutty, and brown flecks form on the bottom, 5–7 minutes. Pour into a large bowl and let cool 5–10 minutes.
  - Toast the walnuts — spread them on a sheet pan and bake at 350°F for 7–9 minutes until fragrant and slightly darkened, then cool and chop.
  - Whisk the brown sugar and granulated sugar into the warm (not hot) browned butter, then whisk in the mashed bananas, eggs, cream of coconut, vanilla, and lime zest until smooth.
  - In a separate bowl, whisk the flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and cardamom. Fold the dry into the wet just until combined — a few flour streaks are fine.
  - Fold in the chocolate, walnuts, and shredded coconut, saving a little chocolate for the top.
  - Scrape into a greased or parchment-lined 9×5-inch loaf pan, scatter the reserved chocolate over, and bake at 350°F for 50–60 minutes, until a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs (not wet batter).
  - Cool in the pan 15 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.
tips:
  - Bananas not ripe enough? Roast them whole in their skins at 300°F for 15 minutes until soft and blackened, then mash.
  - Don't overmix once the flour goes in — overworked batter bakes up tough and domed instead of tender.
  - For a crackly, bakery-style top, sprinkle a tablespoon of turbinado or raw sugar over the batter before it bakes.
  - Chopped bar chocolate melts into luscious pools; chips hold their shape if you'd rather have distinct bites.
extras:

    note: Swap in ¼ cup sour cream or Greek yogurt — less tropical, every bit as moist.
  - label: Make it muffins
    note: Divide into a lined 12-cup tin and bake at 350°F for about 20–24 minutes.
