title: Charred Eggplant Steaks with Romesco

pitch: >-
  A genuine vegetable "steak" with a smoky Spanish sauce — proof a meat-free main can have
  real heft. Thick-cut eggplant planks are scored, oiled, and grilled until the edges char
  and the flesh goes custardy, set over a romesco of roasted red pepper, toasted almond,
  and smoked paprika. *Roasted well past done, the eggplant turns silky and rich and eats
  like a steak — and the romesco does the rest.* The sauce alone is worth it; you'll put it
  on everything.

ingredients:
  - section: Eggplant steaks
    items:
      - 2 large eggplants
      - 3 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tsp smoked paprika
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
      - ½ tsp black pepper
  - section: Romesco
    items:
      - 1 cup roasted red peppers
      - ½ cup almonds, toasted
      - 2 cloves garlic
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
      - 1 tsp smoked paprika
      - Pinch of cayenne
      - ½ tsp salt
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - 2 tbsp parsley, chopped
      - 2 tbsp almonds, chopped
      - Lemon wedges
steps:
  - section: Char the eggplant
    items:
      - Slice each eggplant lengthwise into ¾-inch planks. Score the cut sides in a crosshatch, brush both sides with the oil, and season with the smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
      - Grill over medium-high (or roast at 425°F) 8–10 minutes per side, until deeply charred and fully collapsed and creamy inside.
  - section: Romesco
    items:
      - Blend the roasted peppers, toasted almonds, garlic, oil, vinegar, smoked paprika, cayenne, and salt until thick and just a little textured.
  - section: Plate
    items:
      - Spread the romesco on plates, lay the eggplant steaks on top, and finish with parsley, chopped almonds, and a squeeze of lemon.
tips:
  - Cook the eggplant well past "done" — it should be fully collapsed and custardy, never squeaky or spongy. Char is good.
  - Score the cut sides in a crosshatch so the oil and smoke get in and the flesh cooks through faster.
  - Romesco keeps a week and is a swap-anywhere sauce — it's just as good on the chicken, fish, or scallops elsewhere in this book.
  - No fresh roasted peppers? Jarred piquillo or red peppers make a great shortcut romesco.

Catalan romesco — roasted peppers, almonds, smoked paprika — was made for charred vegetables.
Eggplant cooked until silky is the perfect meaty-but-meatless canvas for it.
