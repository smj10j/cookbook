ingredients:
  - section: Marinade
    items:
      - ¼ cup soy sauce
      - ¼ cup hoisin sauce
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tbsp honey
      - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
      - 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
      - ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
      - "¼ tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)"
  - section: Skewers
    items:
      - 1 lb sirloin steak, cut into 1.5-inch cubes
      - 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1.5-inch squares
      - 1 green bell pepper, cut into 1.5-inch squares
      - 1 red onion, cut into 1.5-inch chunks
      - 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, for garnish
      - 2 scallions, thinly sliced, for garnish
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Whisk all the marinade ingredients in a bowl until smooth. Reserve ¼ cup for glazing later.
      - Add the steak cubes to the remaining marinade, tossing to coat. Cover and refrigerate at least 2 hours, ideally overnight.
      - If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes so they don't scorch.
  - section: Build & grill
    items:
      - Thread the steak onto skewers, alternating with bell pepper and red onion. Leave a little space between pieces so they char rather than steam.
      - Preheat the grill to medium-high and oil the grates. Grill the kebabs 8–10 minutes, turning every 2–3 minutes, until the steak is medium-rare (130–135°F) and the vegetables are charred and tender.
      - In the last minute, brush the skewers with the reserved marinade for a sticky, glossy glaze.
  - section: Finish
    items:
      - Rest the kebabs 5 minutes. Scatter with toasted sesame seeds and sliced scallions and serve over rice.
tips:
  - Always reserve a portion of the marinade before the raw steak goes in — that clean portion is what you brush on at the end and use as a finishing glaze.
  - Cut the steak, peppers, and onion to the same size so everything cooks at the same rate on the skewer.
  - Hoisin and honey both have sugar, so they burn fast; glaze only in the final minute and keep the kebabs moving over the heat.
  - Leave a small gap between pieces on the skewer — crowding traps steam and prevents a good char.
