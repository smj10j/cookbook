  A tagine's magic is spice bloomed in oil and vegetables left alone to go
  silky, and the Instant Pot gets there in a quarter of the time. Cumin,

  slivered apricots melt into the sauce and season it the way salt can't —
  from the sweet side.*

ingredients:
  - section: Tagine
    items:
      - 1 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 small yellow onion, sliced
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - 1 tsp ground cumin
      - 1 tsp ground coriander
      - ½ tsp ground cinnamon
      - ½ tsp sweet paprika
      - Pinch of cayenne
      - 2 carrots, cut into thick coins
      - 1 cup cauliflower florets
      - 1 small zucchini, cut into half-moons
      - ¾ cup canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
      - 3 dried apricots, slivered
      - ⅔ cup low-sodium vegetable broth
      - ¼ tsp kosher salt
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - ⅓ cup couscous
      - 2 tbsp cilantro or mint, chopped
      - Lemon wedges
steps:
  - On sauté mode, warm the oil and soften the onion 3–4 minutes; add the garlic and all the spices and stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
  - Add the carrots, cauliflower, zucchini, chickpeas, apricots, broth, and salt; scrape the bottom well.
  - Pressure cook 4 minutes, then quick-release. If the sauce is thin, simmer it a minute on sauté to gloss.
  - Meanwhile hydrate the couscous per the package with boiling water; fluff with a fork.
  - Spoon the tagine over the couscous and finish with herbs and a squeeze of lemon.
tips:
  - Blooming the spices in the oil before the liquid goes in is non-negotiable — raw spice never recovers.
  - Cut the carrots thick; they should arrive tender, not blown out.
  - No Instant Pot mood — a covered Dutch oven at a low simmer for 25 minutes lands in the same place.
  - It reheats beautifully; the spices settle in overnight.
