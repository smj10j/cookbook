title: Summer Caprese Pasta with Roasted Squash Ribbons & Burst Tomatoes

pitch: >-
  This is a garden party in a bowl — ribbons of yellow squash roasted until their
  edges caramelize, cherry tomatoes blistered until they burst and release their
  sweet juices, all tossed with spaghetti and torn fresh basil. The warm pasta
  softens cool pearls of mozzarella just enough to go molten at the edges, and a
  homemade balsamic glaze, reduced down to a syrup, lands the sweet-tart finish.
  It's the warm-and-cool contrast that makes it sing — hot, glossy noodles against
  creamy cheese and bright herbs. *Shave the squash thin with a Y-peeler and roast
  the ribbons just long enough to brown the edges, never long enough to go soggy —
  that caramelized edge is the whole dish.*

ingredients:
  - section: Pasta & vegetables
    items:
      - 8 oz spaghetti (or linguine or bucatini)
      - 1 medium yellow squash (or half yellow squash, half zucchini)
      - 2 cups cherry tomatoes (mixed colors if you have them)
      - 3–4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
      - 6–7 tbsp olive oil, divided
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - ½ cup reserved pasta water
  - section: Balsamic glaze
    items:
      - ½ cup balsamic vinegar
      - 1 tsp honey or sugar (optional, for gloss)
  - section: To finish
    items:
      - ¾–1 cup mozzarella pearls (or 1 ball fresh mozzarella, torn)
      - 1 small bunch fresh basil, torn
      - Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
steps:
  - section: Balsamic glaze
    items:
      - In a small saucepan over medium heat, bring the balsamic vinegar to a gentle simmer and stir in the honey if using.
      - Simmer, stirring occasionally, until reduced by half and thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, 8–10 minutes. Watch it closely near the end so it doesn't burn; lower the heat if it bubbles hard. Set aside to cool and thicken.
  - section: Roast the vegetables
    items:
      - Preheat the oven to 425°F. Using a Y-peeler or mandoline, shave the squash into thin ribbons.
      - On a parchment-lined sheet pan, toss the squash ribbons with olive oil, salt, and pepper and spread them on one half in a single layer. On the other half, toss the tomatoes with olive oil, sliced garlic, salt, and pepper.
      - Roast 10–12 minutes, until the tomatoes blister and burst and the squash edges turn golden.
  - section: Cook the pasta
    items:
      - Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti to al dente. Reserve ½ cup pasta water, then drain.
  - section: Toss & plate
    items:
      - In a large bowl or wide sauté pan, combine the hot pasta, roasted tomatoes with all their juices, and the squash ribbons. Add 2–3 tbsp olive oil and a splash of pasta water and toss until glossy and loose.
      - Stir in the torn basil and red pepper flakes if using; add more pasta water if it looks dry.
      - Twirl into shallow bowls, nestle in the mozzarella so it softens from the heat, drizzle generously with the balsamic glaze, and finish with more basil and cracked black pepper.
tips:
  - Roast the squash just until the edges brown — pull it before it slumps, or the ribbons go limp and watery in the bowl.
  - Add the mozzarella off the heat at the very end so it stays in soft, molten pockets instead of dissolving into the sauce.
  - This is just as good at room temperature — let it cool and it becomes a stellar summer pasta salad for the next day.
  - No balsamic glaze on hand? The reduction here takes ten minutes; or blend basil, olive oil, and a pinch of salt into a quick basil oil instead.
