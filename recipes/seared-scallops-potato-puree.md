title: Seared Scallops over Silky Potato Purée with Brown Butter & Charred Lemon

pitch: >-
  The date-night showstopper that looks like an afternoon of work and takes thirty
  minutes. Big dry-packed scallops, patted bone-dry, hit a screaming pan and build a deep
  caramel crust in two minutes flat over a velvety potato purée whipped silky with butter
  and cream. *The whole dish is a study in contrast — crackling crust, sweet just-warm
  center, cool creamy purée underneath.* Brown butter, capers, and a charred lemon pull it
  together.
serves: 2

  prep: 15

ingredients:
  - section: Scallops
    items:
      - 1 lb large dry sea scallops (about 10–12)
      - Kosher salt and black pepper
      - 1 tbsp neutral oil
      - 1 tbsp butter
  - section: Potato purée
    items:
      - 1 lb Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
      - 4 tbsp butter
      - ½ cup heavy cream, warmed
      - Salt and white pepper
  - section: Brown butter & finish
    items:
      - 3 tbsp butter
      - 1 lemon, halved
      - 1 tbsp capers (optional)
      - 2 tbsp chopped chives
steps:
  - section: Purée
    items:
      - Cover the potatoes with cold salted water, bring to a boil, and cook until very tender, 15–18 minutes. Drain well and return to the pan over low heat for a minute to steam-dry.
      - Pass through a ricer (or mash very smooth). Beat in the 4 tbsp butter, then the warm cream, until silky. Season with salt and white pepper and keep warm.
  - section: Char & sear
    items:
      - Heat a dry skillet and char the lemon halves cut-side down until deeply browned; set aside.
      - Pat the scallops bone-dry and season. Heat the oil and 1 tbsp butter in a cast-iron skillet over high until just smoking. Add the scallops without crowding and sear undisturbed 2 minutes, until a deep golden crust forms. Flip and cook 1 minute more, just to opaque. Remove.
  - section: Brown butter & plate
    items:
      - Wipe the pan, add the 3 tbsp butter over medium, and swirl until it smells nutty and turns golden-brown. Stir in the capers and, off the heat, a squeeze of charred lemon.
      - Spoon the purée onto plates, set the scallops on top, pour the brown butter over, and finish with chives and the charred lemon halves.
tips:
  - Dry, dry, dry — pat the scallops bone-dry and don't move them for two minutes; that's the only path to a caramel crust instead of a gray steam.
  - Buy "dry" (chemical-free) scallops; "wet" ones leak liquid and refuse to brown.
  - A ricer makes the glossiest purée — and warm the cream and butter before they go in so it stays silky, not gluey.
  - Swap for Stephen — the purée and brown butter are just as good under a seared halibut or cod fillet.
