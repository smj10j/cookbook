ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & marinade
    items:
      - 7 oz boneless skinless chicken thighs
      - 1 stalk lemongrass, tender core minced
      - 1 clove garlic, grated
      - 1½ tsp fish sauce
      - 1½ tsp brown sugar
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 tsp neutral oil
      - Freshly ground black pepper
  - section: Light nuoc cham
    items:
      - 2 tbsp lime juice
      - 2 tbsp water
      - ½ tsp fish sauce
      - 1 tsp sugar
      - 1 serrano, thinly sliced
  - section: Bowls
    items:
      - 3 oz dry rice vermicelli
      - 1 cup cucumber, cut into matchsticks
      - 1 carrot, shaved into ribbons
      - 1 cup mint, cilantro, and basil leaves, torn
steps:
  - Whisk the marinade, coat the chicken, and refrigerate 30–60 minutes.
  - Stir the nuoc cham ingredients until the sugar dissolves; set aside so the serrano steeps.
  - Cook the vermicelli per the package, rinse cold, and drain well.
  - Grill the chicken over high heat 3–4 minutes a side until charred at the edges and cooked through; rest, then slice.
  - Build the bowls — noodles, cucumber, carrot, herbs, chicken — and splash the nuoc cham over at the table.
tips:
  - Only the pale, tender core of the lemongrass goes in the marinade; the woody tops can flavor the grill as smoke.
  - Rinse the noodles until fully cold and shake them truly dry — watery noodles dilute the sauce.
  - Thighs shrug off the high heat that would dry out breasts; keep the portion modest and let the bowl carry the volume.
  - A grill pan indoors works fine — get it nearly smoking first.

bright over salty on purpose, which is what keeps these bowls in the heart and
kidney columns.
