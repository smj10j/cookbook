ingredients:
  - section: Salmon
    items:
      - 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each), skin-on
      - 2 tbsp olive oil (or melted butter)
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - Juice and zest of 1 lemon
      - 1 tbsp fresh dill or parsley, chopped
      - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
      - Lemon slices, for garnish
  - section: Asparagus
    items:
      - 1 bunch asparagus, woody ends trimmed
      - 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
      - 2 cloves garlic, minced
      - Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
      - Grated Parmesan (optional)
      - Lemon wedges, for serving
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Pat the salmon completely dry. In a small bowl, whisk the olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, lemon zest, and chopped herbs with a pinch of salt and pepper.
      - Brush the citrus-garlic mixture over the fillets and let them sit at room temperature 10–15 minutes while you trim the asparagus and toss it with oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  - section: Sear (stove)
    items:
      - Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a cast-iron skillet over medium-high. Lay the salmon skin-side down and press gently for 15 seconds so it stays flat. Cook 4–5 minutes until the skin is crisp and the fish is 70% opaque, then flip and finish 1–2 minutes.
      - Move the salmon to a plate. In the same skillet, sauté the asparagus 5–7 minutes until tender-crisp and lightly blistered. Spoon any pan juices and a squeeze of lemon over both.
  - section: Finish & serve
    items:
      - Return the salmon to the pan for a final minute, spooning the citrus pan sauce over the top. The fish is done when it flakes easily and reads 125–130°F at the thickest point.
      - Plate the salmon and asparagus, shower with Parmesan if using, and finish with lemon slices, fresh herbs, and a wedge on the side.
tips:
  - Dry skin is crisp skin — blot the fillets twice and don't crowd the pan, or they steam instead of sear.
  - Pull the salmon at 125°F; carryover heat finishes it to a silky 130°F while it rests, so it never dries out.
  - Start the salmon first and add the asparagus once you flip the fish, so both land hot at the same moment.
  - A tight-lidded jar mellows raw garlic in the marinade — give it a 10-minute head start before brushing it on.
extras:
  - label: Lemon-garlic, pan or bake
    note: To bake instead, set the oven to 400°F, brush the fillets with the citrus-garlic mix, roast salmon and asparagus on one sheet 12–15 minutes, then broil 2 minutes for a golden top.
  - label: Orange-glazed on the grill
    note: Swap lemon for an orange marinade and fire up the grill to medium-high (375–400°F). Grill skin-on fillets skin-side down 4–5 minutes, flip 3–4 minutes to 145°F; add oiled asparagus after 2–3 minutes so both finish together. Brush with reserved (uncontaminated) marinade for shine.
  - label: From the garden
    note: Stir chopped garden dill or basil into the marinade, and finish with a little fresh thyme. Garden lemon or a Meyer lemon makes the brightest glaze.
  - label: Pairs with
    note: A crisp Sauvignon Blanc or unoaked Chardonnay. Round it out with buttery mashed potatoes, wild rice, or a simple garden salad.

The salmon that earns a permanent spot in the weeknight rotation: a fast
citrus-garlic marinade, a crisp skin from the cast iron, and asparagus blistered
in the very same pan. Lemon keeps it bright on the stove or in the oven; swap in
orange and the grill for a sweeter, smokier summer version.
