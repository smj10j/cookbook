pitch: >-
  Summer dinner, dialed in: build a two-zone fire and let the grill do two jobs at
  once. The corn steams in foil over the cooler side while the strips sear over the
  coals, picking up a charred, smoky crust and clean grill marks. When the steaks
  hit medium-rare you slide them to the cool zone to coast, rest under foil, then
  finish the corn with a fresh pat of butter that melts into the kernels. *The
  two-zone setup is the move — one side to sear hard, one side to coast, so nothing
  flares or overcooks while you tend the other.*

ingredients:
  - section: Steak
    items:
      - 2 New York strip steaks (1–1.5 inches thick)
      - 1 tbsp olive oil (or avocado oil)
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - "Optional: garlic powder, smoked paprika, or fresh rosemary/thyme sprigs"
      - "Optional finish: 1 tbsp garlic-herb butter"
  - section: Corn on the cob
    items:
      - 2 ears corn, husked
      - 2 tbsp butter, plus more for finishing
      - Kosher salt and black pepper
      - "Optional: chopped cilantro or grated Parmesan, for serving"
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Pat the steaks dry and let them sit at room temperature 20–30 minutes. Coat lightly with oil and season generously with salt and pepper (and any optional spices).
      - Rub each ear of corn with butter, season with salt and pepper, and wrap tightly in foil, sealing the ends to trap steam.
  - section: Build the fire
    items:
      - Preheat the grill to medium-high, around 400–450°F. Bank the coals (or set burners) for a two-zone fire — one hot side for searing, one cooler side for indirect cooking.
      - Set the foil-wrapped corn on the cooler side first. Grill 15–20 minutes, turning every 5 minutes, until the kernels are tender and lightly caramelized.
  - section: Grill the steaks
    items:
      - Lay the steaks over the hottest part of the grill and sear with the lid closed, 3–4 minutes per side. For crosshatch marks, rotate each steak 45 degrees halfway through each side.
      - If they need more time to reach doneness, slide them to the cooler side. Pull at 130–135°F for medium-rare (rare 120–125°F, medium 140–145°F).
      - Rest the steaks on a plate, tented loosely with foil, for 5–10 minutes. Add a pat of garlic-herb butter while they rest if you like.
  - section: Finish & serve
    items:
      - Carefully unwrap the corn and add an extra pat of butter to each ear; sprinkle with cilantro or Parmesan if using.
      - Plate each steak with an ear of corn and finish with cracked pepper and flaky salt.
tips:
  - For clean crosshatch grill marks, rotate the steak 45 degrees halfway through each side rather than flipping repeatedly.
  - Start the corn before the steaks — it needs the full 15–20 minutes, while the strips only want a few minutes a side over high heat.
  - A pat of garlic-herb butter added as the steak rests melts down and doubles as a built-in sauce.
  - Pull the steaks 5°F below target; carryover heat finishes them under the foil tent so they land right at medium-rare.
extras:

The classic backyard pairing, run like a line cook would: corn on the cool zone,
steaks on the fire, and a two-zone setup that keeps everything moving without a
single flare-up. Pull the steaks early and let the foil tent finish them.
