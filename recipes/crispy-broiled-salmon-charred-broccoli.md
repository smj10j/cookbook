pitch: >-
  The whole game here is the skin. Pat the fillets bone-dry, score them, slick
  them with oil, and slide them skin-side-up close under a roaring broiler until
  the skin blisters into something that crackles under a fork while the flesh
  stays just-set and buttery. Broccoli roasts hot in the same oven until the tips
  char and go nutty, and garden cherry tomatoes burst sweet-tart against it.
  *A lemon-caper brown butter — nutty, briny, sharp with Dijon — ties cold acid
  to hot fat and pulls the whole plate together.* It's a weeknight dinner that
  eats like a bistro special.

ingredients:
  - section: Salmon
    items:
      - 2 skin-on salmon fillets (5–6 oz each)
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
      - Neutral oil (grapeseed or avocado), for the skin
  - section: Vegetables
    items:
      - 1 small head broccoli (~3–4 cups florets), cut into bite-sized pieces
      - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
      - Zest of 1 lemon
      - Juice of ½ lemon
      - Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  - section: Lemon-herb brown butter
    items:
      - 4 tbsp unsalted butter
      - 1 tsp Dijon mustard
      - 1 tbsp capers, drained
      - 2 sprigs fresh thyme (or ¼ tsp dried)
      - Zest of ½ lemon
      - Pinch of chili flakes or Aleppo pepper (optional)
steps:
  - section: Prep
    items:
      - Move an oven rack to about 6 inches below the broiler. You'll roast the vegetables at 450°F first, then switch to broil for the salmon.
      - Pat the salmon very dry, especially the skin. Score the skin 2–3 times with a sharp knife (don't cut into the flesh), season both sides, and rub the skin with a little neutral oil.
  - section: Roast the vegetables
    items:
      - Toss the broccoli and sliced garlic with the olive oil, lemon zest, salt, and pepper on a sheet pan. Roast at 450°F for 10 minutes.
      - Add the halved cherry tomatoes (tossed with a pinch of salt) and roast 6–8 minutes more, until the broccoli is charred at the tips and the tomatoes have softened but still hold. Don't crowd the pan.
  - section: Broil the salmon
    items:
      - Switch the oven to broil on high. Place the salmon skin-side up on an oiled, foil-lined sheet pan.
      - Broil 6–8 minutes, until the skin is deeply golden and shatter-crisp and the flesh just flakes. Rotate the pan halfway if your broiler runs uneven; for thick fillets, finish with a 1–2 minute rest in the turned-off oven.
  - section: Brown butter & plate
    items:
      - Melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat, swirling, until it foams then browns to amber with a nutty aroma, about 3–5 minutes. Pull it off the heat.
      - Stir in the Dijon, capers, thyme, lemon zest, and optional chili. Taste and brighten with a few drops of lemon juice.
      - Toss the roasted vegetables with a squeeze of lemon and divide between plates. Nestle the salmon on top, skin-side up, and spoon the warm brown butter over the fish and the vegetables.
tips:
  - "Dry skin plus high heat plus oil equals maximum crisp; scoring the skin keeps the fillet from curling and lifting off the pan."
  - Broiling skin-side up renders the fat and crackles the skin while shielding the flesh from the direct element, so it stays moist.
  - Pour the brown butter into a warm bowl the moment it's amber — leftover pan heat will tip it from nutty to burnt in seconds.
  - Salmon carries over off the heat, so pull it a touch early; the residual heat finishes it while you make the sauce.
