pitch: >-
  A sugar cookie has no business tasting this complex. The trick is browning the butter
  until it turns the color of maple and smells like toasted hazelnuts, then chilling it back
  to a scoopable solid so it can be creamed like normal. That nutty, caramelized fat gives
  the cookies a chewy center and a sugar-crusted, crackled top that shatters on the first
  bite.
  *Chilling the browned butter is non-negotiable — solid butter creams and traps air; melted
  butter just makes a greasy puddle.*

ingredients:
  - section: Browned butter
    items:
      - 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
  - section: Wet
    items:
      - 1¼ cups granulated sugar, plus more for rolling
      - 1 large egg plus 1 yolk
      - 2 tsp vanilla extract
  - section: Dry
    items:
      - 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
      - ½ tsp baking soda
      - ½ tsp baking powder
      - ¾ tsp salt
steps:
  - Brown the butter — melt it in a saucepan over medium heat, swirling, until it foams and smells nutty with brown flecks on the bottom, 5–7 minutes.
  - Pour the browned butter into a bowl and chill until semi-solid and scoopable, about 30–45 minutes in the fridge.
  - Cream the chilled browned butter with the 1¼ cups sugar until pale and fluffy, then beat in the egg, yolk, and vanilla.
  - In a separate bowl, whisk the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt, then mix into the butter just until combined.
  - Roll the dough into balls, roll each in granulated sugar, and space them on parchment-lined sheets.
  - Bake at 350°F for 10–12 minutes, until set at the edges but still soft in the center.
  - Let the cookies rest on the sheet a few minutes to finish setting before moving them to a rack.
tips:
  - Don't skip chilling the browned butter — it has to be semi-solid to cream properly, or the cookies spread into thin, greasy disks.
  - Roll the dough balls generously in sugar for that signature crackly, sparkling top.
  - Pull them while the centers still look slightly underdone — they firm up on the hot sheet and stay chewy.
  - For evenly sized cookies, scoop the dough with a tablespoon measure or small disher before rolling.
