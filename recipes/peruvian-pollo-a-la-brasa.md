pitch: >-
  The roast chicken people drive across town for — and the green sauce that's basically
  your flavor profile in a blender. A spatchcocked bird gets a cumin-paprika-soy-lime

  *That sauce is the reason this chicken is famous — you'll want to put it on everything.*
  It's a Sunday-dinner centerpiece that makes its own leftovers.

ingredients:
  - section: Chicken & marinade
    items:
      - 3.5 lb whole chicken, spatchcocked
      - 3 tbsp soy sauce
      - 2 tbsp lime juice
      - 4 cloves garlic, grated
      - 1 tbsp ground cumin
      - 1 tbsp paprika
      - 1 tsp smoked paprika
      - 1 tbsp dried oregano
      - 1 tsp black pepper
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - 1 tsp kosher salt
  - section: Green ají sauce
    items:
      - 2 jalapeños, seeded
      - 1 cup cilantro
      - ½ cup mayonnaise
      - 2 tbsp cotija (or parmesan)
      - 1 tbsp lime juice
      - 1 clove garlic
      - 2 tbsp olive oil
      - Pinch of salt
  - section: To serve
    items:
      - Lime wedges
steps:
  - section: Marinate
    items:
      - Pat the chicken dry and spatchcock it — remove the backbone with shears and press it flat. Whisk together all the marinade ingredients.
      - Rub the marinade all over the chicken, including under the skin. Chill at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
  - section: Roast
    items:
      - Heat the oven to 425°F. Roast the chicken skin-side up on a rack 45–55 minutes, until deeply bronzed and 165°F in the thigh. Rest 10 minutes before carving. (Or grill over two-zone medium heat, skin-side up over indirect, about 40 minutes.)
  - section: Sauce & serve
    items:
      - Blend all the ají sauce ingredients until smooth and bright green, loosening with a splash of water if needed.
      - Carve the chicken and serve with the ají verde and lime wedges.
tips:
  - Spatchcocking lets the whole bird cook flat and evenly with maximum crispy skin — use kitchen shears, or have the butcher do it.
  - Work the marinade under the skin, not just over it, and give it at least 4 hours; overnight is better.
  - The ají verde is the whole point — make a double batch, it keeps several days and goes on everything.
  - "Heat dial: leave the jalapeño seeds in (or add a serrano) for a real kick; seed them fully for a milder green sauce."
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: Garden cilantro is the base of the sauce, and a garden jalapeño or serrano dials the heat.
  - label: Serve with
    note: Crispy potatoes or fries and a simple salad — the classic pollería plate.
  - label: Make it a grill night
    note: Two-zone fire, skin-side up over indirect heat with the lid down, for real charcoal char.

Lima's most famous dish: charcoal-blasted marinated chicken with a creamy, herby, gently
fiery green sauce. The oven gets you most of the way there; the ají verde gets you all the
way.
