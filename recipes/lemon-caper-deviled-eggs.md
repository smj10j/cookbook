title: Lemon-Caper Deviled Eggs
slug: lemon-caper-deviled-eggs
tagline: Classic deviled eggs brightened with lemon zest, capers, and grated garlic.
pitch: >-
  Deviled eggs are a party staple for a reason, and this version earns its spot with a
  hit of brightness — lemon zest and rinsed capers cut the richness of the yolk-and-mayo
  filling, while a whisper of grated garlic keeps it savory. Whir the filling smooth in
  a food processor, pipe it back into the whites from a snipped plastic bag, and dust
  with paprika and parsley. Make the parts a couple days ahead and assemble at the last
  minute.
  *Zest and capers are the upgrade — that little jolt of acid and brine is what makes
  people reach for a second.*

ingredients:
  - 12 large eggs
  - 1 cup mayonnaise
  - 1 tbsp yellow mustard
  - 2 tsp capers, rinsed and drained
  - 1 small clove garlic, grated
  - Zest of 1 lemon
  - ¼ tsp kosher salt
  - ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  - Paprika, for garnish
  - Chopped parsley, for garnish
steps:
  - Bring a large saucepan of water to a boil and lower the eggs in one at a time with a ladle. Reduce to a low boil and cook 10 minutes, then drain and cool.
  - Peel the cooled eggs and slice lengthwise. Pop the yolks into a food processor and arrange the whites cut-side up on a serving platter.
  - Pulse the yolks alone for about 10 seconds. Add the mayonnaise, mustard, capers, garlic, lemon zest, salt, and pepper, and process until smooth, scraping down the sides as needed.
  - Scrape the filling into a resealable plastic bag, seal it, and snip off a corner. Pipe the filling into the egg whites.
  - Garnish with a dusting of paprika and a sprinkle of chopped parsley just before serving.
tips:
  - Make ahead: keep the piped filling and empty whites in separate covered containers for up to two days, then pipe and garnish right before the party.
  - Slightly older eggs (a week or two) peel far more cleanly than fresh ones.
  - For an even silkier filling, push the yolks through a fine sieve before adding the mayo.
