tagline: White rum, muddled garden mint, lime, and soda over crushed ice — the great refresher.
pitch: >-
  You grow mint, you love rum, and you somehow don't have a mojito — that's a gap begging to
  be closed. White rum, muddled garden mint, lime, sugar, and soda over crushed ice: the most
  refreshing long drink in the canon. *Gently bruise the mint, don't shred it — you want the
  perfume, not the bitterness.* It uses your garden directly and is the easiest crowd-pleaser
  to batch for a party.
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5

base: rum
family: highball
methods:

glass: Highball
strength: sessionable
heat: none
tags:
  - refreshing
  - herbal
  - citrusy
ingredients:
  - 2 oz white rum
  - 1 oz lime juice
  - ¾ oz simple syrup
  - 10 mint leaves
  - 2 oz club soda
  - Ice
  - Mint sprig, for garnish
  - Lime wheel, for garnish
steps:
  - Gently muddle the mint leaves with the simple syrup in the bottom of a highball glass — press, don't shred.
  - Add the lime juice and rum, then fill the glass with crushed ice.
  - Top with club soda and stir up from the bottom to lift the mint.
  - Garnish with a mint sprig and a lime wheel.
tips:
  - Bruise the mint, don't pulverize it — you want the perfume, not the bitter chlorophyll.
  - Slap the garnish sprig between your palms before adding; it releases the aroma without shredding.
  - Crushed ice keeps it frosty and dilutes just enough as you sip.
  - Stephen's version — muddle a slice of jalapeño with the mint for a spicy mojito.

  - label: Batch for two
    note: Muddle mint and syrup in a pitcher, add the rum and lime, and top with soda over ice.

Havana's gift to hot afternoons: rum, lime, sugar, mint, and soda. The whole trick is a
gentle hand with the mint and plenty of ice.
