title: Spicy Pineapple Margarita with Tajín Rim

pitch: >-
  This is the margarita that tastes like a beach vendor's fruit cup — pineapple juice
  shaken cold with blanco tequila and fresh lime until it turns frothy and gold. The
  Tajín rim does the heavy lifting, dragging a little chili-lime salt into every sip so
  the sweetness never gets cloying. It lands tropical and tart with a slow, friendly burn
  that builds the longer you sit with it.
  *The rim is the whole trick — chili-lime Tajín turns each sip sweet, then bright, then warm.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: tequila
family: margarita
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Rocks
strength: medium
heat: mild
tags:
  - tropical
  - spicy
ingredients:
  - 2 oz blanco tequila
  - 1 oz triple sec
  - 1½ oz pineapple juice
  - 1 oz fresh lime juice
  - ½ oz simple syrup or agave
  - Tajín, for the rim
  - 1 lime wedge, for the rim
  - Ice
  - Pineapple wedge or lime wheel, for garnish
steps:
  - Rim a rocks glass — run the lime wedge around the edge and dip it into a saucer of Tajín, then fill the glass with fresh ice.
  - Add the tequila, triple sec, pineapple juice, lime juice, and syrup to a shaker filled with ice.
  - Shake hard until the shaker frosts over, about 12–15 seconds.
  - Strain into the rimmed glass over the fresh ice.
  - Garnish with a pineapple wedge or lime wheel.
tips:
  - Rim only half the glass so you can choose a chili-lime sip or a clean one as you go.
  - Use ½ oz agave instead of simple syrup for a rounder, more authentic margarita backbone.
  - Shake with a thin slice of jalapeño in the tin (then strain it out) for heat without the burn of biting in.
