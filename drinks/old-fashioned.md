title: Classic Old Fashioned
slug: old-fashioned
kind: drink
tagline: Bourbon stirred down with sugar, bitters, and a flamed orange peel — the original cocktail.
pitch: >-
  Your bar has no whiskey at all — this is the foundation stone to fix that. Good bourbon
  (or rye) stirred down with a little sugar, a few dashes of Angostura, and an expressed
  orange peel until it's cold, silky, and spirit-forward. *It's the original cocktail —
  two ounces of good whiskey and the discipline to add almost nothing else.* It's the
  natural counterweight to all the bright, tropical pours on this list.
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: whiskey
family: old-fashioned
methods:
  - stirred
glass: Rocks
strength: spirit-forward
heat: none
tags:
  - boozy
ingredients:
  - 2 oz bourbon or rye
  - ½ oz simple syrup
  - 3 dashes Angostura bitters
  - Ice
  - Orange peel, for garnish
  - Cocktail cherry, for garnish (optional)
steps:
  - Add the simple syrup and bitters to a rocks glass.
  - Add a large ice cube and the bourbon, then stir 20–30 seconds to chill and dilute.
  - Express the orange peel over the top — squeeze the skin to spritz its oils — then drop it in. Add a cherry if you like.
tips:
  - One large cube melts slower than a handful of small ones — less dilution, and a colder drink for longer.
  - Express the orange peel skin-side down over the glass; that spritz of citrus oil is half the aroma.
  - The traditional move is to muddle a sugar cube with the bitters and a splash of water instead of syrup.
  - Stephen's pour — rye for more spice and a drier edge; bourbon for a rounder, sweeter old fashioned.

  - label: From the garden
    note: A sprig of expressed thyme or rosemary over the top is a nice seasonal touch.
  - label: Batch for two
    note: Double everything, stir with ice, and split over two large cubes.
