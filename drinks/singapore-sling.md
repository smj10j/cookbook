title: Singapore Sling
slug: singapore-sling
kind: drink
tagline: The pink, layered gin sling born at Raffles Hotel — tropical, frothy, and quietly complex.
pitch: >-
  Ngiam Tong Boon dreamed this up behind the Long Bar at Raffles Hotel around 1915, and
  it still drinks like a postcard from the tropics — gin braced with cherry brandy,
  Bénédictine, and a whisper of Cointreau, lengthened with pineapple and lime into a
  blushing, sunset-pink highball. Fresh pineapple juice is the trick — shaken hard, it
  throws up a frothy cap that makes the whole thing look like dessert and drink like a fruit
  punch with a backbone.
  *Under that cheerful pink it hides a dozen moving parts, which is exactly why it never gets old.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: medium
base: gin
family: sling
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Highball
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - fruity
  - tropical
ingredients:
  - 1½ oz gin
  - ½ oz cherry brandy (cherry liqueur, such as Cherry Heering)
  - ¼ oz Cointreau
  - ¼ oz Bénédictine
  - 4 oz pineapple juice
  - ½ oz fresh lime juice
  - ⅓ oz grenadine
  - 1 dash Angostura bitters
  - Ice
  - Pineapple slice and a cherry, for garnish
steps:
  - Add everything to a shaker filled with ice.
  - Shake hard until the shaker frosts and the juice whips up a froth.
  - Strain into an ice-filled highball.
  - Garnish with a pineapple slice and a cherry.
tips:
  - Use fresh pineapple juice if you can — it gives the signature frothy top that the bottled stuff can't.
  - No Bénédictine on hand — a touch more cherry brandy plus a little extra Cointreau approximates its honeyed, herbal note.
  - Build it for two in a small pitcher and split between glasses so the froth stays even across both pours.
