title: White Russian
slug: white-russian
kind: drink
tagline: Vodka and coffee liqueur over ice, crowned with a slow float of cold cream.
pitch: >-
  This is the after-dinner drink that doubles as dessert — vodka and coffee liqueur over
  a glass of ice, with cold cream poured slow over the back of a spoon so it pools and
  clouds down through the dark. Stirred, it turns a uniform mocha-beige; left alone, it
  layers like a tiny cream parfait. Either way it drinks rich, boozy, and just sweet
  enough, all coffee and cream with a spirit kick underneath.
  *That slow cream float is pure theater — pour it over the back of a barspoon and let it billow before you decide to stir or leave it layered.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: vodka
family: dessert
methods:
  - built
glass: Rocks
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - creamy
  - boozy
ingredients:
  - 2 oz vodka
  - 1 oz coffee liqueur (such as Kahlúa)
  - 1 oz heavy cream
  - Ice
steps:
  - Fill a rocks glass with ice.
  - Pour in the vodka and coffee liqueur.
  - Float the cream over the back of a barspoon so it clouds down through the drink.
  - Stir gently to combine into a uniform beige, or leave it layered for the photogenic look.
tips:
  - Use milk or half-and-half instead of heavy cream for a lighter, less rich pour.
  - Leave it layered for the photo, or give it a slow stir for an even, mocha-beige drink.
  - A tiny grate of fresh nutmeg or cinnamon over the top adds a warm aromatic lift.
extras:
  - label: Make it boozier
    note: Swap an ounce of the cream for cold-brew concentrate, or add a barspoon of crème de cacao, to push it darker and more grown-up.
