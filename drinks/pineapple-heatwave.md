pitch: >-
  This is a tiki drink that bites back without ever shouting. Light rum and fresh pineapple
  go in bright and tropical, lime and bitters pull it back from candy, and a single thin
  slice of jalapeño or habanero steeped in the shaker leaves a slow warmth that builds across
  the glass. Shake it hard so it comes out frothy and cold, then double-strain over fresh
  ice so the heat reads clean instead of muddy.
  *The chili creeps — thirty seconds in the shaker is plenty, because it only gets louder as you drink.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: rum
family: tiki
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Rocks
strength: medium
heat: mild
tags:
  - tropical
  - spicy
  - refreshing
ingredients:
  - 2 oz light or gold rum
  - 1½ oz pineapple juice
  - ¾ oz fresh lime juice
  - ½ oz simple syrup
  - 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  - 1 thin slice fresh jalapeño or habanero, seeds removed (optional)
  - Ice
  - Pineapple wedge, for garnish
steps:
  - Optionally steep the chili slice in the empty shaker for 30–60 seconds — it creeps, so don't overdo it.
  - Add the rum, pineapple juice, lime juice, simple syrup, and bitters.
  - Fill with ice and shake hard for 12–15 seconds until frothy and cold.
  - Double-strain over fresh ice in a rocks glass.
  - Garnish with a pineapple wedge.
tips:
  - It's built to pair with a mango-habanero salsa — the pineapple tames the fire while the chili echoes it.
  - Muddle a coin of fresh ginger into the simple syrup before you build for a deeper, rounder heat.
  - Batch it ×4 in a pitcher (hold the chili and ice) for a porch round, then shake and steep to order.
