  The only warm drink on the whole menu, and a perfect after-dinner one: hot coffee, Irish
  whiskey, brown sugar, and a thick float of lightly whipped cream you sip the coffee
  through. *That cool cream against the hot, boozy coffee is one of the great cocktail
  textures.* Cozy, creamy, and spirit-forward — built for a cold night with Pam.

tags:

ingredients:
  - 1½ oz Irish whiskey
  - 4 oz hot brewed coffee
  - 1 tsp brown sugar
  - 1 oz heavy cream, lightly whipped
steps:
  - Warm a glass mug with hot water, then empty it.
  - Add the brown sugar and hot coffee and stir to dissolve.
  - Stir in the Irish whiskey.
  - Float the lightly whipped cream over the back of a spoon so it sits on top — then sip the hot coffee through the cool cream.
tips:
  - Warm the mug first so the drink stays hot to the last sip.
  - Whip the cream only to a loose, just-pourable float — too stiff and it sits like a lid, too loose and it sinks.
  - Don't stir after the cream goes on; the whole point is hot coffee pulled through cool cream.
  - Pam's pour — a second measure of whiskey makes it a proper nightcap.

Invented at Foynes airport in 1940s Ireland to warm cold travelers — and still the
benchmark for a hot boozy coffee. The cool cream float is everything.
