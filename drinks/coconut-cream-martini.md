title: Coconut Cream Martini
slug: coconut-cream-martini

tagline: A grown-up Almond Joy in a coupe — coconut, coffee, and cream shaken silky cold.
pitch: >-
  This is dessert in a glass and it knows it — coconut tequila and vodka for backbone,
  Baileys and Kahlúa for that mocha-and-cream swirl, all whipped over ice until the shaker
  frosts and the drink turns pale and glossy. A swirl of chocolate syrup down the inside of
  a chilled coupe makes it look like something off a pastry cart, and a dust of toasted
  coconut on top brings the candy-bar nostalgia home.
  *Think a boozy Almond Joy, only smoother and colder, sliding down far too easily.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: liqueur
family: dessert
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - creamy
  - dessert
ingredients:
  - 1 oz coconut tequila
  - 1 oz vodka
  - 1 oz Baileys Irish Cream
  - ¾ oz Kahlúa
  - ½ oz simple syrup (optional)
  - 1 dash vanilla extract or a pinch of sea salt (optional)
  - Ice
  - Shaved chocolate or toasted coconut, for garnish
steps:
  - Chill a coupe in the freezer while you build the drink.
  - Add everything to a shaker filled with ice and shake hard for 15–20 seconds, until frosty.
  - Optionally drizzle chocolate syrup around the inside of the chilled glass for a swirl.
  - Strain into the coupe and garnish with shaved chocolate or toasted coconut.
tips:
  - Rim the glass with crushed graham cracker or toasted coconut for extra candy-bar drama.
  - Blend it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a frozen, milkshake-style version.
  - Taste before you strain and dial the sweetness — add the simple syrup for Lauren, leave it out for Stephen.
