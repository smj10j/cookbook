---
title: French Martini
slug: french-martini
kind: drink
tagline: Vodka, Chambord, and pineapple shaken hard into a fruity coupe with a frothy crown.
pitch: >-
  Don't let the name fool you — there's no gin, no vermouth, just vodka, raspberry
  Chambord, and pineapple juice shaken until it whips up a pale, frothy cap. It drinks
  silky and fruit-forward, deep berry underneath with that bright pineapple lift on top,
  sweet without ever going cloying. It's a coupe that looks like a sunset and goes down
  like a treat.
  *Shake it hard and long — the pineapple juice is what foams up that signature frothy crown, so don't shortchange it.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: vodka
family: martini
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - fruity
  - refreshing
ingredients:
  - 2 oz vodka
  - ½ oz Chambord (raspberry liqueur)
  - 1 oz pineapple juice
  - Ice
  - 3 raspberries or a lemon twist, for garnish
steps:
  - Add the vodka, Chambord, and pineapple juice to a shaker filled with ice.
  - Shake HARD until the shaker frosts and the juice whips into foam, about 15 seconds.
  - Strain into a chilled coupe so the frothy cap sits on top.
  - Garnish with raspberries on a pick or a lemon twist.
tips:
  - Shake harder and longer than you think — the foam comes entirely from beating air into the pineapple juice.
  - Use good, fresh pineapple juice; the cheap-from-concentrate stuff foams poorly and tastes tinny.
  - Keep everything cold — chill the coupe and use fresh ice so the crown holds instead of collapsing.

The chicest of the fruity martinis — deep raspberry, bright pineapple, and a foam cap
