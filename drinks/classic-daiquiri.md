tagline: Rum, lime, and sugar shaken bone-cold into the cleanest three-ingredient cocktail there is.
pitch: >-
  The daiquiri gets a bad rap from the blender — the real thing is three ingredients,
  shaken hard, with nowhere to hide. Good white rum, fresh-squeezed lime, and just enough
  simple syrup to round the edges, beaten over ice until the shaker frosts and your hand
  aches. It comes out bracing and bone-dry with a whisper of sweetness, the kind of drink
  that disappears before you mean it to.
  *Everything rides on fresh lime juice — bottled goes flat and bitter, so squeeze it to order.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: rum
family: daiquiri
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: spirit-forward
heat: none
tags:
  - citrusy
  - refreshing
ingredients:
  - 2 oz white rum
  - 1 oz fresh lime juice
  - ¾ oz simple syrup
  - Ice
  - Lime wheel, for garnish
steps:
  - Add the rum, lime juice, and simple syrup to a shaker filled with ice.
  - Shake hard until the shaker frosts over, about 12–15 seconds.
  - Double-strain into a chilled coupe so it lands silky and ice-shard free.
  - Garnish with a lime wheel.
tips:
  - Chill the coupe in the freezer while you shake — a warm glass kills it by the third sip.
  - "¾ oz syrup is the classic balance; drop to ½ oz for a drier, more spirit-forward pour (Stephen) or nudge up for Lauren."
  - A barspoon of maraschino liqueur turns this into a Hemingway-leaning daiquiri if you have a bottle around.

  - label: From the garden
    note: Muddle a few mint leaves in before you shake, or float a basil leaf, for a garden-fresh lift.
