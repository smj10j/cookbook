  The creamy tropical tiki classic that's basically engineered for Lauren — dark rum,
  pineapple, orange, and a generous pour of cream of coconut, shaken and dusted with fresh

  once.

tags:

ingredients:
  - 2 oz dark rum
  - 4 oz pineapple juice
  - 1 oz orange juice
  - 1 oz cream of coconut
  - Ice
  - Grated nutmeg, for garnish
  - Pineapple wedge, for garnish
steps:
  - Shake the rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, and cream of coconut with ice until cold.
  - Pour unstrained into a glass filled with fresh crushed ice.
  - Grate fresh nutmeg generously over the top.
  - Garnish with a pineapple wedge.
tips:
  - Fresh-grated nutmeg over the top is non-negotiable — it's the aroma that defines a Painkiller.
  - Pusser's rum is the traditional pour, but any rich dark or aged rum works.
  - Use real cream of coconut (Coco López), not coconut milk, for the silky body.
  - Lauren's pour — a touch more cream of coconut; Stephen's — float a smoky aged rum on top.

Born at the Soggy Dollar Bar in the British Virgin Islands — a richer, dark-rum cousin of
the piña colada. The nutmeg on top is what makes it unforgettable.
