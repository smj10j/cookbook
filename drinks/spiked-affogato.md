title: Spiked Affogato

pitch: >-
  Dessert and cocktail in one glass: a scoop of vanilla ice cream drowned in hot espresso
  with a shot of bourbon, amaro, or coffee liqueur poured over. *It melts as you eat it,
  turning from dessert into a boozy coffee shake halfway down.* Endlessly customizable — keep
  it gentle for Lauren, pour it heavier for Pam — and genuinely creative.
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: whiskey
family: dessert
methods:
  - built
glass: Coupe
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - creamy
  - dessert
  - boozy
ingredients:
  - ½ cup vanilla ice cream
  - 1 oz espresso
  - 1 oz bourbon
  - Chocolate shavings, for garnish
steps:
  - Add a scoop of vanilla ice cream to a chilled coupe or small glass.
  - Pull a hot shot of espresso.
  - Pour the bourbon over the ice cream, then the hot espresso.
  - Garnish with chocolate shavings and serve immediately with a spoon.
tips:
  - Serve it the second it's built — affogato is a race between hot espresso and melting ice cream, and that's the fun.
  - Use a really good vanilla ice cream; it's most of the flavor.
  - Keep it gentle for Lauren with coffee liqueur, or pour it heavier for Pam with a full shot of bourbon or amaro.
  - A pinch of flaky salt or a dusting of cocoa over the top sharpens the sweetness.
extras:
  - label: Amaro version
    note: Montenegro or Averna over the ice cream makes a more bitter, grown-up affogato.
  - label: Make it a float
    note: An extra splash of cold coffee liqueur turns it into a boozy affogato float.
  - label: Garnish up
    note: A few coffee beans or a mint leaf for a pretty finish.

The Italian "drowned" dessert, given a pour of booze — part nightcap, part dessert, and gone
before it fully melts. Pick your spirit and your sweetness.
