title: Peach Rum Sour
slug: peach-rum-sour
kind: drink
tagline: Rum, lemon, and ripe peach dry-shaken to a thick foam cap, finished with a swirl of bitters.
pitch: >-
  The sour template never misses, and peach was made for it. Rum and lemon do the bones,
  peach nectar brings late-summer sweetness, and an egg white — dry-shaken first, without ice —
  whips the whole thing into a dense, glossy foam that holds a pour of bitters like a canvas.
  Strain it into a chilled coupe, drop a few dashes of bitters on the foam and swirl them into
  a feather, and you've got something that looks like a cocktail bar charged eighteen dollars for.
  *The dry shake is the magic — beat it hard with no ice first, and the foam comes out thick enough to write on.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: rum
family: sour
methods:
  - dry-shake
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - fruity
  - refreshing
ingredients:
  - 2 oz rum
  - 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  - 1 oz peach nectar (or 2 canned peach slices, muddled)
  - ½ oz simple syrup
  - 1 egg white (optional, for froth)
  - Ice
  - Peach slice or lemon twist, for garnish
steps:
  - Dry-shake everything without ice for about 15 seconds to froth the egg white.
  - Add ice and shake again until cold.
  - Fine-strain into a chilled coupe.
  - Garnish with a peach slice or lemon twist and a few drops of bitters swirled across the foam.
tips:
  - Skip the egg and use ¾ oz aquafaba (chickpea liquid) for the same thick foam, fully vegan.
  - Stephen — a dash of Angostura or peach bitters in the shake adds depth and a drier, more grown-up edge.
  - In peak season, muddle a wedge of fresh ripe peach in the shaker in place of (or alongside) the nectar.
extras:
  - label: From the garden
    note: A basil or thyme leaf slapped and dropped in before the dry shake gives the peach a soft herbal back-note.
  - label: Make it sparkle
    note: Build it without the egg white, strain over ice in a Collins glass, and top with soda for a longer, lower peach fizz.
