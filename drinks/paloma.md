  Your only tequila drinks are both margaritas — the Paloma is the other great agave cooler,

  is one of those pairings that just clicks — bitter, bright, and built for the heat.*

tags:

ingredients:
  - 2 oz blanco tequila
  - 2 oz grapefruit juice
  - ½ oz lime juice
  - ½ oz simple syrup
  - 2 oz club soda
  - Pinch of salt
  - Ice
  - Grapefruit wedge, for garnish
steps:
  - Salt the rim of a highball glass if you like, then fill it with ice.
  - Add the tequila, grapefruit juice, lime juice, simple syrup, and a pinch of salt.
  - Top with club soda and stir gently.
  - Garnish with a grapefruit wedge.
tips:
  - The pinch of salt is the secret — it tames the grapefruit's bitterness and makes everything pop.
  - Classic shortcut — skip the juice and syrup and just top tequila and lime with grapefruit soda (Squirt or Jarritos).
  - Fresh-squeezed grapefruit juice is brighter and less sweet than bottled; ruby red is prettiest.
  - Stephen's version — a Tajín or chili-salt rim ties it to your spicy margarita.
