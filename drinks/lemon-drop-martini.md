title: Lemon Drop Martini
slug: lemon-drop-martini
kind: drink
tagline: A sugar-rimmed coupe of cold vodka and fresh lemon, dialed right between tart and sweet.
pitch: >-
  This is dessert and aperitif in one — vodka and triple sec shaken hard with fresh
  lemon until the shaker frosts, then poured into a coupe rimmed with crackling sugar.
  The first sip lands sweet off the rim, then snaps tart in the middle, then finishes
  clean. It drinks like grown-up lemonade with a backbone, bright enough for Lauren and
  spirited enough to mean it.
  *Sugar the rim and taste before you pour — that crystallized edge is the whole trick, and the lemon should sing, not pucker.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: vodka
family: martini
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - citrusy
  - refreshing
ingredients:
  - 2 oz vodka
  - ¾ oz triple sec
  - 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  - ½–¾ oz simple syrup
  - Granulated sugar, for the rim
  - Ice
  - Lemon twist, for garnish
steps:
  - Run a lemon wedge around the rim of a chilled coupe, then dip the rim in granulated sugar.
  - Add the vodka, triple sec, lemon juice, and simple syrup to a shaker filled with ice.
  - Shake hard until the shaker frosts over, about 12–15 seconds.
  - Fine-strain into the sugar-rimmed coupe.
  - Express a lemon twist over the top and drop it in.
tips:
  - Taste the shaker before you pour and adjust the syrup — lemons vary, so start at ½ oz and climb toward ¾ for Lauren.
  - Use fresh-squeezed lemon only; bottled juice goes flat and metallic and there is nowhere for it to hide here.
  - Chill the coupe in the freezer while you shake so the sugar rim stays crisp.
extras:
  - label: Raspberry Lemon Drop
    note: Add ½ oz Chambord, or muddle 4–5 fresh raspberries in the shaker before you add ice, for a blush-pink, fruitier pour.
