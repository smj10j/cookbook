title: Brown Sugar Banana Daiquiri
slug: brown-sugar-banana-daiquiri
kind: drink
tagline: Ripe banana muddled with brown sugar and shaken into a daiquiri with banana-bread depth.
pitch: >-
  Forget the frozen poolside slush — this is a proper shaken daiquiri that happens to taste
  like the corner of a warm banana loaf. Spotty-ripe banana gets muddled into brown sugar
  syrup, then beaten cold with white rum and fresh lime until the shaker frosts and the
  fruit goes silky. It pours out caramel-gold and bracing, with a final grate of nutmeg
  that smells like a bakery and a kitchen at once.
  *It's shaken, not blended — banana-bread depth without a drop of slush.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: rum
family: daiquiri
methods:
  - muddled
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - fruity
  - dessert
ingredients:
  - 2 oz rum
  - 1 oz fresh lime juice
  - ½ oz brown sugar syrup (1:1 brown sugar and water)
  - 3 slices ripe banana
  - Ice
  - Freshly grated nutmeg or a banana chip, for garnish
steps:
  - Muddle the banana slices with the brown sugar syrup in the bottom of a shaker until smooth.
  - Add the lime juice and rum.
  - Fill the shaker with ice and shake hard until it frosts over, about 12–15 seconds.
  - Fine-strain (double-strain) into a chilled coupe so it lands silky and free of banana threads.
  - Grate fresh nutmeg over the top, or perch a banana chip on the rim.
tips:
  - Make the brown sugar syrup 1:1 by weight — equal parts brown sugar and hot water, stirred until clear, then cooled.
  - Use a very ripe, spotty banana; it muddles into the drink and brings the deepest banana-bread flavor.
  - Float a barspoon of dark rum on top for a molasses-rich finish that drinks more spirit-forward (Stephen).
extras:

  - label: From the garden
