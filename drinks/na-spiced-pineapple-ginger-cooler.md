pitch: >-
  You have no zero-proof option, and this one is unmistakably yours — tropical, a little
  spicy, garden-bright. Fresh pineapple and lime shaken with ginger and a touch of honey,
  topped with soda and finished with a Tajín rim, echoing your spicy pineapple margarita
  without the tequila. *It proves a no-proof drink can have real backbone — heat, acid, and
  fruit doing all the work the booze usually does.* Great for a designated driver, a dry
  night, or just an afternoon cooler that still feels like a cocktail.
serves: 1

  prep: 5

family: highball

glass: Highball

tags:
  - tropical
  - refreshing

ingredients:
  - 3 oz pineapple juice
  - ½ oz lime juice
  - ½ oz honey syrup
  - ½ oz ginger syrup
  - 3 oz club soda
  - Ice
  - Tajín, for the rim
  - Pineapple wedge, for garnish
steps:
  - Rim a highball glass with Tajín — run a lime wedge around the rim and dip it in the Tajín — then fill with ice.
  - Shake the pineapple juice, lime juice, honey syrup, and ginger syrup with ice until cold.
  - Strain into the glass and top with club soda.
  - Garnish with a pineapple wedge.
tips:
  - The Tajín rim does the heavy lifting — it brings the chili-salt-lime kick that usually comes from tequila.
  - Fresh ginger syrup (simmer sliced ginger with sugar and water) gives real warmth; bottled works in a pinch.
  - Shake the juices first for a frothy top, then top gently with soda so it stays lively.
  - Make it boozy — 1.5 oz blanco tequila or white rum turns it into a spicy pineapple cocktail.

  - label: Batch for two
    note: Double the juices and syrups, shake, and split over ice, topping each with soda.
