  The modern classic that fits you perfectly — it's honey, ginger, lemon, and smoke, every

tags:

ingredients:
  - 2 oz blended scotch
  - ¾ oz lemon juice
  - ¾ oz honey-ginger syrup
  - ¼ oz Islay scotch
  - Ice
  - Candied ginger, for garnish
steps:
  - Shake the blended scotch, lemon juice, and honey-ginger syrup with ice until cold.
  - Strain over fresh ice into a rocks glass.
  - Float the Islay scotch on top, pouring gently over the back of a barspoon so it sits on the surface.
  - Garnish with a piece of candied ginger.
tips:
  - The Islay float is the whole trick — pour it slowly over the back of a barspoon so it sits on top and hits your nose first.
  - Make honey-ginger syrup by simmering equal parts honey and water with sliced fresh ginger, then strain and cool.
  - Double-strain to keep stray ginger bits out of a clean-looking drink.
  - Use an inexpensive blended scotch for the body and save the peaty Islay for the float.

  - label: Make it milder
    note: Lighten or skip the Islay float if heavy smoke isn't your thing.
  - label: Batch for two
    note: Shake doubles, strain over ice in two glasses, and float the Islay on each.

Sam Ross's 2005 modern classic — and proof that smoke, honey, ginger, and citrus belong
together. The peaty float is what tips it from a good sour into something unforgettable.
