pitch: >-
  Three ingredients, one job: texture. Cold vodka and coffee liqueur meet a shot of
  fresh, hot espresso, then get shaken harder than feels reasonable — the heat and the
  coffee oils whip into a tight tan foam that caps the glass like crema on a proper pull.
  Strain it into a chilled coupe and float three beans on top while the foam is still
  setting. It drinks bittersweet and silky, a little caffeinated mischief in a martini's
  clothing.
  *The foam is the whole flag — if it won't cap, you shook it cold or shook it shy, so use hot espresso and shake like you mean it.*
serves: 1

glass: Coupe

tags:

ingredients:
  - 2 oz vodka
  - 1 oz coffee liqueur (Kahlúa or Mr Black)
  - 1 oz fresh hot espresso, pulled to order
  - ¼ oz simple syrup, optional, to taste
  - Ice
  - 3 coffee beans, for garnish
steps:
  - Pull a fresh shot of espresso and add it to the shaker while it's still hot — the heat is what builds the foam.
  - Add the vodka, coffee liqueur, simple syrup, and a full scoop of ice.
  - Shake hard for 15–20 seconds, well past when the tin frosts — you're whipping air in, not just chilling.
  - Double-strain into a chilled coupe and let the foam settle into a tight, tan cap.
  - Float three coffee beans in a neat triangle on the foam while it's still setting.
tips:
  - Espresso has to be fresh and hot — a shot that's sat even a few minutes goes flat and won't foam. No machine? A moka pot or a strong Nespresso shot works, but use it the second it's pulled.
  - Shake longer than feels right (a slow count to 20). The foam comes from emulsifying the espresso oils with air, so under-shaking is the number-one way to a flat, capless drink.
  - "Dial the sweetness — Mr Black with ¼ oz syrup keeps it drier and more bitter for Stephen; Kahlúa and no extra syrup runs sweeter for Lauren."
  - Chill the coupe in the freezer first; a cold glass holds the foam cap together several sips longer.
extras:
  - label: Nightcap for two
    note: Double everything (4 oz vodka, 2 oz coffee liqueur, 2 oz espresso) and shake in one big tin — it splits cleanly into two coupes with foam to spare.
  - label: Once you've nailed it, riff
    note: Swap ½ oz of the vodka for añejo tequila or aged rum, or add a barspoon of crème de cacao for a mocha lean — the textbook is the launchpad, not the ceiling.
  - label: Decaf trick
    note: Pull the shot decaf so a late-dinner round doesn't keep you up — the foam behaves exactly the same.
