title: French 75

tagline: Gin and fresh lemon shaken cold, topped with Champagne — bright, dry, and celebratory.
pitch: >-
  You have no sparkling cocktail, and this is the most elegant one there is. Gin and fresh
  lemon shaken cold, then topped with Champagne in a flute so it arrives bright, dry, and
  celebratory with a citrus spine. *It drinks like a lemon drop that put on a tuxedo.*
  Perfect for an anniversary or New Year's, and it finally gives gin a real showcase beyond
  the Singapore Sling.
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: gin
family: fizz
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Flute
strength: medium
heat: none
tags:
  - citrusy
  - refreshing
  - boozy
ingredients:
  - 1 oz gin
  - ½ oz lemon juice
  - ½ oz simple syrup
  - 3 oz Champagne
  - Ice
  - Lemon twist, for garnish
steps:
  - Shake the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup with ice until cold.
  - Strain into a chilled Champagne flute.
  - Top slowly with Champagne and garnish with a lemon twist.
tips:
  - Chill the flute first — bubbles and warmth are enemies.
  - Top with the Champagne slowly so it doesn't foam over the rim.
  - Keep it brut; the simple syrup already brings the sweetness.
  - For Stephen's drier take, drop the syrup to ¼ oz.

  - label: Batch for two

Named for a French field gun for the kick it delivers — gin and Champagne, sharpened with
lemon. The most festive thing on the list.
