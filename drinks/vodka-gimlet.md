pitch: >-
  The gimlet is a study in restraint — vodka, fresh lime, a touch of syrup, beaten over
  ice until the shaker frosts and your hand aches. No bitters, no liqueur, nothing to
  blur it; just cold, citrus, and spirit standing exactly where they should. It comes
  out bracing and crystal-clear, the kind of drink that resets your palate and your
  evening at once.
  *Drop the syrup to a half-ounce and it turns dry and spirit-forward — Stephen's lane to a tee.*
serves: 1
times:
  prep: 5
  total: 5
difficulty: easy
base: vodka
family: gimlet
methods:
  - shaken
glass: Coupe
strength: spirit-forward
heat: none
tags:
  - citrusy
  - refreshing
ingredients:
  - 2 oz vodka
  - ¾ oz fresh lime juice
  - ¾ oz simple syrup
  - Ice
  - Lime wheel, for garnish
steps:
  - Add the vodka, lime juice, and simple syrup to a shaker filled with ice.
  - Shake hard until the shaker frosts over, about 12–15 seconds.
  - Fine-strain into a chilled coupe.
  - Garnish with a lime wheel.
tips:
  - Swap the vodka for gin and you have a classic gin gimlet — same ratios, more botanical lift.
  - Drop to ½ oz syrup for a drier, more spirit-forward pour (Stephen) or hold at ¾ for Lauren.
  - Fresh lime is non-negotiable here; bottled juice goes flat and bitter and there is nothing to cover it.
