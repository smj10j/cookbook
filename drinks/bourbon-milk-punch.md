tags:

ingredients:
  - 2 oz bourbon
  - 2 oz milk
  - 1 oz heavy cream
  - ½ oz simple syrup
  - ½ tsp vanilla extract
  - Ice
  - Grated nutmeg, for garnish
steps:
  - Shake the bourbon, milk, cream, simple syrup, and vanilla with ice until cold and frothy.
  - Strain over fresh ice into a rocks glass.
  - Grate fresh nutmeg over the top.
tips:
  - New Orleans serves it brunch-cold and frothy — shake hard with plenty of ice.
  - Whole milk plus a little cream is the right body; all cream is too heavy, all milk too thin.
  - A good vanilla (or a split bean) makes it taste like melted ice cream.
  - Pam's pour — the bourbon stays front and center, so don't be shy with it.

A French-Quarter brunch staple since the 1800s — somewhere between a White Russian and
eggnog, but lighter and built on good bourbon.
